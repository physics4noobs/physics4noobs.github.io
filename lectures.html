Lecture Notes

Structured lecture notes with detailed explanations, diagrams, and step-by-step derivations for every chapter.

🏁 Kinematics

Master the language of motion. From understanding vectors and scalars to analysing projectile trajectories and relative motion between frames, these lectures build the foundational toolkit every physics student needs.

Vectors & Scalars Equations of Motion Projectile Motion Relative Motion

⚖ Newton's Laws

Dive deep into the principles that govern force and acceleration. Learn to draw free body diagrams, tackle friction problems, analyse circular motion dynamics, and handle non-inertial frames with pseudo forces.

Free Body Diagrams Friction Analysis Circular Motion Dynamics Pseudo Forces

⚡ Work Power Energy

Understand energy as the currency of physics. These lectures cover the work-energy theorem, conservation principles for mechanical energy, and the complete analysis of elastic and inelastic collisions.

Work-Energy Theorem Conservation Laws Elastic & Inelastic Collisions

🔄 Rotational Motion

Extend your understanding of mechanics to rotating bodies. From calculating moments of inertia to analysing rolling motion and the fascinating physics of gyroscopes, these lectures connect linear and rotational worlds.

Moment of Inertia Torque & Angular Momentum Rolling Motion Gyroscopes

🌊 Fluid Dynamics

Explore the behaviour of fluids at rest and in motion. From pressure variation in static fluids to Bernoulli’s energy conservation for flowing fluids, and the role of viscosity in determining flow regimes.

Fluid Statics Bernoulli's Principle Viscosity & Reynolds Number

🔥 Heat & Thermodynamics

From the microscopic dance of gas molecules to the macroscopic laws governing heat engines and entropy, these lectures unify the kinetic theory of gases with the four laws of thermodynamics.

Kinetic Theory Laws of Thermodynamics Heat Engines Entropy

〰 SHM & Waves

Understand oscillatory motion and wave propagation from first principles. These lectures cover simple harmonic motion, the wave equation, superposition of waves, standing wave patterns, and the Doppler effect.

Simple Harmonic Motion Wave Equation Superposition Standing Waves Doppler Effect

⚡ Electrostatics & Capacitance

Begin your journey into electromagnetism with Coulomb’s law, Gauss’s law for symmetric charge distributions, electric potential landscapes, and the art of combining capacitors in series and parallel.

Coulomb's Law Gauss's Law Electric Potential Capacitor Combinations

🔌 Current Electricity

Understand steady-state current flow through conductors. From Ohm’s law and Kirchhoff’s circuit rules to precision measurement techniques using the Wheatstone bridge and potentiometer.

Ohm's Law Kirchhoff's Laws Wheatstone Bridge Potentiometer

🧲 Moving Charges & Magnetism

Discover how moving charges create and respond to magnetic fields. These lectures cover the Biot-Savart law, Ampere’s circuital law, the Lorentz force on charged particles, and cyclotron motion.

Biot-Savart Law Ampere's Law Lorentz Force Cyclotron

♻ Electromagnetic Induction

See how changing magnetic flux gives rise to electric fields and currents. From Faraday’s law and Lenz’s law to self and mutual inductance, culminating in the working principle of an AC generator.

Faraday's Law Lenz's Law Inductance AC Generator

~~ Alternating Current

Analyse AC circuits using phasor diagrams and complex impedances. These lectures cover resonance in RLC circuits, the working of transformers, and the concept of power factor in AC systems.

Phasor Diagrams Resonance Transformers Power Factor

📡 Electromagnetic Waves

Unify electricity and magnetism through Maxwell’s equations. Understand how oscillating electric and magnetic fields propagate as waves, explore the EM spectrum, and study wave properties like polarization.

Maxwell's Equations EM Spectrum Wave Properties

🔍 Ray Optics

Trace light rays through mirrors, lenses, and prisms. These lectures derive the mirror and lens formulae, analyse dispersion through prisms, and explore optical instruments from microscopes to telescopes.

Mirror Formula Lens Formula Prism Optical Instruments

🌈 Wave Optics

Go beyond rays to understand light as a wave. From Young’s double slit interference to single-slit diffraction, polarization of light, and the resolving power of optical instruments.

Young's Double Slit Diffraction Polarization Resolving Power

⚛ Modern Physics

Step into the quantum realm with the photoelectric effect and Bohr’s atomic model. These lectures then explore nuclear reactions, binding energy, and the mathematics of radioactive decay.

Photoelectric Effect Bohr Model Nuclear Reactions Radioactive Decay
