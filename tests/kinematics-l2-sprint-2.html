Kinematics Level 2 — Sprint Test 2

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A body is allowed to fall from a height of 100 m. If the time taken for the first 50 m is t1 and for the remaining 50 m is t2. Which of these is correct regarding t1 and t2?
A. t1 = t2
B. t1 > t2
C. t1 < t2
D. Depends upon the mass
Solution: The body accelerates as it falls. It is slower during the first 50 m and faster during the second 50 m. So it takes more time for the first 50 m: t1 > t2.
Question 2
In the previous question, the ratio of t1 and t2 is nearly:
A. 5 : 2
B. 3 : 1
C. 3 : 2
D. 5 : 3
Solution: For first 50 m: 50 = (1/2)(10)t12, so t1 = √10. For total 100 m: 100 = (1/2)(10)t2, so t = √20 = 2√5. Then t2 = 2√5 − √10 = √10(√2 − 1). Therefore t1/t2 = √10 / [√10(√2 − 1)] = 1/(√2 − 1) = (√2 + 1) ≈ 2.414 ≈ 5/2.
Question 3
Acceleration of a particle has a value ‘a’ for a time t. It is followed immediately by a retardation of magnitude ‘a’ for time t/2. Consider this as one cycle. Initial velocity of particle was zero. The displacement of the particle after n such cycles in succession is:
A. n(3n + 4)at2/8
B. n(n + 1)at2/2
C. (n2 + n + 1)at2/4
D. nat2
Solution: In one cycle — Phase 1 (time t, acceleration a): v1 = at, s1 = (1/2)at2. Phase 2 (time t/2, retardation a): v2 = at − a(t/2) = at/2, s2 = at(t/2) − (1/2)a(t/2)2 = at2/2 − at2/8 = 3at2/8. The net displacement per cycle increases as the initial velocity at the start of each cycle grows. After careful summation over n cycles, the total displacement = n(3n + 4)at2/8.
Question 4
A particle starts from rest and traverses a distance ℓ with uniform acceleration, then moves uniformly over a further distance 2ℓ and finally comes to rest after moving a further distance 3ℓ under uniform retardation. Assuming entire motion to be rectilinear motion, the ratio of average speed over the journey to the maximum speed on its way is:
A. 1/5
B. 2/5
C. 3/5
D. 4/5
Solution: Let max speed = v. Phase 1: ℓ = v·t1/2 → t1 = 2ℓ/v. Phase 2: 2ℓ = v·t2 → t2 = 2ℓ/v. Phase 3: 3ℓ = v·t3/2 → t3 = 6ℓ/v. Total time = 10ℓ/v. Total distance = 6ℓ. Average speed = 6ℓ / (10ℓ/v) = 3v/5. Ratio = 3/5.
Question 5
A particle of mass m initially at rest is acted upon by a unidirectional force F = F0cos(πt/2). The distance travelled by the particle when it stops first in the subsequent motion is:
A. 2F0/(mπ)
B. 4F0/(π2m)
C. 8F0/(mπ2)
D. 2F0/(π2m)
Solution: a = (F0/m)cos(πt/2). Integrating: v = (2F0/mπ)sin(πt/2). The particle stops again when sin(πt/2) = 0, i.e., πt/2 = π → t = 2. Distance x = ∫02 (2F0/mπ)sin(πt/2) dt = (2F0/mπ)[−2/π · cos(πt/2)]02 = (4F0/mπ2)[cos(0) − cos(π)] = (4F0/mπ2)(1 + 1) = 8F0/(mπ2).
Question 6
Starting from rest a particle moves in a straight line with acceleration a = {2 + |t − 2|} m/s2. Velocity of particle at the end of 4 s will be:
A. 16 m/s
B. 20 m/s
C. 8 m/s
D. 12 m/s
Solution: For 0 ≤ t ≤ 2: a = 2 + (2 − t) = 4 − t. For 2 ≤ t ≤ 4: a = 2 + (t − 2) = t. Therefore v = ∫02(4 − t) dt + ∫24 t dt = [4t − t2/2]02 + [t2/2]24 = (8 − 2) + (8 − 2) = 6 + 6 = 12 m/s.
Question 7
The displacement x of a particle varies with time t as x = ae−αt + beβt, where a, b, α and β are positive constants. The velocity of the particle will:
A. Go on decreasing with time
B. Be independent of α and β
C. Drop to zero when α = β
D. Go on increasing with time
Solution: v = dx/dt = −aαe−αt + bβeβt. As t → ∞, the first term → 0 and the second term → ∞. The eβt term dominates, so the velocity keeps increasing without bound.
Question 8
A point moves in a straight line so that its displacement x at time t is given by x2 = 1 + t2. Its acceleration at any time t is:
A. t/x3
B. −t/x3
C. 1/x − t2/x3
D. 1/x − 1/x2
Solution: Given x2 = 1 + t2. Differentiating: 2x · v = 2t → v = t/x. Differentiating again using the quotient rule: a = dv/dt = (x − t · dx/dt)/x2 = (x − t · t/x)/x2 = (x2 − t2)/x3. Since x2 − t2 = 1, we get a = 1/x3. Note that 1/x − t2/x3 = (x2 − t2)/x3 = 1/x3, confirming option C.
Question 9
A car starts from rest with an acceleration of 6 m/s2 which decreases to zero linearly in 10 seconds, after which the car continues at a constant speed. Find the time required for the car to travel 400 m from the start.
A. 16.67 s
B. 25.6 s
C. 10.56 s
D. 9.56 s
Solution: a = 6(1 − t/10) for 0 ≤ t ≤ 10. Integrating: v = 6t − 3t2/10. At t = 10: v = 60 − 30 = 30 m/s. Distance in first 10 s: s = [3t2 − t3/10]010 = 300 − 100 = 200 m. Remaining distance = 400 − 200 = 200 m at constant 30 m/s: t = 200/30 ≈ 6.67 s. Total time = 10 + 6.67 = 16.67 s.
Question 10
A ball of weight W is thrown upward with a velocity v. If air exerts an average resisting force F, the velocity with which it returns back to the thrower is:
A. v√(W/(W + F))
B. v√(W/(W − F))
C. v√((W − F)/(W + F))
D. v√((W + F)/W)
Solution: Going up, net retarding force = W + F (weight and air resistance both oppose motion), so (W + F)h = (1/2)mv2. Coming down, net force = W − F (weight drives motion, air resistance opposes), so (W − F)h = (1/2)mv′2. Dividing: v′2/v2 = (W − F)/(W + F). Therefore v′ = v√((W − F)/(W + F)).
