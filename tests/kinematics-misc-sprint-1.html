Miscellaneous • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes

Question 1
Water drops fall at regular intervals from a tap which is 5 m above the ground. The third drop is leaving the tap at the instant the first drop touches the ground. How far above the ground is the second drop at that instant?
A. 2.50 m
B. 3.75 m
C. 4.00 m
D. 1.25 m
Solution: Time for first drop: t = √(2h/g) = 1 s. Drops fall at 0.5 s intervals. Second drop has fallen for 0.5 s: d = (1/2)(10)(0.25) = 1.25 m. Height = 5 − 1.25 = 3.75 m.
Question 2
A particle has a velocity u towards east at t = 0. Its acceleration is towards west and is constant. Let xA and xB be the magnitude of displacements in the first 10 seconds and the next 10 seconds. Then:
A. xA < xB
B. xA = xB
C. xA > xB
D. The information is insufficient to decide the relation of xA with xB
Solution: The displacement depends on whether the particle reverses direction. If u > 10a, xA > xB. If u < 10a, the particle reverses and xB could be larger in magnitude. Without knowing u and a, we cannot determine the relation.
Question 3
A body A starts from rest with an acceleration a1. After 2 seconds, another body B starts from rest with an acceleration a2. If they travel equal distances in the 5th second, after start of A, then the ratio a1 : a2 is equal to:
A. 5 : 9
B. 5 : 7
C. 9 : 5
D. 9 : 7
Solution: 5th second for A is the 5th second of its motion. 5th second for B (which started 2s later) is the 3rd second of B's motion. Distance in nth second: (a/2)(2n − 1). (a1/2)(9) = (a2/2)(5). a1/a2 = 5/9.
Question 4
A target is made of two plates, one of wood and the other of iron. The thickness of the wooden plate is 4 cm and that of iron plate is 2 cm. A bullet fired goes through the wood first and then penetrates 1 cm into iron. A similar bullet fired with the same velocity from opposite direction goes through iron first and then penetrates 2 cm into wood. If a1 and a2 be the retardation offered to the bullet by wood and iron plates respectively, then:
A. a1 = 2a2
B. a2 = 2a1
C. a1 = a2
D. Data insufficient
Solution: Case I: v12 = u2 − 2a1(4), 0 = v12 − 2a2(1). So u2 = 8a1 + 2a2. Case II: v22 = u2 − 2a2(2), 0 = v22 − 2a1(2). So u2 = 4a1 + 4a2. Equating: 8a1 + 2a2 = 4a1 + 4a2 → 4a1 = 2a2 → a2 = 2a1.
Question 5
A body starts from rest and is uniformly accelerated. The distance travelled in the first 5 s is x1, next 10 s is x2 and the next 20 s is x3. Then x1 : x2 : x3 is:
A. 1 : 3 : 9
B. 1 : 9 : 49
C. 1 : 8 : 40
D. 1 : 3 : 12
Solution: x1 = (1/2)a(25) = 25a/2. x2 = (1/2)a(225) − (1/2)a(25) = 200a/2. x3 = (1/2)a(1225) − (1/2)a(225) = 1000a/2. Ratio = 25 : 200 : 1000 = 1 : 8 : 40.
Question 6
A stone is thrown vertically upwards with an initial speed u from the top of a tower. It reaches the ground with speed 3u. The height of the tower is:
A. 3u2/g
B. 4u2/g
C. 6u2/g
D. 9u2/g
Solution: (3u)2 = u2 + 2gH (taking downward positive). 9u2 = u2 + 2gH. H = 4u2/g.
Question 7
Two cars start off to race with velocities 4 m/s and 2 m/s and travel in straight line with uniform accelerations of 1 m/s2 and 2 m/s2 respectively. If they reach the finish point at the same instant, the length of the path is:
A. 30 m
B. 32 m
C. 20 m
D. 24 m
Solution: 4t + (1/2)(1)t2 = 2t + (1/2)(2)t2. 4t + t2/2 = 2t + t2. 2t = t2/2. t = 4 s. s = 4(4) + (1/2)(1)(16) = 16 + 8 = 24 m.
Question 8
A body starts from rest and moves with a constant acceleration. The ratio of distance covered in the nth second to the distance covered in n seconds is:
A. (2/n) − (1/n2)
B. (1/n2) − (1/n)
C. (2/n2) − (1/n)
D. (2/n) + (1/n2)
Solution: Snth = (a/2)(2n − 1). Sn = (1/2)an2. Ratio = (2n − 1)/n2 = 2/n − 1/n2.
Question 9
A particle is moving along x-axis, its position varying with time as x = (t − 2)2, where x is in metres and t is in seconds. The distance covered by the particle in first 5 seconds is:
A. 5 m
B. 9 m
C. 13 m
D. 4 m
Solution: x(0) = 4, x(2) = 0, x(5) = 9. The particle first moves from x = 4 to x = 0 (distance = 4), then from x = 0 to x = 9 (distance = 9). Total distance = 4 + 9 = 13 m.
Question 10
The height of the ball from the ground after time T/3, where T is the total time to fall from height h, is:
A. h/9
B. 7h/9
C. 8h/9
D. 17h/18
Solution: h = (1/2)gT2. Distance fallen at T/3: d = (1/2)g(T/3)2 = h/9. Height from ground = h − h/9 = 8h/9.
