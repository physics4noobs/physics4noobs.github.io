Fluid Mechanics — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
The pressure at the bottom of a lake of depth 10 m is (atmospheric pressure = 1.013 × 105 Pa, density of water = 1000 kg/m³, g = 9.8 m/s²):
A. 1.993 × 105 Pa
B. 0.98 × 105 Pa
C. 1.013 × 105 Pa
D. 2.993 × 105 Pa
Solution: P = Patm + ρgh = 1.013 × 105 + 1000 × 9.8 × 10 = 1.013 × 105 + 0.98 × 105 = 1.993 × 105 Pa.
Question 2
In a hydraulic press, the area of the small piston is 10 cm² and the area of the large piston is 200 cm². A force of 50 N is applied on the small piston. The force on the large piston is:
A. 50 N
B. 500 N
C. 1000 N
D. 2500 N
Solution: By Pascal's law: F1/A1 = F2/A2. F2 = F1 × A2/A1 = 50 × 200/10 = 1000 N.
Question 3
A wooden block of density 600 kg/m³ floats in water. The fraction of the block submerged is:
A. 0.4
B. 0.6
C. 0.8
D. 1.0
Solution: For a floating body, fraction submerged = ρbody/ρliquid = 600/1000 = 0.6 or 60%.
Question 4
Water flows through a pipe of cross-sectional area 4 cm² at 2 m/s. If the pipe narrows to 2 cm², the speed of water is:
A. 1 m/s
B. 2 m/s
C. 3 m/s
D. 4 m/s
Solution: By equation of continuity: A1v1 = A2v2. v2 = A1v1/A2 = 4 × 2/2 = 4 m/s.
Question 5
The speed of efflux of a liquid through an orifice at a depth h below the surface is (Torricelli's theorem):
A. √(2gh)
B. √(gh)
C. 2gh
D. √(gh/2)
Solution: By Torricelli's theorem (applying Bernoulli's equation), the speed of efflux v = √(2gh), analogous to a body falling from height h.
Question 6
A body weighs 100 N in air and 75 N when fully immersed in water. The buoyant force on the body is:
A. 100 N
B. 25 N
C. 75 N
D. 175 N
Solution: Buoyant force = Weight in air − Weight in water = 100 − 75 = 25 N.
Question 7
A sphere of radius r falls through a viscous fluid. The terminal velocity is proportional to:
A. r
B. r³
C. r²
D. 1/r
Solution: Terminal velocity vt = 2r²(ρs − ρl)g/(9η). So vt ∝ r².
Question 8
Water is flowing through a horizontal pipe. At point A the velocity is 2 m/s and pressure is 2 × 105 Pa. At point B the velocity is 4 m/s. The pressure at B is (ρ = 1000 kg/m³):
A. 1.94 × 105 Pa
B. 2.06 × 105 Pa
C. 1.5 × 105 Pa
D. 2.5 × 105 Pa
Solution: By Bernoulli's theorem (same height): PA + ½ρvA² = PB + ½ρvB². PB = PA + ½ρ(vA² − vB²) = 2 × 105 + ½(1000)(4 − 16) = 2 × 105 − 6000 = 1.94 × 105 Pa.
Question 9
A U-tube manometer contains mercury. One arm is open and the other is connected to a gas supply. If the mercury level in the open arm is 10 cm higher, the gauge pressure of the gas is (ρHg = 13600 kg/m³, g = 9.8 m/s²):
A. 1360 Pa
B. 9800 Pa
C. 1.013 × 105 Pa
D. 13328 Pa
Solution: Gauge pressure = ρgh = 13600 × 9.8 × 0.10 = 13328 Pa.
Question 10
An ice cube floats in a glass of water filled to the brim. When the ice melts completely, the water level:
A. Rises
B. Remains the same
C. Falls
D. First rises then falls
Solution: When floating, the ice displaces a volume of water equal to its own weight. When it melts, the resulting water occupies exactly the volume that was displaced. So the water level remains the same.
Question 11
The Reynolds number for a flow to be laminar in a pipe should be less than:
A. 500
B. 1000
C. 2000
D. 4000
Solution: For flow in a pipe, the critical Reynolds number is approximately 2000. Below this value, the flow is generally laminar.
Question 12
A tank is filled with water to a height of 5 m. The velocity of efflux from a small hole at the bottom is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 10 m/s
B. 5 m/s
C. √50 m/s
D. 20 m/s
Solution: v = √(2gh) = √(2 × 10 × 5) = √100 = 10 m/s.
Question 13
An object of density 800 kg/m³ and volume 0.01 m³ is placed in a liquid of density 1200 kg/m³. The buoyant force on the object is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 80 N
B. 120 N
C. 96 N
D. 40 N
Solution: Since ρobject < ρliquid, the object floats. Weight of object = ρobjVg = 800 × 0.01 × 10 = 80 N. For floating, buoyant force = weight = 80 N. But if fully submerged (as stated), buoyant force = ρliquidVg = 1200 × 0.01 × 10 = 120 N. Since the object is placed in the liquid, the maximum buoyant force when fully submerged is 120 N.
Question 14
The surface tension of a liquid is 0.07 N/m. The excess pressure inside a soap bubble of radius 0.01 m is (soap bubble has two surfaces):
A. 7 Pa
B. 14 Pa
C. 21 Pa
D. 28 Pa
Solution: For a soap bubble (two surfaces): ΔP = 4T/r = 4 × 0.07/0.01 = 28 Pa.
Question 15
Two drops of mercury of equal radii r merge to form a single drop. The radius of the new drop is:
A. r
B. 2r
C. 21/3r
D. √2 r
Solution: Volume is conserved: 2 × (4/3)πr³ = (4/3)πR³. So R³ = 2r³, giving R = 21/3r.
