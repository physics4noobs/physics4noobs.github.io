Magnetism — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
A proton moving with velocity 106 m/s enters a uniform magnetic field of 0.5 T perpendicular to its velocity. The radius of the circular path described by the proton is (mp = 1.67 × 10−27 kg):
A. 0.0104 m
B. 0.104 m
C. 0.0209 m
D. 0.209 m
Solution: r = mv/(qB) = (1.67 × 10−27 × 106) / (1.6 × 10−19 × 0.5) = 1.67 × 10−21 / (8 × 10−20) = 0.0209 m.
Question 2
The magnetic field at the centre of a circular loop of radius 10 cm carrying a current of 5 A is (μ0 = 4π × 10−7 T·m/A):
A. π × 10−5 T
B. 2π × 10−5 T
C. π × 10−6 T
D. 2π × 10−6 T
Solution: B = μ0I / (2R) = (4π × 10−7 × 5) / (2 × 0.1) = 20π × 10−7 / 0.2 = π × 10−5 T.
Question 3
Two long parallel wires carrying currents of 10 A each in opposite directions are separated by 20 cm. The force per unit length between them is:
A. 10−4 N/m, attractive
B. 10−4 N/m, repulsive
C. 10−5 N/m, attractive
D. 10−5 N/m, repulsive
Solution: F/L = μ0I1I2 / (2πd) = (4π × 10−7 × 10 × 10) / (2π × 0.2) = 4 × 10−5 / (0.4) = 10−4 N/m. Since currents are in opposite directions, the force is repulsive.
Question 4
A charged particle enters a uniform magnetic field parallel to the field direction. The path of the particle will be:
A. A circle
B. A helix
C. A parabola
D. A straight line
Solution: When a charged particle moves parallel to B, the angle θ = 0°, so F = qvB sin0° = 0. With no force acting, the particle continues in a straight line.
Question 5
A rectangular coil of 100 turns, each of area 0.04 m², carrying a current of 0.5 A is placed in a uniform magnetic field of 0.2 T. The maximum torque acting on the coil is:
A. 0.4 N·m
B. 0.04 N·m
C. 4 N·m
D. 40 N·m
Solution: Maximum torque τmax = NIAB = 100 × 0.5 × 0.04 × 0.2 = 0.4 N·m (when the plane of the coil is parallel to B).
Question 6
The magnetic field inside a long solenoid having n turns per unit length and carrying current I is:
A. μ0I / n
B. μ0I / (2n)
C. μ0nI
D. μ0n²I
Solution: By Ampere's circuital law, the magnetic field inside an ideal solenoid is B = μ0nI, where n is the number of turns per unit length.
Question 7
An electron moves in a circular orbit of radius r with speed v in a magnetic field B perpendicular to its velocity. The time period of revolution is:
A. 2πr/v
B. 2πm/(eB)
C. 2πeB/m
D. 2πv/(eB)
Solution: T = 2πr/v. Since r = mv/(eB), T = 2π × mv/(eB) × 1/v = 2πm/(eB). The time period is independent of the speed and radius.
Question 8
A current-carrying conductor of length L and current I is placed perpendicular to a magnetic field B. The force on the conductor is:
A. BIL cosθ
B. Zero
C. B²IL
D. BIL
Solution: F = BIL sinθ. When the conductor is perpendicular to B, θ = 90°, so sin90° = 1, and F = BIL.
Question 9
The magnetic field at a distance d from a long straight wire carrying current I is:
A. μ0I / (2πd)
B. μ0I / (4πd)
C. μ0I² / (2πd)
D. μ0Id / (2π)
Solution: By Ampere's law or Biot-Savart law, the magnetic field at distance d from a long straight conductor is B = μ0I / (2πd).
Question 10
A charged particle moves with velocity v at angle 30° to a uniform magnetic field B. The path of the particle is:
A. A circle
B. A straight line
C. A helix
D. A parabola
Solution: When a charged particle enters B at an angle other than 0° or 90°, the perpendicular component (v sinθ) causes circular motion while the parallel component (v cosθ) moves the particle along B. The combination produces a helical path.
Question 11
In a moving coil galvanometer, the deflection is proportional to the current because of:
A. The presence of soft iron core
B. The radial magnetic field providing constant torque per unit current
C. The uniform magnetic field
D. Absence of magnetic field
Solution: The radial magnetic field ensures that the plane of the coil is always parallel to B (sinθ = 1), so τ = NIAB. At equilibrium, NIAB = kφ, giving φ = (NAB/k)I. Since NAB/k is constant, deflection φ is directly proportional to I.
Question 12
The magnetic moment of a circular coil of N turns, radius r, and carrying current I is:
A. NIπr²
B. NIπr
C. NI/(2πr)
D. NIπr²/2
Solution: Magnetic moment M = NIA = NI × πr² = NIπr².
Question 13
A toroid with 1000 turns, mean radius 10 cm and carrying current 2 A has a magnetic field inside it of:
A. 4 × 10−4 T
B. 2 × 10−3 T
C. 2 × 10−4 T
D. 4 × 10−3 T
Solution: B = μ0NI / (2πr) = (4π × 10−7 × 1000 × 2) / (2π × 0.1) = (8π × 10−4) / (0.2π) = 4 × 10−3 T.
Question 14
A galvanometer of resistance 100 Ω gives full-scale deflection for 10 mA. To convert it into an ammeter of range 0–10 A, the shunt resistance required is:
A. 1 Ω
B. 0.01 Ω
C. 0.1 Ω (approx.)
D. 10 Ω
Solution: S = IgG / (I − Ig) = (0.01 × 100) / (10 − 0.01) = 1 / 9.99 ≈ 0.1 Ω.
Question 15
The work done by a magnetic force on a charged particle moving in a magnetic field is:
A. Positive
B. Always zero
C. Negative
D. Depends on the direction of the field
Solution: The magnetic force (F = qv × B) is always perpendicular to the velocity of the charged particle. Since the force is perpendicular to displacement, the work done W = F · ds = 0 always. The magnetic force can change direction but not speed or kinetic energy.
