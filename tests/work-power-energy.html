Work, Power & Energy — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
A force of 10 N acts on a body and displaces it by 5 m at an angle of 60° with the force. The work done is:
A. 50 J
B. 25 J
C. 25√3 J
D. 10 J
Solution: W = Fd cosθ = 10 × 5 × cos60° = 50 × 0.5 = 25 J.
Question 2
A spring of spring constant 200 N/m is compressed by 0.1 m. The potential energy stored in the spring is:
A. 2 J
B. 20 J
C. 1 J
D. 0.1 J
Solution: PE = ½kx² = ½ × 200 × (0.1)² = ½ × 200 × 0.01 = 1 J.
Question 3
A body of mass 2 kg moving with velocity 10 m/s is brought to rest by a constant force. The work done by the force is:
A. −100 J
B. 100 J
C. −200 J
D. 200 J
Solution: By work-energy theorem, W = ΔKE = ½mv2f − ½mv2i = 0 − ½ × 2 × 100 = −100 J.
Question 4
A pump lifts 200 kg of water per minute to a height of 10 m. The power of the pump is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 200 W
B. 2000 W
C. 20000 W
D. 333.3 W
Solution: P = mgh/t = 200 × 10 × 10/60 = 20000/60 = 333.3 W.
Question 5
A ball of mass 1 kg is dropped from a height of 20 m. Its kinetic energy just before hitting the ground is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 100 J
B. 200 J
C. 20 J
D. 400 J
Solution: By conservation of energy, all PE converts to KE: KE = mgh = 1 × 10 × 20 = 200 J.
Question 6
Two bodies of masses 1 kg and 4 kg have equal kinetic energies. The ratio of their momenta is:
A. 1 : 4
B. 4 : 1
C. 1 : 2
D. 2 : 1
Solution: KE = p²/(2m), so p = √(2mKE). For equal KE: p1/p2 = √(m1/m2) = √(1/4) = 1/2. Ratio is 1 : 2.
Question 7
A ball of mass 0.5 kg moving at 6 m/s collides head-on with a stationary ball of mass 0.5 kg. If the collision is perfectly elastic, the velocity of the first ball after collision is:
A. 0 m/s
B. 6 m/s
C. 3 m/s
D. −6 m/s
Solution: In a perfectly elastic head-on collision between equal masses, the first body comes to rest and the second moves with the velocity of the first. So v1 = 0 m/s.
Question 8
A body of mass 2 kg moving at 4 m/s collides with a stationary body of mass 2 kg. They stick together after collision. The loss in kinetic energy is:
A. 0 J
B. 4 J
C. 16 J
D. 8 J
Solution: By conservation of momentum: (2)(4) = (2+2)v, v = 2 m/s. KEi = ½(2)(16) = 16 J. KEf = ½(4)(4) = 8 J. Loss = 16 − 8 = 8 J.
Question 9
A car of mass 1000 kg moves at 36 km/h on a level road. If the engine is switched off and the car comes to rest in 100 m due to friction, the coefficient of friction is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 0.1
B. 0.05
C. 0.5
D. 0.2
Solution: v = 36 km/h = 10 m/s. By work-energy theorem: ½mv² = μmgd. So μ = v²/(2gd) = 100/(2 × 10 × 100) = 100/2000 = 0.05.
Question 10
A block of mass 5 kg slides down a frictionless incline of height 4 m and then moves on a rough horizontal surface (μ = 0.5) until it stops. The distance travelled on the rough surface is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 4 m
B. 16 m
C. 8 m
D. 2 m
Solution: By energy conservation: mgh = μmgd. So d = h/μ = 4/0.5 = 8 m.
Question 11
The kinetic energy of a body is increased by 300%. The percentage increase in its momentum is:
A. 100%
B. 150%
C. 300%
D. 200%
Solution: p = √(2mKE). If KE becomes 4 × KE (300% increase), p' = √(2m × 4KE) = 2p. Percentage increase = (2p − p)/p × 100 = 100%.
Question 12
A force F = 3x² + 2x + 1 (in N) acts on a particle. The work done in displacing the particle from x = 0 to x = 2 m is:
A. 10 J
B. 14 J
C. 18 J
D. 12 J
Solution: W = ∫F dx from 0 to 2 = ∫(3x² + 2x + 1)dx = [x³ + x² + x] from 0 to 2 = (8 + 4 + 2) − 0 = 14 J.
Question 13
An engine develops 20 kW of power. The maximum speed at which a 1000 kg car can be driven on a level road against a resistance of 500 N is:
A. 20 m/s
B. 10 m/s
C. 50 m/s
D. 40 m/s
Solution: At maximum speed, the engine force equals resistance. P = Fv, so v = P/F = 20000/500 = 40 m/s.
Question 14
A bullet of mass 20 g moving at 500 m/s strikes a wooden block and penetrates 25 cm before stopping. The average resistive force of the block is:
A. 10000 N
B. 5000 N
C. 2500 N
D. 1000 N
Solution: By work-energy theorem: F × d = ½mv². F = mv²/(2d) = (0.02 × 250000)/(2 × 0.25) = 5000/0.5 = 10000 N.
Question 15
A particle of mass m is attached to a light string of length l and released from a horizontal position. The speed of the particle at the lowest point is:
A. √(gl)
B. √(gl/2)
C. √(2gl)
D. √(3gl)
Solution: The string is horizontal initially, so the height fallen = l (the length of string). By conservation of energy: mgl = ½mv², so v = √(2gl).
