Kinematics Level 1 — Sprint Test 5

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A body starts from the origin and moves along the X-axis such that the velocity at any instant is given by (4t3 − 2t), where t is in sec and velocity in m/s. What is the acceleration of the particle when it is 2 m from the origin?
A. 28 m/s2
B. 22 m/s2
C. 12 m/s2
D. 10 m/s2
Solution: v = 4t3 − 2t. Integrating, x = t4 − t2. When x = 2: t4 − t2 = 2 ⇒ t4 − t2 − 2 = 0 ⇒ (t2 − 2)(t2 + 1) = 0 ⇒ t = √2. a = dv/dt = 12t2 − 2. At t = √2: a = 12 × 2 − 2 = 22 m/s2.
Question 2
A particle is projected with velocity v0 along the x-axis. The deceleration of the particle is proportional to the square of the distance from the origin i.e. a = −αx2. The distance at which the particle stops is:
A. √(3v0 / (2α))
B. (3v0 / (2α))1/3
C. √(2v02 / (3α))
D. (3v02 / (2α))1/3
Solution: a = v(dv/dx) = −αx2. Integrating: ∫v dv = ∫−αx2 dx ⇒ v2/2 = −αx3/3 + C. At x = 0, v = v0, so C = v02/2. When v = 0: 0 = −αx3/3 + v02/2 ⇒ x3 = 3v02/(2α) ⇒ x = (3v02/(2α))1/3.
Question 3
Which one of the following represents uniformly accelerated motion?
A. x = √((t − a)/b)
B. x = (t − a)/b
C. t = √((x − a)/b)
D. x = √(t + a)
Solution: For option (C): t2 = (x − a)/b ⇒ x = bt2 + a. Differentiating, v = 2bt and a = 2b (constant). This represents uniformly accelerated motion.
Question 4
A body is thrown upwards. If air resistance is to be taken into account, then the time during which the body rises is:
A. Equal to the time of fall
B. Less than the time of fall
C. Greater than the time of fall
D. Twice the time of fall
Solution: During ascent, both gravity and air resistance act downward, giving a net deceleration greater than g. During descent, gravity acts downward but air resistance acts upward, giving a net acceleration less than g. The body decelerates faster on the way up and accelerates slower on the way down — so time of rise < time of fall.
Question 5
A particle is thrown upwards from ground. It experiences a constant air resistance force which produces a retardation of 2 ms−2. The ratio of time of ascent to time of descent is: (g = 10 ms−2)
A. 1 : 1
B. √(2/3)
C. 2/3
D. √(3/2)
Solution: During ascent: net deceleration = g + 2 = 12 m/s2. During descent: net acceleration = g − 2 = 8 m/s2. Let max height = H. Time of ascent: t1 = u/12, and H = u2/24. Time of descent: H = (1/2) × 8 × t22 ⇒ t2 = √(2H/8) = √(u2/96) = u/(4√6). Ratio: t1/t2 = (u/12) / (u/(4√6)) = 4√6/12 = √6/3 = √(2/3).
Question 6
Statement-1: Two bodies of masses M and m (M > m) are allowed to fall from the same height. If the air resistance for each be the same, then both bodies will reach the ground simultaneously. Statement-2: For the same air resistance, the acceleration of both bodies will be different.
A. Statement-1 is true, Statement-2 is true; Statement-2 is a correct explanation for Statement-1
B. Statement-1 is true, Statement-2 is true; Statement-2 is not a correct explanation for Statement-1
C. Statement-1 is true, Statement-2 is false
D. Statement-1 is false, Statement-2 is true
Solution: Statement-1 is false — different masses with the same air resistance force have different net accelerations (a = g − F/m), so the heavier body (M) reaches the ground first. Statement-2 is true — with the same resistive force F but different masses, aM = g − F/M and am = g − F/m are indeed different.
Question 7
A bus moves over a straight level road with a constant acceleration a. A boy in the bus drops a ball outside. The acceleration of the ball with respect to the bus and the earth are respectively:
A. a and g
B. a + g and g − a
C. √(a2 + g2) and g
D. √(a2 + g2) and a
Solution: With respect to the Earth: once released, the ball has only gravity acting on it, so its acceleration = g (downward). With respect to the bus: the ball has g (downward) plus a pseudo-acceleration a (backward, opposite to bus motion) ⇒ resultant = √(a2 + g2).
Question 8
A ball A is thrown up vertically with a speed u and at the same instant another ball B is released from a height h. At time t, the speed of A relative to B is:
A. u
B. 2u
C. u − 2gt
D. √(u2 − gt)
Solution: Taking upward as positive: vA = u − gt. vB = 0 − gt = −gt. Relative velocity of A with respect to B: vAB = vA − vB = (u − gt) − (−gt) = u. The relative speed is constant and equal to u throughout the motion.
Question 9
A body A is projected upwards with a velocity of 98 m/s. A second body B is projected upwards with the same initial velocity but after 4 sec. Both the bodies meet t seconds after A is projected. Then the value of t is: (g = 9.8 m/s2)
A. 6 sec
B. 8 sec
C. 10 sec
D. 12 sec
Solution: Position of A at time t: yA = 98t − (1/2)(9.8)t2. Position of B (launched at t = 4 s): yB = 98(t − 4) − (1/2)(9.8)(t − 4)2. Setting yA = yB: 98t − 4.9t2 = 98(t − 4) − 4.9(t − 4)2. Expanding: −4.9t2 = −392 − 4.9t2 + 39.2t − 78.4. ⇒ 0 = −470.4 + 39.2t ⇒ t = 470.4 / 39.2 = 12 s.
Question 10
Two cars A and B are at rest at the same point initially. If A starts with a uniform velocity of 40 m/s and B starts in the same direction with a constant acceleration of 4 m/s2, then B will catch A after:
A. 10 sec
B. 20 sec
C. 30 sec
D. 35 sec
Solution: Distance covered by A: sA = 40t. Distance covered by B: sB = (1/2) × 4 × t2 = 2t2. When B catches A: 2t2 = 40t ⇒ t(2t − 40) = 0 ⇒ t = 20 s (ignoring t = 0).
