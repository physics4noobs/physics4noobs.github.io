Gravitation — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
Two particles of masses 10 kg and 40 kg are separated by 1 m. The gravitational force between them is (G = 6.67 × 10−11 N·m²/kg²):
A. 2.668 × 10−8 N
B. 6.67 × 10−11 N
C. 2.668 × 10−8 N
D. 6.67 × 10−9 N
Solution: F = Gm1m2/r² = 6.67 × 10−11 × 10 × 40 / 1² = 6.67 × 400 × 10−11 = 2.668 × 10−8 N.
Question 2
The acceleration due to gravity at a height equal to the radius of the Earth (R) above its surface is (g0 = acceleration at surface):
A. g0/2
B. g0/4
C. g0/8
D. g0/16
Solution: g at height h: g' = g0R²/(R + h)². For h = R: g' = g0R²/(2R)² = g0/4.
Question 3
The escape velocity from the surface of the Earth is approximately (R = 6400 km, g = 9.8 m/s²):
A. 11.2 km/s
B. 7.9 km/s
C. 3.2 km/s
D. 22.4 km/s
Solution: ve = √(2gR) = √(2 × 9.8 × 6.4 × 106) = √(1.2544 × 108) ≈ 11.2 × 103 m/s = 11.2 km/s.
Question 4
The orbital velocity of a satellite close to the Earth's surface is approximately:
A. 11.2 km/s
B. 3.1 km/s
C. 5.6 km/s
D. 7.9 km/s
Solution: vo = √(gR) = √(9.8 × 6.4 × 106) ≈ 7.9 × 103 m/s = 7.9 km/s. Also vo = ve/√2 = 11.2/1.414 ≈ 7.9 km/s.
Question 5
The gravitational potential energy of a mass m at a distance r from the centre of the Earth (r > R) is:
A. GMm/r
B. −GMm/r
C. −GMm/r²
D. GMm/r²
Solution: The gravitational PE is U = −GMm/r. The negative sign indicates that the system is bound.
Question 6
If the radius of the Earth were to shrink by 2% keeping mass the same, the value of g on the surface would:
A. Decrease by 2%
B. Decrease by 4%
C. Increase by 4%
D. Increase by 2%
Solution: g = GM/R². So Δg/g = −2(ΔR/R). If R decreases by 2%, ΔR/R = −0.02, then Δg/g = −2(−0.02) = +0.04 = 4% increase.
Question 7
The time period of a geostationary satellite is:
A. 24 hours
B. 12 hours
C. 48 hours
D. 6 hours
Solution: A geostationary satellite orbits with the same angular velocity as the Earth's rotation, so its time period is 24 hours.
Question 8
The value of g at a depth d below the surface of the Earth (uniform density assumed) is:
A. g0(1 + d/R)
B. g0R²/(R − d)²
C. g0d/R
D. g0(1 − d/R)
Solution: Inside the Earth, g' = g0(1 − d/R). At the centre (d = R), g = 0, consistent with this formula.
Question 9
A planet has twice the mass and twice the radius of the Earth. The escape velocity from this planet compared to Earth is:
A. 2 times
B. Same
C. √2 times
D. Half
Solution: ve = √(2GM/R). For the planet: v' = √(2G(2M)/(2R)) = √(2GM/R) = ve. The escape velocity is the same.
Question 10
According to Kepler's third law, if the orbital radius of a planet is doubled, its time period becomes:
A. 2 times
B. 4 times
C. 2√2 times
D. 8 times
Solution: T² ∝ r³. If r' = 2r, then T'² = T² × (2)³ = 8T², so T' = T√8 = 2√2 T.
Question 11
The total energy of a satellite of mass m in a circular orbit of radius r around the Earth (mass M) is:
A. −GMm/(2r)
B. −GMm/r
C. GMm/(2r)
D. GMm/r
Solution: KE = GMm/(2r), PE = −GMm/r. Total energy E = KE + PE = GMm/(2r) − GMm/r = −GMm/(2r).
Question 12
The gravitational field intensity at the centre of a uniform spherical shell is:
A. GM/R²
B. Zero
C. GM/2R²
D. 2GM/R²
Solution: By the shell theorem, the gravitational field inside a uniform spherical shell is zero everywhere, including at the centre.
Question 13
If the distance between two masses is tripled, the gravitational force between them becomes:
A. 1/3 of the original
B. 3 times the original
C. 1/6 of the original
D. 1/9 of the original
Solution: F ∝ 1/r². If r becomes 3r, F' = F/(3)² = F/9.
Question 14
The minimum energy required to launch a body of mass m from the surface of the Earth to infinity is:
A. mgR
B. 2mgR
C. mgR/2
D. mg/R
Solution: Energy = GMm/R. Since g = GM/R², GM = gR². So energy = gR²m/R = mgR.
Question 15
Two satellites A and B orbit the Earth at radii r and 4r respectively. The ratio of their orbital speeds vA/vB is:
A. 1 : 2
B. 4 : 1
C. 2 : 1
D. 1 : 4
Solution: Orbital speed v = √(GM/r). So vA/vB = √(rB/rA) = √(4r/r) = 2. The ratio is 2 : 1.
