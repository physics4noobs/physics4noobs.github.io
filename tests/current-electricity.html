Current Electricity — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
Five resistors of 10 Ω each are connected in parallel. The equivalent resistance of the combination is:
A. 50 Ω
B. 2 Ω
C. 5 Ω
D. 0.5 Ω
Solution: For n identical resistors in parallel: Req = R/n = 10/5 = 2 Ω.
Question 2
A cell of EMF 2 V and internal resistance 0.5 Ω is connected to a 1.5 Ω external resistance. The current flowing through the circuit is:
A. 0.5 A
B. 2 A
C. 1 A
D. 4 A
Solution: I = EMF / (R + r) = 2 / (1.5 + 0.5) = 2 / 2 = 1 A.
Question 3
In a Wheatstone bridge, resistances in the four arms are P = 10 Ω, Q = 20 Ω, R = 15 Ω and S = 30 Ω. The bridge is:
A. Balanced
B. Unbalanced with current from B to D
C. Unbalanced with current from D to B
D. Cannot be determined
Solution: For a balanced Wheatstone bridge: P/Q = R/S. Here P/Q = 10/20 = 1/2, and R/S = 15/30 = 1/2. Since P/Q = R/S, the bridge is balanced.
Question 4
The resistivity of a wire depends on:
A. Length of the wire
B. Cross-sectional area
C. Shape of the wire
D. Material and temperature
Solution: Resistivity (ρ) is an intrinsic property of the material. It depends on the nature of the material and its temperature, not on the geometry (length, area, or shape) of the conductor.
Question 5
Two cells of EMFs E1 = 4 V and E2 = 8 V with internal resistances r1 = 1 Ω and r2 = 2 Ω are connected in parallel. The equivalent EMF and internal resistance are:
A. 6 V, 1.5 Ω
B. 16/3 V, 2/3 Ω
C. 12 V, 3 Ω
D. 4 V, 0.5 Ω
Solution: For parallel combination: Eeq = (E1/r1 + E2/r2) / (1/r1 + 1/r2) = (4/1 + 8/2) / (1/1 + 1/2) = (4 + 4) / (3/2) = 8 × 2/3 = 16/3 V. req = 1/(1/r1 + 1/r2) = 1/(1 + 0.5) = 2/3 Ω.
Question 6
In a potentiometer experiment, a standard cell of EMF 1.02 V is balanced at 51 cm. An unknown EMF is balanced at 75 cm. The unknown EMF is:
A. 1.02 V
B. 1.0 V
C. 1.5 V
D. 2.0 V
Solution: In a potentiometer, E1/E2 = l1/l2. So E2 = E1 × l2/l1 = 1.02 × 75/51 = 1.02 × 25/17 = 1.5 V.
Question 7
A 100 W, 220 V bulb and a 60 W, 220 V bulb are connected in series to a 220 V supply. Which bulb glows brighter?
A. The 60 W bulb
B. The 100 W bulb
C. Both glow equally
D. Neither glows
Solution: In series, the same current flows through both. The bulb with higher resistance dissipates more power (P = I²R). R = V²/P, so R60W = 220²/60 > R100W = 220²/100. The 60 W bulb has higher resistance, so it glows brighter in series.
Question 8
The drift velocity of electrons in a copper wire carrying a current of 1 A with cross-sectional area 10−6 m² and electron density 8.5 × 1028 m−3 is approximately:
A. 7.4 × 10−2 m/s
B. 7.4 × 10−5 m/s
C. 7.4 × 10−3 m/s
D. 7.4 × 10−8 m/s
Solution: vd = I/(nAe) = 1 / (8.5 × 1028 × 10−6 × 1.6 × 10−19) = 1 / (8.5 × 1.6 × 103) = 1/13600 ≈ 7.4 × 10−5 m/s.
Question 9
The terminal voltage of a cell of EMF E and internal resistance r when drawing a current I is:
A. E + Ir
B. E × r
C. Ir
D. E − Ir
Solution: When a cell discharges (supplies current), the terminal voltage V = E − Ir, where Ir is the voltage drop across the internal resistance.
Question 10
A wire of resistance R is stretched uniformly to double its original length. Its new resistance becomes:
A. R
B. 2R
C. 4R
D. R/2
Solution: When a wire is stretched to double its length, volume remains constant: Al = A'(2l), so A' = A/2. New resistance R' = ρ(2l)/(A/2) = 4ρl/A = 4R.
Question 11
Using Kirchhoff's junction rule, at any junction in a circuit:
A. The sum of currents entering equals the sum of currents leaving
B. The sum of voltages is zero
C. The sum of resistances is zero
D. The current is the same in all branches
Solution: Kirchhoff's junction (current) rule states that the algebraic sum of currents at any junction is zero, i.e., total current entering = total current leaving. This is based on the conservation of charge.
Question 12
A meter bridge is balanced when the jockey touches the wire at 40 cm from the left end. If the known resistance on the right gap is 6 Ω, the unknown resistance is:
A. 6 Ω
B. 4 Ω
C. 9 Ω
D. 3 Ω
Solution: In a meter bridge, X/R = l/(100 − l). So X/6 = 40/60 = 2/3. Therefore X = 6 × 2/3 = 4 Ω.
Question 13
The power dissipated in a 4 Ω resistor carrying 3 A of current is:
A. 12 W
B. 24 W
C. 36 W
D. 48 W
Solution: Power P = I²R = (3)² × 4 = 9 × 4 = 36 W.
Question 14
In a potentiometer, the sensitivity can be increased by:
A. Increasing the length of the potentiometer wire
B. Decreasing the length of the potentiometer wire
C. Increasing the EMF of the driver cell
D. Using a thicker wire
Solution: Sensitivity of a potentiometer is the potential drop per unit length (k = V/L). For a given EMF, increasing the length L decreases k, meaning smaller EMFs can be detected, thus increasing sensitivity.
Question 15
A galvanometer of resistance 50 Ω gives full-scale deflection for 1 mA. To convert it into a voltmeter of range 0–10 V, the resistance to be connected in series is:
A. 950 Ω
B. 10000 Ω
C. 5000 Ω
D. 9950 Ω
Solution: For a voltmeter, R = V/Ig − G = 10/(1 × 10−3) − 50 = 10000 − 50 = 9950 Ω.
