Newton's Laws — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
Two blocks of masses 2 kg and 3 kg are placed in contact on a smooth horizontal surface. A force of 10 N is applied on the 2 kg block. The contact force between the blocks is:
A. 10 N
B. 4 N
C. 6 N
D. 5 N
Solution: System acceleration a = F/(m1 + m2) = 10/5 = 2 m/s². Contact force on the 3 kg block = m2 × a = 3 × 2 = 6 N.
Question 2
A block of mass 5 kg rests on a rough inclined plane of angle 30° with coefficient of kinetic friction μk = 0.2. The acceleration of the block sliding down the incline is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 5 m/s²
B. 3.27 m/s²
C. 6.73 m/s²
D. 1.73 m/s²
Solution: Along the incline: ma = mg sin30° − μkmg cos30°. So a = g(sin30° − 0.2 cos30°) = 10(0.5 − 0.2 × 0.866) = 10(0.5 − 0.1732) = 10 × 0.3268 = 3.27 m/s².
Question 3
Two masses 4 kg and 6 kg are connected by a light string passing over a frictionless pulley. The acceleration of the system is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 2 m/s²
B. 4 m/s²
C. 5 m/s²
D. 1 m/s²
Solution: For an Atwood machine, a = (m2 − m1)g/(m1 + m2) = (6 − 4) × 10/(6 + 4) = 20/10 = 2 m/s².
Question 4
The tension in the string of the Atwood machine in Q3 is:
A. 40 N
B. 60 N
C. 50 N
D. 48 N
Solution: T = 2m1m2g/(m1 + m2) = 2 × 4 × 6 × 10/(4 + 6) = 480/10 = 48 N.
Question 5
A block of mass 10 kg is placed on a rough horizontal surface (μs = 0.4). The minimum horizontal force required to just start moving the block is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 100 N
B. 40 N
C. 50 N
D. 20 N
Solution: The block just starts moving when the applied force equals the maximum static friction: F = μs × mg = 0.4 × 10 × 10 = 40 N.
Question 6
A person of mass 60 kg stands on a weighing scale inside a lift accelerating upward at 2 m/s². The reading of the scale is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 600 N
B. 480 N
C. 720 N
D. 120 N
Solution: When a lift accelerates upward, apparent weight = m(g + a) = 60(10 + 2) = 60 × 12 = 720 N.
Question 7
A body of mass 2 kg is kept on a rough horizontal surface. It is found that the body does not slide when a horizontal force of 7 N is applied. The coefficient of static friction is at least (g = 9.8 m/s²):
A. 0.36
B. 0.50
C. 0.25
D. 0.70
Solution: For the block not to slide: μs × mg ≥ F. So μs ≥ F/(mg) = 7/(2 × 9.8) = 7/19.6 ≈ 0.357 ≈ 0.36.
Question 8
Three blocks of masses 1 kg, 2 kg, and 3 kg are connected by strings and pulled by a force F = 12 N on a smooth surface. The tension in the string between the 1 kg and 2 kg blocks is:
A. 12 N
B. 6 N
C. 8 N
D. 10 N
Solution: System acceleration a = 12/(1+2+3) = 2 m/s². The string between the 1 kg and 2 kg blocks must pull the 2 kg and 3 kg blocks: T = (2 + 3) × 2 = 10 N.
Question 9
A 5 kg block is placed on a 10 kg block which lies on a smooth floor. The coefficient of friction between the blocks is 0.3. A horizontal force of 20 N is applied to the 5 kg block. The acceleration of the 10 kg block is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 2 m/s²
B. 1.5 m/s²
C. 1 m/s²
D. 0 m/s²
Solution: Maximum friction between blocks = μm1g = 0.3 × 5 × 10 = 15 N. Check if they move together: a = 20/15 = 1.33 m/s², friction needed = 10 × 1.33 = 13.3 N < 15 N, so they move together. a = 20/(5+10) = 20/15 = 1.33 m/s². But let us reconsider: if they move together a = 20/15 ≈ 1.33 m/s²; friction on lower block = 10 × 1.33 = 13.3 N < 15 N. So acceleration of 10 kg block = friction/m2 = 15/10 = 1.5 m/s². Actually since they do move together, both have a = 1.33 m/s². Rechecking: f needed for 10 kg = 10 × 1.33 = 13.3 N ≤ 15 N. So blocks move together at a = 4/3 ≈ 1.33 m/s². The closest answer is 1.5 m/s² which occurs if they slide: the 10 kg block gets friction = 15 N, a = 15/10 = 1.5 m/s². Since 20 N > 15 N the top block would accelerate at (20 − 15)/5 = 1 m/s² if sliding, but 1 < 1.5 means the top block is slower — contradiction. They must move together. a = 20/15 = 1.33 m/s². The correct answer is 1.5 m/s² for the lower block when blocks slide. Here blocks move together at 1.33 m/s², closest is B.
Question 10
A monkey of mass 40 kg climbs a rope that can sustain a maximum tension of 600 N. The maximum acceleration with which the monkey can climb upward is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 10 m/s²
B. 15 m/s²
C. 5 m/s²
D. 25 m/s²
Solution: While climbing upward: T − mg = ma. So a = (T − mg)/m = (600 − 40 × 10)/40 = (600 − 400)/40 = 200/40 = 5 m/s².
Question 11
A block of mass m is placed on a smooth inclined plane of angle θ and is connected by a string over a pulley to a block of mass m hanging vertically. For the system to be in equilibrium, θ must be:
A. 90°
B. 45°
C. 30°
D. 60°
Solution: For the hanging mass: T = mg. For the block on the incline: T = mg sinθ. Equating: mg = mg sinθ, so sinθ = 1, giving θ = 90°.
Question 12
A block of mass 2 kg is acted upon by two forces: 6 N horizontally to the right and 8 N vertically upward. What is the magnitude of its acceleration?
A. 3 m/s²
B. 5 m/s²
C. 7 m/s²
D. 10 m/s²
Solution: Net force = √(6² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 N. Acceleration a = F/m = 10/2 = 5 m/s².
Question 13
In a tug of war, two teams pull on a rope. Team A pulls with a force of 500 N and Team B with 450 N. The rope has mass 5 kg. The acceleration of the rope and the tension at a point midway along the rope are:
A. 10 m/s², 500 N
B. 10 m/s², 450 N
C. 10 m/s², 500 N
D. 10 m/s², 475 N
Solution: a = (500 − 450)/5 = 10 m/s² toward Team A. Consider the half of rope closer to Team B (mass 2.5 kg): T − 450 = 2.5 × 10 = 25, so T = 475 N. Alternatively, from Team A's side: 500 − T = 2.5 × 10, T = 475 N.
Question 14
A block rests on the floor of a lift. When the lift starts descending with acceleration g, the normal reaction on the block is:
A. mg
B. 2mg
C. 0
D. mg/2
Solution: When the lift descends with acceleration a = g, we apply Newton's second law: mg − N = ma = mg. So N = 0. The block experiences weightlessness (free fall).
Question 15
Two masses m1 = 5 kg and m2 = 10 kg are connected by a string passing over a pulley fixed to the edge of a smooth table. m1 hangs vertically while m2 lies on the table. The acceleration of the system is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 10/3 m/s²
B. 5 m/s²
C. 10/7 m/s²
D. 2 m/s²
Solution: The hanging mass provides the driving force. For the system: m1g = (m1 + m2)a. So a = m1g/(m1 + m2) = 5 × 10/(5 + 10) = 50/15 = 10/3 m/s².
