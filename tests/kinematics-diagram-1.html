Kinematics — Graphs & Diagrams (L1)

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A position-time (x-t) graph shows a particle moving on the X-axis. The curve rises (increasing x) and gradually flattens, becoming horizontal at time t = t0. Which option correctly describes the particle's motion?
A. The particle is continuously going in positive x direction
B. The particle is at rest
C. The velocity increases up to time t0, and then becomes constant
D. The particle moves at a constant velocity up to time t0, and then stops
Solution: The x-t graph shows x increasing linearly (constant slope = constant velocity) until t0, then becoming flat (slope = 0, particle stops). So the particle moves at constant velocity until t0, then stops.
Question 2
A ball is thrown vertically upward and returns back. Upward direction is taken as positive. Which description correctly represents the velocity-time (v-t) graph?
A. v starts at 0 and increases linearly with time
B. v starts positive, increases to a peak, then decreases back to 0
C. v is constant and positive throughout
D. v starts at a positive value and decreases linearly, crossing zero and becoming negative
Solution: Ball thrown up has initial positive velocity. Under gravity (constant −g), velocity decreases linearly: v = u − gt. It crosses zero at the top and becomes negative on the way down. This is a straight line from +u to negative values.
Question 3
An object is moving with uniform acceleration parallel to its instantaneous direction of motion. The displacement (s) vs velocity (v) graph is:
A. A straight line with positive slope (s increases linearly with v)
B. A straight line with negative slope
C. An upward-opening parabola (s increases as v2)
D. A curve that starts steep and flattens out
Solution: From v2 = u2 + 2as, we get s = (v2 − u2)/(2a). So s is proportional to v2, which gives an upward-opening parabola.
Question 4
The acceleration-time graph of a particle moving in a straight line shows: acceleration a = 4 m/s2 for 0 < t < 1 s, and a = 0 for 1 s < t < 2 s. The velocity at t = 0 is 2 m/s. The velocity after 2 seconds will be:
A. 6 m/s
B. 4 m/s
C. 2 m/s
D. 8 m/s
Solution: From t = 0 to 1 s: v = 2 + 4(1) = 6 m/s. From t = 1 to 2 s: a = 0, so v remains 6 m/s. Final velocity = 6 m/s.
Question 5
A particle moves along a straight line. Its velocity-displacement (v-s) graph is a straight line starting from the origin, making an angle of 60° with the s-axis, reaching v = 4 m/s at s = 3 m. What is the acceleration when the displacement is 3.0 m?
A. −4√3 m/s2
B. 3√3 m/s2
C. √3 m/s2
D. (4/√3) m/s2
Solution: The v-s graph is linear: v = (tan 60°)s = √3 · s. At s = 3 m, v = 4 m/s. Acceleration a = v(dv/ds). Since the line passes through the origin with positive slope but the velocity must decrease after the peak (making dv/ds negative here), dv/ds = −√3. So a = v(dv/ds) = 4 × (−√3) = −4√3 m/s2.
Question 6
The v-x graph for a particle moving along a straight line shows a curve passing through three marked points: point 1 (near the origin, v and x both small and increasing), point 2 (at the peak of the curve, maximum v), and point 3 (v decreasing as x increases further). At which of the marked points is the acceleration negative?
A. 1
B. 3
C. 1 and 2
D. 3 and 2
Solution: a = v(dv/dx). At point 3, v is positive but dv/dx is negative (the curve is decreasing), so a = v(dv/dx) < 0. At point 1 the slope is positive (a > 0), and at point 2 the slope is zero (a = 0). Only at point 3 is the acceleration negative.
Question 7
The displacement-time (s-t) graph for a particle shows: s increases slowly at first (concave up), then increases rapidly (steeper), then the rate of increase slows down (concave down), finally flattening to a constant value. The best description of the velocity-time (v-t) graph is:
A. v starts at zero, increases to a maximum, then decreases back to zero (bell-shaped curve)
B. v increases linearly with time
C. v is constant throughout
D. v starts at maximum and decreases to zero
Solution: The slope of the s-t graph gives the velocity. The slope starts at 0 (flat curve), increases (concave up portion), reaches a maximum, then decreases (concave down portion), and returns to 0 (flat again). This traces out a bell-shaped v-t curve.
Question 8
The velocity-position (v-s) graph of a particle moving along a straight line is a straight line: v = 20 m/s at s = 0 and v = 0 at s = 30 m (linearly decreasing). The acceleration of the particle at s = 15 m is:
A. (2/3) ms−2
B. −(2/3) ms−2
C. (20/3) ms−2
D. −(20/3) ms−2
Solution: The line equation: v = 20 − (20/30)s = 20 − (2/3)s. So dv/ds = −2/3. At s = 15 m: v = 20 − (2/3)(15) = 20 − 10 = 10 m/s. Acceleration a = v(dv/ds) = 10 × (−2/3) = −20/3 m/s2.
Question 9
The displacement of a body is given to be proportional to the cube of time elapsed (s ∝ t3). The velocity-time graph of the body is:
A. A straight line through origin
B. A parabola (v increases as t2)
C. A horizontal straight line (constant v)
D. An exponential curve
Solution: If s = kt3, then v = ds/dt = 3kt2. This is a parabolic relation in the v-t graph (v proportional to t2), which opens upward and passes through the origin.
Question 10
The v-x graph for a car on a straight road shows: velocity v = 0 for x < 10 m, then v increases linearly from 0 to 8 m/s as x goes from 10 m to 20 m. Which description matches the correct acceleration-position (a-x) graph?
A. a is constant and positive from x = 10 to 20 m, zero elsewhere
B. a increases linearly from x = 10 to 20 m
C. a is constant at 6.4 m/s2 from x = 10 to 20 m (since a = v · dv/dx increases linearly from 0 to 6.4), zero elsewhere
D. a decreases from 6.4 to 0 between x = 10 and 20 m, then becomes −6.4
Solution: In the region x = 10 to 20 m: v = 0.8(x − 10), so dv/dx = 0.8. Acceleration a = v(dv/dx) = 0.8(x − 10) × 0.8 = 0.64(x − 10). At x = 10 m: a = 0; at x = 20 m: a = 6.4 m/s2. So a increases linearly from 0 to 6.4 m/s2 between x = 10 and x = 20 m, and is zero elsewhere. The a-x graph shows this linearly increasing acceleration in that interval.
