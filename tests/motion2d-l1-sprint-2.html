Motion in 2D Level 1 — Sprint Test 2

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A particle is projected with a speed u. If after 2 seconds of projection it is found to be making an angle of 45° with the horizontal and 0° after 3 sec, then:
A. Angle of projection is tan−1(3)
B. Angle of projection is tan−1(1/3)
C. Speed of projection = 30√2 m/s
D. Speed of projection is 30 m/s
Solution: At t = 3 s the projectile is at maximum height, so vertical velocity = 0, meaning u sinθ = g × 3 = 30. At t = 2 s, vertical velocity = u sinθ − g × 2 = 30 − 20 = 10 m/s. Since angle is 45°, vertical velocity = horizontal velocity, so u cosθ = 10. Therefore tanθ = 30/10 = 3, giving θ = tan−1(3). Speed u = √(302 + 102) = √1000 = 10√10 m/s.
Question 2
Two balls A and B are projected from the same location simultaneously. Ball A is projected vertically upwards and ball B at 30° to the vertical. They reach the ground simultaneously. The velocities of projection of A and B are in the ratio:
A. √3 : 1
B. 1 : √3
C. √3 : 2
D. 2 : √3
Solution: Both reach the ground simultaneously, so they have the same time of flight. For A (vertical): T = 2uA/g. For B (at 30° to vertical, i.e. 60° to horizontal): T = 2uBsin60°/g = 2uB(√3/2)/g = uB√3/g. Equating: 2uA/g = uB√3/g ⇒ uA/uB = √3/2.
Question 3
A projectile has a range R and time of flight T. If the range is doubled (by increasing the speed of projection, without changing the angle of projection), the time of flight will become:
A. T/√2
B. √2 T
C. T/2
D. 2T
Solution: R = u2sin2θ/g ∝ u2. T = 2u sinθ/g ∝ u. If R doubles, u2 doubles, so u becomes u√2. Therefore T becomes T√2.
Question 4
The maximum height attained by a projectile is increased by 10% by increasing its speed of projection, without changing the angle of projection. The percentage increase in the horizontal range will be:
A. 20%
B. 15%
C. 10%
D. 5%
Solution: H = u2sin2θ/(2g) ∝ u2. R = u2sin2θ/g ∝ u2. Both H and R are proportional to u2. If H increases by 10%, then u2 increases by 10%, so R also increases by 10%.
Question 5
The ceiling of a tunnel is 5 m high. What is the maximum horizontal distance that a ball thrown with a speed of 20 ms−1 can go without hitting the ceiling of the tunnel? (g = 10 ms−2)
A. 10√3 m
B. 20√3 m
C. 30√3 m
D. 40√3 m
Solution: Maximum height H = u2sin2θ/(2g) ≤ 5. So sin2θ ≤ 5 × 2 × 10/400 = 1/4, sinθ ≤ 1/2, θ ≤ 30°. For maximum range at θ = 30°: R = u2sin2θ/g = 400 × sin60°/10 = 40 × (√3/2) = 20√3 m.
Question 6
A body is thrown at different angles with the same speed K and from the same point and it has the same range in both the cases. If x1 and x2 are the heights attained in the two cases, then x1 + x2 is equal to:
A. K2/g
B. 2K2/g
C. K2/2g
D. K2/4g
Solution: Same range with same speed requires complementary angles: θ and (90° − θ). x1 = K2sin2θ/(2g) and x2 = K2cos2θ/(2g). x1 + x2 = K2(sin2θ + cos2θ)/(2g) = K2/(2g).
Question 7
The speed of projectile at its maximum height is (√3/2) times its initial speed. If the range of the projectile is N times the maximum height attained by it, then the value of N is:
A. 3/5
B. 4/3
C. 3√2
D. 4√3
Solution: At max height, speed = u cosθ = (√3/2)u, so cosθ = √3/2, θ = 30°. R = u2sin60°/g = u2√3/(2g). H = u2sin230°/(2g) = u2/(8g). N = R/H = [u2√3/(2g)] / [u2/(8g)] = 4√3.
Question 8
A body is projected at time t = 0 from a certain point on a planet’s surface with a certain velocity at a certain angle with the planet’s surface (assumed horizontal). The horizontal and vertical displacements x and y (in meters) respectively vary with time t (in second) as x = 10√3 t ; y = 10t − t2. What is the magnitude and direction of the velocity with which the body is projected?
A. 20 ms−1 at an angle of 30° with the horizontal
B. 20 ms−1 at an angle of 60° with the horizontal
C. 10 ms−1 at an angle of 30° with the horizontal
D. 10 ms−1 at an angle of 60° with the horizontal
Solution: Horizontal velocity ux = dx/dt = 10√3 ms−1. Vertical velocity at t = 0: uy = dy/dt|t=0 = 10 ms−1. Magnitude u = √(ux2 + uy2) = √(300 + 100) = √400 = 20 ms−1. Angle θ = tan−1(uy/ux) = tan−1(10/10√3) = tan−1(1/√3) = 30°.
Question 9
Which one of the following statements is NOT true about the motion of a projectile?
A. The time of flight of a projectile is proportional to the speed with which it is projected
B. The horizontal range of a projectile is proportional to the square-root of the speed with which it is projected
C. For a given speed of projection, the angle of projection for maximum range is 45°
D. At maximum height, the acceleration due to gravity is perpendicular to the velocity of the projectile
Solution: B is NOT true. R = u2sin2θ/g ∝ u2, so range is proportional to the square of the speed, not the square-root. A is true: T = 2u sinθ/g ∝ u. C is true. D is true: at maximum height, velocity is purely horizontal while g acts vertically.
Question 10
A body is projected with a velocity v⃗ = (3î + 4ĵ) ms−1. (g = 10 ms−2). The maximum height attained by the body is:
A. 0.8 m
B. 8 m
C. 80 m
D. 800 m
Solution: The vertical component of velocity is uy = 4 ms−1. Maximum height H = uy2/(2g) = 16/(2 × 10) = 16/20 = 0.8 m.
