Electromagnetic Induction — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
A conducting rod of length 0.5 m moves with a velocity of 4 m/s perpendicular to a uniform magnetic field of 0.2 T. The EMF induced in the rod is:
A. 0.2 V
B. 0.4 V
C. 0.8 V
D. 1.0 V
Solution: Motional EMF ε = Bvl = 0.2 × 4 × 0.5 = 0.4 V.
Question 2
According to Lenz's law, the direction of the induced current is such that it:
A. Opposes the change in magnetic flux that produces it
B. Aids the change in magnetic flux
C. Is always clockwise
D. Is always counterclockwise
Solution: Lenz's law states that the induced current flows in a direction such that it opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it. This is a consequence of conservation of energy.
Question 3
The magnetic flux through a coil of 200 turns changes from 0.1 Wb to 0.04 Wb in 0.02 s. The magnitude of the induced EMF is:
A. 30 V
B. 300 V
C. 600 V
D. 60 V
Solution: By Faraday's law, |ε| = N|dΦ/dt| = 200 × |0.04 − 0.1| / 0.02 = 200 × 0.06/0.02 = 200 × 3 = 600 V.
Question 4
The self-inductance of a solenoid of 500 turns, length 50 cm and cross-sectional area 4 × 10−4 m² is:
A. 2.51 × 10−5 H
B. 2.51 × 10−3 H
C. 1.26 × 10−3 H
D. 2.51 × 10−4 H
Solution: L = μ0N²A/l = (4π × 10−7 × 500² × 4 × 10−4) / 0.5 = (4π × 10−7 × 250000 × 4 × 10−4) / 0.5 = (4π × 10−7 × 100) / 0.5 = 4π × 10−5 / 0.5 = 8π × 10−5 ≈ 2.51 × 10−4 H.
Question 5
The energy stored in an inductor of 5 H carrying a current of 4 A is:
A. 20 J
B. 40 J
C. 80 J
D. 10 J
Solution: Energy U = ½LI² = ½ × 5 × (4)² = ½ × 5 × 16 = 40 J.
Question 6
A circular coil of radius 10 cm and 50 turns is rotated about its vertical diameter with an angular speed of 300 rad/s in a uniform horizontal magnetic field of 0.1 T. The maximum EMF induced is:
A. 15π V
B. 1.5π V
C. 150π V
D. 0.15π V
Solution: ε0 = NBAω = 50 × 0.1 × π × (0.1)² × 300 = 50 × 0.1 × 0.01π × 300 = 15π V.
Question 7
Two coils have mutual inductance of 0.5 H. If the current in the first coil changes at a rate of 10 A/s, the EMF induced in the second coil is:
A. 0.5 V
B. 0.05 V
C. 5 V
D. 50 V
Solution: ε = M × dI/dt = 0.5 × 10 = 5 V.
Question 8
In an AC generator, the induced EMF is maximum when the angle between the magnetic field and the area vector of the coil is:
A. 0°
B. 45°
C. 180°
D. 90°
Solution: Φ = NBA cosθ. ε = −dΦ/dt = NBAω sinθ. The EMF is maximum when sinθ = 1, i.e., θ = 90°. At this point the coil plane is parallel to B (area vector perpendicular to B).
Question 9
A metallic ring is dropped from rest above a region with a horizontal magnetic field. The acceleration of the ring as it enters the field region is:
A. Less than g
B. Equal to g
C. Greater than g
D. Zero
Solution: As the ring enters the magnetic field, the changing flux induces a current (by Lenz's law) that opposes the motion. This creates an upward force that opposes gravity, so net acceleration is less than g.
Question 10
A coil of self-inductance 2 H carries a current of 1 A. The time taken for the current to reduce to zero if the EMF induced is 4 V is:
A. 1 s
B. 0.5 s
C. 2 s
D. 0.25 s
Solution: ε = L × dI/dt. So dI/dt = ε/L = 4/2 = 2 A/s. Time to go from 1 A to 0 A = ΔI/(dI/dt) = 1/2 = 0.5 s.
Question 11
A sliding rod of length l moves with velocity v on two parallel conducting rails in a uniform field B (perpendicular to rails). If the circuit resistance is R, the force needed to maintain constant velocity is:
A. BIl
B. Bvl/R
C. B²l²v/R
D. B²v²l/R
Solution: Induced EMF = Bvl. Current I = Bvl/R. Force on rod due to field = BIl = B × (Bvl/R) × l = B²l²v/R. To maintain constant velocity, an equal external force is needed.
Question 12
The coefficient of mutual inductance between two coils depends on:
A. The geometry of the coils and their relative orientation
B. The current flowing through the coils
C. The rate of change of current
D. The resistance of the coils
Solution: Mutual inductance M depends on the geometry (number of turns, area, length) and the relative orientation/distance of the two coils. It does not depend on the current or its rate of change.
Question 13
Two inductors of self-inductance L1 = 3 mH and L2 = 4 mH are connected in series with zero mutual inductance. The equivalent inductance is:
A. 12/7 mH
B. 7 mH
C. 1 mH
D. 12 mH
Solution: For inductors in series (no mutual inductance): Leq = L1 + L2 = 3 + 4 = 7 mH.
Question 14
Eddy currents can be reduced by:
A. Increasing the thickness of the conductor
B. Using a single solid block of metal
C. Increasing the magnetic field
D. Using laminated sheets of metal
Solution: Eddy currents are reduced by laminating the core, i.e., using thin insulated sheets of metal instead of a solid block. This increases the resistance to eddy current paths and reduces their magnitude.
Question 15
The dimensions of magnetic flux are:
A. [ML²T−3A−1]
B. [ML²T−1A−1]
C. [ML²T−2A−1]
D. [MLT−2A−1]
Solution: Magnetic flux Φ = BA. B has dimensions [MT−2A−1] (from F = qvB). So Φ = [MT−2A−1] × [L²] = [ML²T−2A−1]. Alternatively, Φ has units of Wb = V·s = kg·m²·s−2·A−1.
