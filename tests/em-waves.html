Electromagnetic Waves — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
Maxwell's modification of Ampere's circuital law introduced:
A. Conduction current
B. Displacement current
C. Eddy current
D. Thermionic current
Solution: Maxwell modified Ampere's law by adding the displacement current term: Id = ε0 dΦE/dt. This accounts for the changing electric field between capacitor plates and ensures continuity of current.
Question 2
The speed of electromagnetic waves in vacuum is given by:
A. 1/√(μ0ε0)
B. √(μ0ε0)
C. μ0ε0
D. 1/(μ0ε0)
Solution: c = 1/√(μ0ε0) = 1/√(4π × 10−7 × 8.85 × 10−12) ≈ 3 × 108 m/s.
Question 3
Which of the following electromagnetic waves has the longest wavelength?
A. Gamma rays
B. X-rays
C. Radio waves
D. Microwaves
Solution: In the EM spectrum, the order from longest to shortest wavelength is: Radio waves > Microwaves > Infrared > Visible > Ultraviolet > X-rays > Gamma rays. Radio waves have the longest wavelength.
Question 4
Electromagnetic waves are:
A. Longitudinal waves
B. Mechanical waves
C. Longitudinal and require a medium
D. Transverse waves that do not require a medium
Solution: EM waves are transverse in nature: E and B oscillate perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation. They do not require a material medium and can travel through vacuum.
Question 5
The wavelength range of visible light is approximately:
A. 400 nm to 700 nm
B. 1 nm to 400 nm
C. 700 nm to 1 mm
D. 1 mm to 1 m
Solution: Visible light ranges from about 400 nm (violet) to 700 nm (red). Below 400 nm is ultraviolet and above 700 nm is infrared.
Question 6
In an electromagnetic wave, the electric and magnetic fields are:
A. Parallel to each other
B. Perpendicular to each other and in phase
C. Perpendicular and out of phase by 90°
D. Anti-parallel to each other
Solution: In an EM wave, E and B are mutually perpendicular and also perpendicular to the direction of propagation. They oscillate in phase, reaching their maxima and minima at the same time.
Question 7
Which EM radiation is used in RADAR systems?
A. Infrared
B. Ultraviolet
C. Microwaves
D. Radio waves
Solution: RADAR (Radio Detection And Ranging) uses microwaves because of their short wavelength, which provides good resolution and directional properties for detecting objects.
Question 8
The ratio of the amplitudes of the electric field (E0) and magnetic field (B0) in an electromagnetic wave in vacuum is equal to:
A. c (speed of light)
B. 1/c
C. c²
D. √c
Solution: In an EM wave, E0/B0 = c = 3 × 108 m/s. This relationship holds because E and B are related by E = cB at every instant.
Question 9
The displacement current through a parallel plate capacitor of capacitance 20 μF is 0.1 A. The rate of change of voltage across the capacitor is:
A. 2 × 103 V/s
B. 0.5 × 103 V/s
C. 2 × 106 V/s
D. 5 × 103 V/s
Solution: Id = C × dV/dt. So dV/dt = Id/C = 0.1 / (20 × 10−6) = 0.1 / (2 × 10−5) = 5 × 103 V/s.
Question 10
The intensity of an electromagnetic wave is proportional to:
A. E0
B. E0²
C. E03
D. √E0
Solution: The intensity I = ½ε0cE0². Since ε0 and c are constants, I ∝ E0². Intensity is proportional to the square of the amplitude of the electric field.
Question 11
Which of the following EM waves is produced by the deceleration of high-energy electrons striking a metal target?
A. Gamma rays
B. Ultraviolet
C. X-rays
D. Infrared
Solution: X-rays are produced when high-energy electrons are decelerated upon hitting a heavy metal target (bremsstrahlung/braking radiation). Gamma rays are emitted from atomic nuclei during radioactive decay.
Question 12
The radiation pressure exerted by an electromagnetic wave of intensity I on a perfectly absorbing surface is:
A. I/c
B. 2I/c
C. I/c²
D. Ic
Solution: For a perfectly absorbing surface, radiation pressure P = I/c. For a perfectly reflecting surface, P = 2I/c (momentum change is doubled).
Question 13
The ozone layer in the atmosphere absorbs which type of radiation from the sun?
A. Infrared
B. Visible light
C. Radio waves
D. Ultraviolet
Solution: The ozone (O3) layer in the stratosphere absorbs most of the harmful ultraviolet radiation from the sun, protecting life on Earth from UV-induced damage.
Question 14
An EM wave of frequency 1010 Hz belongs to which part of the EM spectrum?
A. Radio waves
B. Microwaves
C. Infrared
D. Visible light
Solution: Wavelength λ = c/f = 3 × 108 / 1010 = 3 × 10−2 m = 3 cm. This falls in the microwave range (wavelength ~1 mm to ~30 cm, frequency ~109 to ~3 × 1011 Hz).
Question 15
The energy of an electromagnetic wave is carried by:
A. Only the electric field
B. Only the magnetic field
C. Both the electric and magnetic fields equally
D. Neither field; it is carried by the medium
Solution: In an EM wave, the energy is shared equally between the electric and magnetic fields. The energy density u = ½ε0E² + B²/(2μ0), and at any instant ½ε0E² = B²/(2μ0), confirming equal sharing.
