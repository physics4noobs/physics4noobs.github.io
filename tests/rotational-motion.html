Rotational Motion — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
The moment of inertia of a solid sphere of mass M and radius R about its diameter is:
A. MR²
B. (2/5)MR²
C. (1/2)MR²
D. (2/3)MR²
Solution: The standard result for a solid sphere about its diameter is I = (2/5)MR².
Question 2
The moment of inertia of a thin uniform rod of mass M and length L about an axis through one end and perpendicular to its length is:
A. ML²/12
B. ML²/6
C. ML²/3
D. ML²/2
Solution: About centre: Ic = ML²/12. By parallel axis theorem about one end: I = ML²/12 + M(L/2)² = ML²/12 + ML²/4 = ML²/3.
Question 3
A disc of moment of inertia I is rotating with angular velocity ω. A second disc of moment of inertia 2I (initially at rest) is gently placed on top. The final angular velocity of the combination is:
A. ω/3
B. ω/2
C. 2ω/3
D. ω
Solution: By conservation of angular momentum: Iω = (I + 2I)ω'. So ω' = Iω/3I = ω/3.
Question 4
A solid cylinder, a hollow cylinder, a solid sphere, and a hollow sphere all of same mass and radius roll down a smooth incline from the same height. Which one reaches the bottom first?
A. Hollow cylinder
B. Solid cylinder
C. Hollow sphere
D. Solid sphere
Solution: Acceleration for rolling: a = g sinθ/(1 + I/(MR²)). The body with the smallest I/(MR²) has the largest acceleration. Solid sphere: 2/5, Solid cylinder: 1/2, Hollow sphere: 2/3, Hollow cylinder: 1. So the solid sphere reaches first.
Question 5
A wheel of radius 0.5 m rotates with a constant angular acceleration of 2 rad/s². The tangential acceleration of a point on the rim is:
A. 0.5 m/s²
B. 1 m/s²
C. 2 m/s²
D. 4 m/s²
Solution: Tangential acceleration at = Rα = 0.5 × 2 = 1 m/s².
Question 6
A torque of 10 N·m is applied on a wheel of moment of inertia 5 kg·m². The angular acceleration produced is:
A. 2 rad/s²
B. 50 rad/s²
C. 0.5 rad/s²
D. 5 rad/s²
Solution: τ = Iα. So α = τ/I = 10/5 = 2 rad/s².
Question 7
The moment of inertia of a disc of mass M and radius R about an axis tangent to the disc and in its plane is:
A. MR²/2
B. MR²
C. (5/4)MR²
D. (3/2)MR²
Solution: MOI of disc about a diameter = MR²/4. By parallel axis theorem (shifting by R): I = MR²/4 + MR² = (5/4)MR².
Question 8
A solid cylinder of mass 10 kg rolls without slipping on a horizontal surface with velocity 5 m/s. Its total kinetic energy is:
A. 125 J
B. 187.5 J
C. 250 J
D. 375 J
Solution: For a solid cylinder rolling without slipping: KE = ½mv²(1 + I/(mR²)) = ½mv²(1 + ½) = ¾mv² = ¾ × 10 × 25 = 187.5 J.
Question 9
An ice skater spinning with arms outstretched has moment of inertia 4 kg·m² and angular velocity 2 rad/s. On pulling arms in, the moment of inertia becomes 1 kg·m². The new angular velocity is:
A. 2 rad/s
B. 4 rad/s
C. 6 rad/s
D. 8 rad/s
Solution: By conservation of angular momentum: I1ω1 = I2ω2. So ω2 = 4 × 2/1 = 8 rad/s.
Question 10
A flywheel rotating at 600 rpm is brought to rest uniformly in 10 seconds. The number of revolutions made before stopping is:
A. 50
B. 100
C. 25
D. 300
Solution: Average angular velocity = (600 + 0)/2 = 300 rpm = 5 rev/s. Number of revolutions = 5 × 10 = 50.
Question 11
Four particles each of mass m are placed at the four corners of a square of side a. The moment of inertia about an axis passing through the centre and perpendicular to the plane is:
A. ma²
B. 4ma²
C. 2ma²
D. ½ma²
Solution: Each particle is at distance a/√2 from the centre (half-diagonal). I = 4 × m × (a/√2)² = 4 × m × a²/2 = 2ma².
Question 12
The angular momentum of a particle of mass 2 kg moving in a circle of radius 3 m with speed 6 m/s is:
A. 12 kg·m²/s
B. 36 kg·m²/s
C. 18 kg·m²/s
D. 6 kg·m²/s
Solution: L = mvr = 2 × 6 × 3 = 36 kg·m²/s.
Question 13
A uniform disc and a uniform ring, each of mass M and radius R, are rolling without slipping with the same kinetic energy. The ratio of their speeds (vdisc/vring) is:
A. √(4/3)
B. √(3/4)
C. 1
D. √2
Solution: KEdisc = ¾Mvd², KEring = Mvr². For equal KE: ¾vd² = vr², so vd/vr = √(4/3).
Question 14
A constant torque of 20 N·m acts on a wheel initially at rest. After 5 seconds, the angular momentum of the wheel is:
A. 4 kg·m²/s
B. 20 kg·m²/s
C. 50 kg·m²/s
D. 100 kg·m²/s
Solution: τ = dL/dt. For constant torque, L = τ × t = 20 × 5 = 100 kg·m²/s.
Question 15
A solid sphere of mass 2 kg and radius 0.1 m rolls without slipping down an incline of angle 30°. Its acceleration is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 5 m/s²
B. 5/3 m/s²
C. 25/7 m/s²
D. 10/3 m/s²
Solution: For a solid sphere rolling down an incline: a = g sinθ/(1 + 2/5) = g sinθ/(7/5) = 5g sinθ/7 = 5 × 10 × 0.5/7 = 25/7 m/s².
