Electrostatics — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
Two point charges of +2 μC and +8 μC are separated by 12 cm in vacuum. The point on the line joining them where the net electric field is zero lies at a distance from the +2 μC charge of:
A. 6 cm
B. 8 cm
C. 4 cm
D. 3 cm
Solution: For the net field to be zero between two like charges, the point is closer to the smaller charge. Let x be the distance from +2 μC. Then kq1/x² = kq2/(12 − x)². So 2/x² = 8/(12 − x)². Taking square root: √2/x = 2√2/(12 − x), giving 12 − x = 2x, so x = 4 cm.
Question 2
The electric flux through a closed surface enclosing a charge of 8.85 × 10−8 C is (take ε0 = 8.85 × 10−12 C²/N·m²):
A. 103 N·m²/C
B. 104 N·m²/C
C. 105 N·m²/C
D. 102 N·m²/C
Solution: By Gauss's law, Φ = q/ε0 = 8.85 × 10−8 / 8.85 × 10−12 = 104 N·m²/C.
Question 3
The force between two charges of 1 C each separated by 1 m in vacuum is approximately:
A. 9 × 109 N
B. 9 × 106 N
C. 9 × 103 N
D. 9 × 1012 N
Solution: By Coulomb's law, F = kq1q2/r² = 9 × 109 × 1 × 1 / 1² = 9 × 109 N.
Question 4
An electric dipole of moment p is placed in a uniform electric field E. The torque experienced by the dipole is maximum when the angle between p and E is:
A. 0°
B. 180°
C. 45°
D. 90°
Solution: Torque τ = pE sinθ. This is maximum when sinθ = 1, i.e., θ = 90°.
Question 5
Three capacitors of capacitances 2 μF, 3 μF, and 6 μF are connected in series. The equivalent capacitance of the combination is:
A. 11 μF
B. 1 μF
C. 0.5 μF
D. 2 μF
Solution: For series combination: 1/Ceq = 1/2 + 1/3 + 1/6 = (3 + 2 + 1)/6 = 6/6 = 1. So Ceq = 1 μF.
Question 6
A parallel plate capacitor with air between the plates has capacitance C. If the distance between the plates is doubled and a dielectric of constant K = 4 is introduced, the new capacitance is:
A. C
B. 4C
C. 2C
D. C/2
Solution: Original C = ε0A/d. New capacitance C' = Kε0A/(2d) = 4ε0A/(2d) = 2ε0A/d = 2C.
Question 7
The energy stored in a capacitor of 10 μF charged to 200 V is:
A. 0.2 J
B. 0.4 J
C. 2 J
D. 0.02 J
Solution: Energy U = ½CV² = ½ × 10 × 10−6 × (200)² = ½ × 10−5 × 4 × 104 = 0.2 J.
Question 8
The electric potential at a point on the axial line of a short electric dipole at distance r from its centre is proportional to:
A. 1/r
B. 1/r³
C. 1/r4
D. 1/r²
Solution: The electric potential at a point on the axial line of a short dipole is V = kp/r² (for r >> a). Hence V ∝ 1/r².
Question 9
A charge Q is distributed uniformly on a thin spherical shell of radius R. The electric field at a point inside the shell (r < R) is:
A. kQ/r²
B. Zero
C. kQ/R²
D. kQr/R³
Solution: By Gauss's law, for a Gaussian surface inside a uniformly charged spherical shell, the enclosed charge is zero. Therefore, the electric field inside the shell is zero.
Question 10
Two identical capacitors are first connected in series and then in parallel across a battery. The ratio of total energy stored in the series combination to the parallel combination is:
A. 1 : 2
B. 2 : 1
C. 1 : 4
D. 4 : 1
Solution: For identical capacitors of capacitance C: series Ceq = C/2; parallel Ceq = 2C. Energy = ½CeqV². Ratio = (C/2)/(2C) = 1/4, i.e., 1 : 4.
Question 11
The electric field intensity at a point on the equatorial line of a short dipole at distance r is proportional to:
A. 1/r³
B. 1/r²
C. 1/r
D. 1/r4
Solution: The electric field on the equatorial line of a short dipole is E = kp/r³ (for r >> a). Hence E ∝ 1/r³.
Question 12
A conducting sphere of radius 10 cm has an unknown charge. If the electric potential at a distance of 20 cm from its centre is 1.5 kV, the surface charge density on the sphere is approximately:
A. 26.5 nC/m²
B. 265 nC/m²
C. 2.65 μC/m²
D. 53 nC/m²
Solution: V = kQ/r, so Q = Vr/k = 1500 × 0.2 / (9 × 109) = 33.3 × 10−9 C. Surface charge density σ = Q/(4πR²) = 33.3 × 10−9 / (4π × 0.01) ≈ 265 × 10−9 C/m² = 265 nC/m².
Question 13
A charge of +5 μC is placed at the centre of a cube of side 10 cm. The electric flux through one face of the cube is:
A. 5.65 × 105 N·m²/C
B. 5.65 × 104 N·m²/C
C. 5.65 × 103 N·m²/C
D. 9.41 × 104 N·m²/C
Solution: Total flux through the cube = Q/ε0 = 5 × 10−6 / 8.85 × 10−12 = 5.65 × 105 N·m²/C. By symmetry, flux through one face = total/6 = 5.65 × 105/6 ≈ 9.41 × 104 N·m²/C.
Question 14
Work done in moving a charge of 2 μC from a point at potential 100 V to a point at potential 50 V is:
A. 10−4 J
B. −10−3 J
C. −10−4 J
D. 10−3 J
Solution: Work done by external agent W = q(Vf − Vi) = 2 × 10−6 × (50 − 100) = 2 × 10−6 × (−50) = −10−4 J. The negative sign indicates the electric field does the work.
Question 15
Two capacitors of 6 μF and 3 μF are connected in series across a 300 V supply. The charge on each capacitor is:
A. 600 μC
B. 900 μC
C. 300 μC
D. 1800 μC
Solution: In series, 1/Ceq = 1/6 + 1/3 = 1/6 + 2/6 = 3/6 = 1/2. So Ceq = 2 μF. Charge Q = Ceq × V = 2 × 10−6 × 300 = 600 μC. In series, each capacitor holds the same charge.
