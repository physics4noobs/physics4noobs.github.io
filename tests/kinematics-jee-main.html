Question 1 [JEE Main 2002]
Two balls A and B of same masses are thrown from the top of a building. A is thrown upward with speed V and B is thrown downward with speed V, then:
A. Velocity of A is more than B at the ground
B. Velocity of B is more than A at the ground
C. Both A and B strike the ground with same velocity
D. None of these
Solution: Both balls reach the ground with the same speed. Using energy conservation: v2 = V2 + 2gh for both, regardless of direction of throw. The initial kinetic energy is the same for both.
Question 2 [JEE Main 2002]
Speeds of two identical cars are u and 4u at a specific instant. The ratio of the respective distances in which the two cars are stopped from that instant is:
A. 1 : 1
B. 1 : 4
C. 1 : 8
D. 1 : 16
Solution: Stopping distance ∝ u2 (using v2 = u2 − 2as, with v = 0 and same deceleration). Ratio = u2 : (4u)2 = u2 : 16u2 = 1 : 16.
Question 3 [JEE Main 2002]
A bullet fired into a fixed target loses half of its velocity after penetrating 3 cm. How much further will it penetrate before coming to rest, assuming constant resistance?
A. 1.0 cm
B. 1.5 cm
C. 2.0 cm
D. 3.0 cm
Solution: After 3 cm: (u/2)2 = u2 − 2a(3), giving (3/4)u2 = 6a, so a = u2/8. For the remaining distance s: 0 = (u/2)2 − 2(u2/8)s → s = (u2/4) / (u2/4) = 1 cm.
Question 4 [JEE Main 2003]
A car moving at 50 km/h can be stopped by brakes after at least 6 m. If the same car is moving at 100 km/h, the minimum stopping distance is:
A. 12 m
B. 18 m
C. 24 m
D. 6 m
Solution: Stopping distance s ∝ v2. So s2/s1 = (100/50)2 = 4. s2 = 4 × 6 = 24 m.
Question 5 [JEE Main 2004]
A particle moves in a straight line with retardation proportional to its displacement. Its loss of kinetic energy for any displacement x is proportional to:
A. x2
B. ex
C. x
D. logex
Solution: Retardation a = −kx. Using v dv/dx = −kx and integrating: v2 = u2 − kx2. Loss in KE = (1/2)m(u2 − v2) = (1/2)mkx2 ∝ x2.
Question 6 [JEE Main 2004]
A ball is released from the top of a tower of height h metres. It takes T seconds to reach the ground. What is the position of the ball at T/3 seconds?
A. (8h/9) metre from the ground
B. (7h/9) metre from the ground
C. (h/9) metre from the ground
D. (17h/18) metre from the ground
Solution: h = (1/2)gT2. Distance fallen at T/3: d = (1/2)g(T/3)2 = (1/2)gT2/9 = h/9. Height from ground = h − h/9 = 8h/9.
Question 7 [JEE Main 2004]
A car travelling at 60 km/h can stop within 20 m. If going twice as fast (120 km/h), the stopping distance will be:
A. 60 m
B. 40 m
C. 20 m
D. 80 m
Solution: s ∝ v2. s2 = 20 × (120/60)2 = 20 × 4 = 80 m.
Question 8 [JEE Main 2005]
A car starting from rest accelerates at rate f through distance S, then continues at constant speed for time t and decelerates at rate f/2 to come to rest. The total distance covered is 15S. Then:
A. S = (1/4)ft2
B. S = (1/2)ft2
C. S = (1/6)ft2
D. S = (1/72)ft2
Solution: Phase 1: S = (1/2)ft12, v = ft1, so v = √(2Sf). Phase 2 distance: d2 = vt = t√(2Sf). Phase 3: d3 = v2/(2 × f/2) = v2/f = 2S. Total: S + t√(2Sf) + 2S = 15S → t√(2Sf) = 12S → t2(2Sf) = 144S2 → S = ft2/72.
Question 9 [JEE Main 2005]
The relation between time t and distance x is t = αx2 + βx, where α and β are constants. The acceleration is:
A. 2αv2
B. −2αv3
C. 2βv3
D. −2αβv2
Solution: dt/dx = 2αx + β = 1/v. Differentiating again with respect to x: −(1/v2)(dv/dx) = 2α. Thus a = v(dv/dx) = −2αv3.
Question 10 [JEE Main 2005]
A parachutist after bailing out falls 50 m without friction. When the parachute opens, it decelerates at 2 m/s2. He reaches the ground with speed 3 m/s. At what height did he bail out? (g = 9.8 m/s2)
A. 111 m
B. 293 m
C. 182 m
D. 91 m
Solution: After 50 m free fall: v12 = 2 × 9.8 × 50 = 980, v1 ≈ 31.3 m/s. With deceleration of 2 m/s2: 32 = 31.32 − 2(2)h2 → h2 = (980 − 9)/4 ≈ 242.75 m. Total height = 50 + 242.75 ≈ 293 m.
Question 11 [JEE Main 2006]
A particle at x = 0 at t = 0 starts moving with velocity v = α√x where α is a constant. The displacement varies with time as:
A. t1/2
B. t3
C. t2
D. t
Solution: dx/dt = α√x → dx/√x = α dt → integrating: 2√x = αt → x = α2t2/4 ∝ t2.
Question 12 [JEE Main 2007]
The velocity of a particle is v = v0 + gt + ft2 where v0, g, f are constants. If position x = 0 at t = 0, the displacement after unit time (t = 1) is:
A. v0 + (g/2) + (f/3)
B. v0 + g + f
C. v0 + (g/2) + f
D. v0 + 2g + 3f
Solution: x = ∫01 v dt = v0(1) + g(1)2/2 + f(1)3/3 = v0 + g/2 + f/3.
Question 13 [JEE Main 2008]
A body starts from rest at x = 0 at t = 0, moving in the +x direction with constant acceleration. At the same instant another body passes through x = 0 in the +x direction with constant speed. The positions are x1(t) and x2(t). The graph of (x1 − x2) versus time t is:
A. Always positive and increasing
B. First becomes negative, reaches a minimum, then becomes positive
C. Always negative
D. First positive, then negative
Solution: x1 = (1/2)at2, x2 = vt. So x1 − x2 = (1/2)at2 − vt. At t = 0: value = 0. For small t, the −vt term dominates (goes negative). At large t, the (1/2)at2 term dominates (goes positive). Hence the difference first goes negative, reaches a minimum, then becomes positive.
Question 14 [JEE Main 2009]
A rubber ball freely falls from height h = 4.9 m onto a horizontal elastic plate. The collision is totally elastic, and its duration is negligible. Which option correctly represents the v-t and y-t graphs?
A. v is a sawtooth (always positive); y shows parabolas of decreasing height
B. v alternates between +v1 and −v1 linearly; y shows parabolic bounces decreasing in height
C. v goes from 0 to −v1 repeatedly (all negative); y decreases in steps
D. v alternates between +v1 and −v1 (zigzag crossing zero); y shows repeated identical parabolic arcs reaching same height h
Solution: Since the collision is totally elastic, no energy is lost and the ball bounces back to the same height each time. The velocity-time graph shows linear segments alternating between +v1 and −v1 (crossing zero at each bounce), and y-t shows identical parabolic arcs all reaching height h. Answer D.
Question 15 [JEE Main 2011]
An object moving with speed 6.25 m/s is decelerated at a rate dv/dt = −2.5√v. The time taken by the object to come to rest is:
A. 1 s
B. 2 s
C. 4 s
D. 8 s
Solution: dv/(−2.5√v) = dt. Integrating: ∫ v−1/2 dv = −2.5∫ dt → 2√v = −2.5t + C. At t = 0: 2√6.25 = 2 × 2.5 = 5 = C. At v = 0: 0 = −2.5t + 5 → t = 2 s.
Question 16 [JEE Main 2014]
From a tower of height H, a particle is thrown vertically upwards with speed u. The time taken by the particle to hit the ground is n times the time taken by it to reach the highest point. The relation between H, u and n is:
A. 2gH = nu2(n − 2)
B. gH = (n − 2)u2
C. 2gH = n2u2
D. gH = (n − 2)2u2
Solution: Time to highest point: t1 = u/g. Total time to hit ground: T = nt1 = nu/g. Taking downward as positive: H = −u(T) + (1/2)g(T)2 = −u(nu/g) + (1/2)g(nu/g)2 = −nu2/g + n2u2/(2g) = nu2(n − 2)/(2g). Therefore 2gH = nu2(n − 2).
Question 17 [JEE Main 2015]
Two stones are thrown up simultaneously from the edge of a cliff 240 m high with initial speeds 10 m/s and 40 m/s respectively. Which graph best represents the time variation of relative position of the second stone with respect to the first? (g = 10 m/s2)
A. (y2 − y1) increases linearly to 240 m at t = 8 s, then drops sharply to 0 at t = 12 s
B. (y2 − y1) increases linearly from 0 until t = 12 s, reaching 240 m
C. (y2 − y1) increases linearly to 240 m at t ≈ 8 s, then decreases linearly, reaching 0 at t = 12 s (triangular shape)
D. (y2 − y1) curves upward to 240 m, then decreases to 0
Solution: Relative position y2 − y1 = (40 − 10)t = 30t while both stones are in the air (linear). Stone 1 hits ground: 240 = −10t + 5t2 → t = 8 s. Stone 2 hits ground: 240 = −40t + 5t2 → t = 12 s. For 0 < t < 8 s: y2 − y1 = 30t (linear, reaches 240 at t = 8). For 8 < t < 12 s: stone 1 is on ground (y1 = 0), so the difference decreases. Overall shape is triangular — answer C.
Question 18 [JEE Main 2017]
A body is thrown vertically upwards. Which v-t graph correctly represents the motion?
A. v starts high and positive, decreases to 0 (upward journey only shown, no continuation)
B. v starts positive, decreases linearly through zero to negative values (complete journey, single straight line with negative slope)
C. v starts positive, goes to 0, then increases back to positive (V-shape, always positive)
D. v is constant positive for first half, then constant negative (step function)
Solution: v = u − gt is a continuous straight line with constant negative slope (due to constant gravitational deceleration). The velocity starts positive (upward), passes through zero at the highest point, then becomes negative (downward). This is represented by a single straight line crossing the time axis — answer B.
Question 19 [JEE Main 2018]
All the graphs below are intended to represent the same motion. One of them does it incorrectly. Pick it up:
A. Velocity vs Position: straight line with negative slope
B. Distance vs Time: straight line (implying constant speed throughout)
C. Position vs Time: parabola starting at the origin
D. Velocity vs Time: straight line starting positive and going to negative
Solution: For a ball thrown upward under constant deceleration: position-time is parabolic (C is correct), velocity-time is linear crossing zero (D is correct), velocity-position is linear (A is correct). However, distance-time cannot be a straight line — that would imply constant speed. In reality, the ball decelerates going up then accelerates coming down. The distance always increases but at a variable rate, making a curved distance-time graph. A straight line (B) is incorrect.
