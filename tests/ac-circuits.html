AC Circuits — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
The RMS value of an alternating voltage given by V = 200 sin(314t) is:
A. 200 V
B. 200√2 V
C. 100√2 V
D. 100 V
Solution: V0 = 200 V. Vrms = V0/√2 = 200/√2 = 100√2 V ≈ 141.4 V.
Question 2
In a pure capacitive AC circuit, the current:
A. Leads the voltage by 90°
B. Lags the voltage by 90°
C. Is in phase with the voltage
D. Leads the voltage by 45°
Solution: In a pure capacitive circuit, the current leads the voltage by π/2 (90°). This is because the capacitor must charge before a potential difference appears across it.
Question 3
An LCR series circuit has L = 0.1 H, C = 10 μF, and R = 50 Ω. The resonant frequency is:
A. 50/π Hz
B. 50√10/π Hz
C. 100/π Hz
D. 500/π Hz
Solution: f0 = 1/(2π√LC) = 1/(2π√(0.1 × 10 × 10−6)) = 1/(2π√(10−6)) = 1/(2π × 10−3) = 1000/(2π) = 500/π Hz. Wait, let me recalculate: √(0.1 × 10−5) = √(10−6) = 10−3. So f0 = 1/(2π × 10−3) = 500/π Hz. The answer is D.
Question 4
The impedance of a series LCR circuit at resonance is:
A. √(R² + (XL − XC)²)
B. Zero
C. XL + XC
D. R
Solution: At resonance, XL = XC, so the impedance Z = √(R² + (XL − XC)²) = √(R² + 0) = R. The impedance is minimum and equals the resistance.
Question 5
A transformer has 500 primary turns and 5000 secondary turns. If the primary voltage is 220 V, the secondary voltage is:
A. 2200 V
B. 22 V
C. 440 V
D. 11000 V
Solution: Vs/Vp = Ns/Np. So Vs = Vp × Ns/Np = 220 × 5000/500 = 220 × 10 = 2200 V.
Question 6
The power factor of a series RLC circuit at resonance is:
A. 0
B. 1
C. 0.5
D. √2
Solution: Power factor cosφ = R/Z. At resonance Z = R, so cosφ = R/R = 1. The voltage and current are in phase, and maximum power is transferred.
Question 7
The reactance of a capacitor of 50 μF at 50 Hz is:
A. 100/π Ω
B. 200π Ω
C. 200/π Ω
D. 100π Ω
Solution: XC = 1/(2πfC) = 1/(2π × 50 × 50 × 10−6) = 1/(5000π × 10−6) = 106/(5000π) = 200/π Ω ≈ 63.7 Ω.
Question 8
In a pure inductive AC circuit, the average power dissipated over a complete cycle is:
A. Vrms × Irms
B. Vrms² / XL
C. Irms² × XL
D. Zero
Solution: In a pure inductor, the phase difference between V and I is 90°. Average power P = VrmsIrmscosφ = VrmsIrmscos90° = 0. Energy is alternately stored in and released from the magnetic field.
Question 9
The impedance of a series circuit with R = 30 Ω, XL = 60 Ω, and XC = 20 Ω is:
A. 50 Ω
B. 110 Ω
C. 70 Ω
D. 40 Ω
Solution: Z = √(R² + (XL − XC)²) = √(30² + (60 − 20)²) = √(900 + 1600) = √2500 = 50 Ω.
Question 10
A step-down transformer reduces 2200 V to 220 V. If the secondary coil has 50 turns, the number of turns in the primary coil is:
A. 5
B. 500
C. 5000
D. 250
Solution: Np/Ns = Vp/Vs. So Np = Ns × Vp/Vs = 50 × 2200/220 = 50 × 10 = 500 turns.
Question 11
The quality factor (Q-factor) of a series LCR circuit is given by:
A. R/(ω0L)
B. ω0R/L
C. ω0L/R
D. Rω0C
Solution: The Q-factor = ω0L/R = 1/(Rω0C) = (1/R)√(L/C). It measures the sharpness of resonance. Higher Q means sharper resonance peak.
Question 12
The frequency of AC mains in India is:
A. 50 Hz
B. 60 Hz
C. 100 Hz
D. 220 Hz
Solution: The AC mains frequency in India is 50 Hz (angular frequency ω = 2π × 50 = 100π rad/s).
Question 13
The inductive reactance of a 2 H inductor at 50 Hz is:
A. 100 Ω
B. 50π Ω
C. 100π Ω
D. 200π Ω
Solution: XL = 2πfL = 2π × 50 × 2 = 200π Ω ≈ 628 Ω.
Question 14
An ideal transformer is based on the principle of:
A. Self-induction
B. Mutual induction
C. Eddy currents
D. Electromagnetic waves
Solution: A transformer works on the principle of mutual induction. An alternating current in the primary coil creates a changing magnetic flux that induces an EMF in the secondary coil.
Question 15
In an AC circuit, the wattless component of current is:
A. I cosφ
B. I tanφ
C. I sinφ
D. I/cosφ
Solution: The wattless (reactive) component of current is I sinφ, which contributes zero average power. The power (wattful) component is I cosφ. Only the component in phase with voltage contributes to real power dissipation.
