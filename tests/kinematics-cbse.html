Kinematics — CBSE Level 0 Practice

CBSE Pattern • 27 Questions • 3 Hours • 70 Marks
Maximum Marks: 70
Question 1 [VSA - 1 Mark]
Explain how can a body be simultaneously at rest and in motion?
Solution: For a passenger in a moving train, a body in the train is at rest while for a person standing outside the train the same body is in motion.
Question 2 [VSA - 1 Mark]
The instantaneous speed is always equal to the magnitude of instantaneous velocity. Why?
Solution: For a small interval of time the magnitude of displacement is equal to length of the path.
Question 3 [VSA - 1 Mark]
Under what condition is the average velocity equal to the instantaneous velocity?
Solution: Both are equal when the body is moving with uniform velocity.
Question 4 [VSA - 1 Mark]
Can a body have zero velocity and finite acceleration?
Solution: A body projected vertically upwards has zero velocity at the highest point but acceleration due to gravity is present.
Question 5 [VSA - 1 Mark]
Can the direction of the velocity of a body change when its acceleration is constant?
Solution: Yes when the body is thrown upwards.
Question 6 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
A particle travels half the distance with a speed v0. The remaining part of the distance was covered with speed v1 for half the time, and with speed v2 for the other half of the time. Find the average speed of the particle averaged over the whole time of motion.
Solution: Let S = Total distance. t1 = S/(2v0). For remaining S/2: S/2 = (v1 + v2)t, so 2t = S/(v1 + v2). Average speed = S/(t1 + 2t) = 2v0(v1 + v2)/(v1 + v2 + 2v0).
Question 7 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
A man starts from his home at 8.00 am to his office. He walks with a speed of 2 ms−1 on a road upto his office 3.0 km away from his home. He stays in the office upto 4.00 pm and returns to his home by bus which moves non-stop with a speed of 10 ms−1. Calculate (i) the time taken by man to reach his office (ii) time taken by man to reach his home. Also plot distance-time graph of his motion.
Solution: (i) t = 3000/2 = 1500 s = 25 min. He reaches at 8:25 am. (ii) Return: t = 3000/10 = 300 s = 5 min. He reaches home at 4:05 pm.
Question 8 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
A particle is moving along x-axis. The position of the particle at any instant is given by x = a + bt2 where, a = 6 m and b = 3.5 ms−2, t is measured in second. Find (i) the velocity of the particle at t = 0 s and t = 3 s. (ii) the average velocity between t = 3 s and t = 6 s.
Solution: x = 6 + 3.5t2. v = dx/dt = 7t. (i) At t = 0: v = 0 ms−1. At t = 3: v = 21.0 ms−1. (ii) Average velocity = [x(6) − x(3)]/(6 − 3) = [(6 + 126) − (6 + 31.5)]/3 = 94.5/3 = 31.5 ms−1.
Question 9 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
Chandigarh and Ambala are connected with a regular bus service. A bus leaving in either direction every T minutes. A boy cycling with a speed of 10 km h−1 from Chandigarh to Ambala notices that a bus crosses him after every 15 minutes towards Ambala and after every 5 minutes towards Chandigarh. Calculate the time T and the speed of the buses. Assume that speed of buses is constant.
Solution: Let speed of buses = v km/h. Relative speed towards Ambala = (v − 10). Relative speed towards Chandigarh = (v + 10). Distance between buses = vT. From 15 min: vT/(v − 10) = 15/60 = 1/4. So 4vT = v − 10 ... (1). From 5 min: vT/(v + 10) = 5/60 = 1/12. So 12vT = v + 10 ... (2). Dividing: (v − 10)/(v + 10) = 1/3. So v = 20 km/h. T = 10/(80) h = 7.5 minutes.
Question 10 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
A ball is thrown upwards with a velocity of 29.4 ms−1 in the absence of air resistance. (i) Mention the direction of the acceleration during the upward motion of the ball. (ii) What are the velocity and acceleration of the ball at the highest point? (iii) How much high the ball goes up? (iv) Find the time taken by the ball to reach the ground. Take g = 9.8 ms−2.
Solution: (i) Downward (opposite to motion). (ii) v = 0, a = 9.8 ms−2 downward. (iii) h = u2/(2g) = (29.4)2/(2 × 9.8) = 44.1 m. (iv) t up = 29.4/9.8 = 3 s. Total time = 6 s.
Question 11 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
Drops of water from the roof of a house 6 m high fall at regular intervals. The first drop reaches the ground at an instant of time when third drop leaves the roof. Find the height of the second drop at that instant.
Solution: Let t = time between drops. First drop: 6 = (1/2)g(2t)2 = 2gt2. So gt2 = 3 ... (1). Second drop has fallen for time t: h2 = (1/2)gt2 = 3/2 = 1.5 m from roof. Height from ground = 6 − 1.5 = 4.5 m.
Question 12 [SA-I - 2 Marks]
Find the ratio of the distances travelled by a freely falling body in first, second and third second of its fall.
Solution: Using Snth = u + (g/2)(2n − 1). h1 = 4.9 m, h2 = 3 × 4.9 m, h3 = 5 × 4.9 m. Ratio h1 : h2 : h3 = 1 : 3 : 5.
Question 13 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Acceleration of a particle is given by a = 3t2 + 2t + 1, where t is time. Find (i) its velocity at time t assuming velocity to be 10 m/s at t = 0. (ii) its position at time t assuming that the particle is at origin at t = 0. (iii) What is the average speed of the particle in the duration t = 0 to t = 1 s? (iv) What is the average acceleration of the particle in the duration t = 0 to t = 1 s?
Solution: (i) v = t3 + t2 + t + 10. (ii) x = t4/4 + t3/3 + t2/2 + 10t. (iii) vavg = x(1)/1 = (1/4 + 1/3 + 1/2 + 10) = 133/12 m/s. (iv) aavg = [v(1) − v(0)]/1 = (13 − 10)/1 = 3 m/s2.
Question 14 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Acceleration of a particle is given by a = 2x, where x is the position of the particle. Find (i) Its velocity at position x. Given that v = 10 m/s when x = 0. (ii) Minimum speed that the particle can attain.
Solution: (i) v dv/dx = 2x. Integrating: v2 = 2x2 + 100. So v = √(2x2 + 100). (ii) vmin = 10 m/s (when x = 0).
Question 15 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Acceleration of a particle is given by a = 1/v, where v is the velocity. Find (i) Velocity of particle at time t, given that v = 10 m/s at t = 0. (ii) Velocity of particle when its position is x, given that speed is 3 m/s at x = 0.
Solution: (i) v dv = dt. v2/2 = t + 50. v = √(2t + 100). (ii) v2 dv = dx. v3/3 = x + 9. v = (3x + 27)1/3.
Question 16 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Acceleration of a particle is increasing linearly with time. Its acceleration is 10 m/s2 at t = 0 and 20 m/s2 at t = 5 s. Also, its speed is 5 m/s at t = 0 and its position is x = −100 m at t = 0. Find at time t its acceleration, velocity and position.
Solution: a = mt + c. At t = 0: c = 10. m = (20 − 10)/5 = 2. So a = 2t + 10. v = t2 + 10t + 5. x = t3/3 + 5t2 + 5t − 100.
Question 17 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Draw the acceleration-time graphs for the given velocity-time graphs of a particle moving along x-axis. (4 graphs: (i) constant positive v, (ii) v increases then decreases, (iii) v oscillates, (iv) v increases then levels off)
Solution: (i) a = 0 (horizontal line at zero). (ii) First positive constant a, then negative constant a. (iii) Alternating positive and negative a. (iv) Positive decreasing a approaching zero.
Question 18 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
The position-time (x-t) graphs for two children A and B returning from their school O to their homes P and Q respectively are shown. Choose the correct entries: (i) (A) lives closer to the school than (B). (ii) (A) starts from the school earlier than (B). (iii) (B) walks faster than (A). (iv) A and B reach home at the same time. (v) (B) overtakes (A) on the road once.
Solution: (i) A lives closer (OP < OQ). (ii) A starts earlier. (iii) B walks faster (steeper slope). (iv) Same time. (v) B overtakes A once.
Question 19 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Two balls of different masses are thrown vertically upward with same initial velocity. Height attained by them are h1 and h2 respectively. What is h1/h2?
Solution: h1/h2 = 1/1 = 1, because the height attained by the projectile does not depend on the masses.
Question 20 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
The velocity time relation of a particle is given by v = (3t2 − 2t − 1) m/s. Calculate the position and acceleration of the particle when velocity of the particle is zero. Given the initial position of the particle is 5 m.
Solution: v = 3t2 − 2t − 1 = 0 → 3t2 − 3t + t − 1 = 0 → t = 1 s (taking positive value). x = t3 − t2 − t + 5. At t = 1: x = 1 − 1 − 1 + 5 = 4 m. a = 6t − 2. At t = 1: a = 4 m/s2.
Question 21 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
A ball is thrown vertically upwards with a velocity of 20 m/s from the top of a building of height 40 m. (i) How high the ball reaches? (ii) How long will it take to reach the ground? (iii) What is the speed at which it strikes the ground?
Solution: (i) S = u2/(2g) = 400/20 = 20 m from top of tower. (ii) −40 = 20t − 5t2 → t2 − 4t − 8 = 0 → t = 2 + 2√3 ≈ 5.46 s. (iii) V2 = 400 + 800 = 1200 → V = 20√3 ≈ 34.6 m/s.
Question 22 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
The displacement of a particle moving along x-axis is given by x = 18t + 5t2. Calculate (i) The instantaneous velocity at t = 2 s. (ii) Average velocity between t = 2 s and t = 3 s. (iii) Instantaneous acceleration.
Solution: v = dx/dt = 18 + 10t. (i) v(2) = 18 + 20 = 38 m/s. (ii) Avg vel = [x(3) − x(2)]/(3 − 2) = [(54 + 45) − (36 + 20)]/1 = 43 m/s. (iii) a = dv/dt = 10 m/s2.
Question 23 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Draw velocity − time graph of uniformly accelerated motion in one dimension. From the velocity − time graph of uniform accelerated motion, deduce the equations of motion in distance and time.
Solution: The v-t graph is a straight line with slope = a. Area under graph = displacement. From graph: v = u + at. s = area = (1/2)(u + v)t = ut + (1/2)at2. Also s = (v2 − u2)/(2a).
Question 24 [SA-II - 3 Marks]
Suppose you are a judge and the case under your consideration is: "Whether a car driver was exceeding a speed limit of 40 km/h before he made an emergency stop, the length of the skid marks on the road was 3 m? You may assume the maximum deceleration to be 10 m/s2."
Solution: v2 = u2 − 2as. 0 = u2 − 2(10)(3). u2 = 60. u = √60 ≈ 7.75 m/s = 27.9 km/h. Speed limit was not exceeded.
Question 25 [LA - 5 Marks]
A drunkard walking in a narrow lane takes 5 steps forward and 3 steps backward, followed again by 5 steps forward and 3 steps backward and so on. Each step is 1 m long and requires 1 s. Plot the x-t graph of his motion. Determine graphically and otherwise, how long the drunkard takes to fall in a pit 13 m away from the start.
Solution: Pattern: 5 forward (5m), 3 backward (3m) = net 2m per 8 steps (8s). Average velocity = 2m/8s = 0.25 m/s. After 4 cycles (32s): 8m. Then 5 more steps forward: 13m at t = 37s. Time = 37 s.
Question 26 [LA - 5 Marks]
Draw the v-t graphs for the given a-t graphs for a particle moving in one dimension. (6 sub-parts with different a-t graph shapes and initial velocity conditions)
Solution: (i) Constant a, V = 10 m/s at t = 0: straight line from 10. (ii) Positive decreasing a, V = 10 at t = 0: curve upward from 10 with decreasing slope. (iii) Negative constant a, V = −10 at t = 0: straight line going more negative. (iv) Negative constant a, V = 10 at t = 0: straight line going down from 10. (v) Positive increasing a, V = 10 at t = 0: curve upward with increasing slope. (vi) a increasing from zero, V = 10 at t = 0: curve with slope = 0 at t = 0 (since a = 0 there).
Question 27 [LA - 5 Marks]
A man is standing on top of a building 100 m high. He throws two balls vertically, one at t = 0 and other after a time interval (less than 2 seconds). The latter ball is thrown at a velocity of half of the first. The vertical gap between first and second ball is +15 m at t = 2 s. The gap is found to remain constant. Calculate the velocity with which the balls were thrown and the exact time interval between their throw.
Solution: Let v1 = 2v, v2 = v. Since gap remains constant, relative velocity = 0 at t = 2s. v1 − g(2) = v2 − g(2 − t2). 2v − 2g = v − g(2 − t2). v = gt2. Also: gap = (2v)(2) − (1/2)g(4) − [v(2 − t2) − (1/2)g(2 − t2)2] = 15. Solving: v1 = 20 m/s, v2 = 10 m/s, t2 = 1 s.
