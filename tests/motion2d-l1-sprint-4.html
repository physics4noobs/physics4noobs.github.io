Motion in 2D Level 1 — Sprint Test 4

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A glass marble projected horizontally from the top of a table falls at a distance x from the edge of the table. If h is the height of the table, then the velocity of projection is:
A. h√(g/2x)
B. x√(g/2h)
C. gxh
D. gx + h
Solution: Time to fall height h: h = (1/2)gt2 ⇒ t = √(2h/g). Horizontal distance: x = u·t = u√(2h/g). Solving for u: u = x/√(2h/g) = x√(g/2h).
Question 2
A bomber plane moves horizontally with a speed of 500 ms−1 and a bomb released from it, strikes the ground in 10 s. Angle with horizontal at which it strikes the ground will be: (g = 10 ms−2)
A. tan−1(1/5)
B. 90°
C. tan−1(1)
D. tan−1(5)
Solution: Horizontal velocity: vx = 500 ms−1 (constant). Vertical velocity after 10 s: vy = gt = 10 × 10 = 100 ms−1. tanθ = vy/vx = 100/500 = 1/5. So θ = tan−1(1/5).
Question 3
A body is projected horizontally from a very high tower with speed 20 ms−1. The approximate displacement of the body after 5 s is:
A. 80 m
B. 120 m
C. 160 m
D. 320 m
Solution: Horizontal displacement: x = 20 × 5 = 100 m. Vertical displacement: y = (1/2) × 10 × 25 = 125 m. Net displacement = √(1002 + 1252) = √(10000 + 15625) = √25625 ≈ 160 m.
Question 4
A tennis ball rolls off the top of a stair case way with a horizontal velocity u ms−1. If the steps are b metre wide and h metre high, the ball will hit the edge of the nth step, if:
A. n = 2hu/gb2
B. n = 2hu2/gb2
C. n = 2hu2/gb
D. n = hu/gb2
Solution: The nth step edge is at horizontal distance nb and vertical distance nh. For projectile: nh = (1/2)g(nb/u)2 = gn2b2/(2u2). Solving: n = 2hu2/(gb2).
Question 5
From the top of a tower of height 40 m, a ball is projected upward with a speed of 20 m/s at an angle of elevation of 30°. The ratio of the total time taken by the ball to hit the ground to the time taken to come back to the same elevation is: (g = 10 m/s2)
A. 2 : 1
B. 3 : 1
C. 3 : 2
D. 1.5 : 1
Solution: Vertical component: uy = 20 sin30° = 10 m/s. Time to return to same elevation: t1 = 2uy/g = 2 × 10/10 = 2 s. After returning to launch level, the ball falls 40 m with initial downward velocity 10 m/s: 40 = 10t + 5t2 ⇒ t = 2 s. Total time = 2 + 2 = 4 s. Ratio = 4:2 = 2:1.
Question 6
In previous question, the horizontal displacement (from the foot of the tower) of the ball is approximately equal to:
A. 50 m
B. 60 m
C. 70 m
D. 80 m
Solution: Horizontal component: ux = 20 cos30° = 20 × (√3/2) = 10√3 m/s ≈ 17.3 m/s. Total time = 4 s (from Q5). Horizontal displacement = ux × t = 17.3 × 4 ≈ 69.3 m ≈ 70 m.
Question 7
A cannon on a level plain is aimed at an angle α above the horizontal and a shell is fired with a muzzle velocity v0 towards a vertical cliff a distance R away. Then the height from the bottom at which the shell strikes the side walls of the cliff is:
A. R sinα − gR2/(2v02sin2α)
B. R cosα − gR2/(2v02cos2α)
C. R tanα − gR2/(2v02cos2α)
D. R tanα − gR2/(2v02sin2α)
Solution: Time to reach cliff: t = R/(v0cosα). Height at that time: y = v0sinα·t − (1/2)gt2 = v0sinα·R/(v0cosα) − (1/2)g·R2/(v02cos2α) = R tanα − gR2/(2v02cos2α).
Question 8
A body is projected horizontally from a point above the ground. The motion of the body is described by the equations x = 2t and y = 5t2, where x and y are the horizontal and vertical displacements (in m) respectively at time t. What is the magnitude of the velocity of the body 0.2 second after it is projected?
A. √2 ms−1
B. 2√2 ms−1
C. 3.5 ms−1
D. 5 ms−1
Solution: vx = dx/dt = 2 m/s (constant). vy = dy/dt = 10t. At t = 0.2 s: vy = 10 × 0.2 = 2 m/s. Magnitude = √(vx2 + vy2) = √(4 + 4) = √8 = 2√2 ms−1.
Question 9
A particle is moving in a circle of radius r centered at O with constant speed u. What is the change in velocity in moving from A to B (∠AOB = 40°)?
A. 2u sin20°
B. 4u sin40°
C. 2u sin40°
D. u sin40°
Solution: The magnitude of change in velocity for uniform circular motion when the angle subtended at centre is θ is given by |Δv| = 2u sin(θ/2). Here θ = 40°, so |Δv| = 2u sin(20°).
Question 10
A point in the rim of a wheel 4 m in diameter has linear velocity of 16 ms−1. The angular velocity of wheel is:
A. 4 rad s−1
B. 8 rad s−1
C. 12 rad s−1
D. 20 rad s−1
Solution: Diameter = 4 m, so radius r = 2 m. Linear velocity v = rω ⇒ ω = v/r = 16/2 = 8 rad s−1.
