Motion in 2D Level 1 — Sprint Test 5

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
The angular displacement of a particle is given by θ = t4 + t3 + t2 + 1 where t is time in seconds. Its angular velocity after 2 sec is:
A. 20 rad s−1
B. 48 rad s−1
C. 52 rad s−1
D. 22 rad s−1
Solution: ω = dθ/dt = 4t3 + 3t2 + 2t. At t = 2 s: ω = 4(8) + 3(4) + 2(2) = 32 + 12 + 4 = 48 rad s−1.
Question 2
The speed of a motor increases uniformly from 600 rpm to 1200 rpm in 10 second. How many revolutions does it make in this period?
A. 150
B. 250
C. 300
D. 325
Solution: Average rpm = (600 + 1200)/2 = 900 rpm. In 10 s = 10/60 min, revolutions = 900 × (10/60) = 150.
Question 3
A point at the periphery of a disc rotating about a fixed axis moves so that its speed varies with time as v = 2t2 m/s. The tangential acceleration of the point at t = 1 s is:
A. 4 m/s2
B. 2√2 m/s
C. √2 m/s2
D. None of these
Solution: Tangential acceleration at = dv/dt = 4t. At t = 1 s: at = 4 m/s2.
Question 4
The angular frequency of a fan increases uniformly from 30 rpm to 60 rpm in π second. A dust particle is present at a distance of 20 cm from axis of rotation. The tangential acceleration of the particle is:
A. 0.2 m s−2
B. 0.4 m s−2
C. 0.6 m s−2
D. 2 m s−2
Solution: ω1 = 30 × 2π/60 = π rad/s, ω2 = 60 × 2π/60 = 2π rad/s. Angular acceleration α = (ω2 − ω1)/t = (2π − π)/π = 1 rad/s2. Tangential acceleration at = αr = 1 × 0.20 = 0.2 m s−2.
Question 5
A ball starts moving in a circular slot with angular acceleration α = 2 rad/s2 at t = 0. The angle between its velocity and acceleration varies with time as:
A. 90°
B. tan−1(2t2)
C. tan−1(t/2)
D. 45°
Solution: Starting from rest, ω = αt = 2t. Radial acceleration ar = ω2R = 4t2R; Tangential acceleration at = αR = 2R. The angle φ between velocity (tangential direction) and net acceleration: tanφ = ar/at = 4t2R / 2R = 2t2. So φ = tan−1(2t2).
Question 6
A particle moves in a circle with constant tangential acceleration starting from rest. At t = 1/2 s radial acceleration has a value 25% of tangential acceleration. The value of tangential acceleration will be correctly represented by:
A. at = R
B. at = R/2
C. at = 2R
D. None of these
Solution: Starting from rest, v = att. Radial acceleration ar = v2/R = at2t2/R. Given ar = 0.25 at at t = 1/2 s: at2(1/4)/R = at/4. Simplifying: at/4R = 1/4, so at = R.
Question 7
For a particle moving along a circular path the radial acceleration ar is proportional to t2 (square of time). If az is tangential acceleration which of the following is independent of time?
A. ar · az
B. az
C. ar / az
D. (ar)2 / az
Solution: ar = v2/R ∝ t2 implies v ∝ t, so dv/dt = constant. Therefore az = dv/dt is independent of time.
Question 8
A particle starts moving with a constant angular acceleration α in a circular path. The time at which the magnitudes of tangential and radial acceleration are equal is:
A. 1/α
B. √α
C. 1/√α
D. α
Solution: Starting from rest, ω = αt. Radial acceleration ar = ω2R = α2t2R. Tangential acceleration at = αR. Setting ar = at: α2t2R = αR ⇒ αt2 = 1 ⇒ t = 1/√α.
Question 9
A particle starts traveling on a circle with constant tangential acceleration. The angle between velocity vector and acceleration vector, at the moment when particle completes half the circular track, is:
A. tan−1(2π)
B. tan−1(π)
C. tan−1(3π)
D. zero
Solution: Starting from rest with constant at, after half revolution: distance = πR, so v2 = 2at(πR). Radial acceleration ar = v2/R = 2πat. The angle φ between velocity and net acceleration: tanφ = ar/at = 2π. So φ = tan−1(2π).
Question 10
A heavy particle is projected from a point on the horizontal at an angle 60° with the horizontal, with a speed of 10 m/s. Then the radius of curvature of its path at the instant of crossing the same horizontal is: (g = 10 m/s2)
A. Infinite
B. 10 m
C. 11.54 m
D. 20 m
Solution: At the point of crossing the same horizontal, the speed equals the launch speed = 10 m/s but direction is 60° below horizontal (by symmetry). The centripetal acceleration is the component of g perpendicular to velocity = g·cos60° = 10 × 0.5 = 5 m/s2. Radius R = v2/ac = 100/5 = 20 m.
