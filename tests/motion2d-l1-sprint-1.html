Motion in 2D Level 1 — Sprint Test 1

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A particle P at the origin starts with velocity v⃗ = (2î − 4ĵ) ms−1 with constant acceleration (3î − 5ĵ) ms−2. After traveling for 2 s, its distance from the origin is:
A. 10 m
B. 20.6 m
C. 9.8 m
D. 11.7 m
Solution: x = uxt + (1/2)axt2 = 2(2) + (1/2)(3)(4) = 4 + 6 = 10 m. y = uyt + (1/2)ayt2 = −4(2) + (1/2)(−5)(4) = −8 − 10 = −18 m. Distance = √(102 + 182) = √(100 + 324) = √424 ≈ 20.6 m.
Question 2
A particle leaves the origin with an initial velocity v⃗ = (3.00î) ms−1 and a constant acceleration a⃗ = (−1.00î − 0.50ĵ) ms−2. When the particle reaches its maximum x-coordinate, what is its y-component of velocity?
A. −2.0 ms−1
B. −1.0 ms−1
C. −1.5 ms−1
D. −1.0 ms−1
Solution: Maximum x-coordinate occurs when vx = 0. vx = ux + axt ⇒ 0 = 3.00 − 1.00t ⇒ t = 3 s. vy = uy + ayt = 0 + (−0.50)(3) = −1.5 ms−1.
Question 3
Range of a projectile with time of flight 10 s and maximum height 100 m is: (g = 10 m/s2)
A. 200 m
B. 125 m
C. 100 m
D. Data incorrect
Solution: Time of flight T = 2u sinθ/g = 10 s ⇒ u sinθ = 50 m/s. Maximum height H = u2sin2θ/(2g) = 502/(20) = 125 m ≠ 100 m. The given data is inconsistent, so the data is incorrect.
Question 4
At what angle with the horizontal should a ball be thrown so that the range R is related to the time of flight as R = 5T2? (Take g = 10 ms−2)
A. 30°
B. 45°
C. 60°
D. 90°
Solution: R = u2sin2θ/g and T = 2u sinθ/g. R = 5T2 ⇒ u2sin2θ/g = 5 × 4u2sin2θ/g2. ⇒ 2sinθcosθ = 20sin2θ/g = 20sin2θ/10 = 2sin2θ. ⇒ cosθ = sinθ ⇒ tanθ = 1 ⇒ θ = 45°.
Question 5
A shot is fired from a point at a distance of 200 m from the foot of a tower 100 m high so that it just passes over it horizontally. The direction of shot with horizontal is:
A. 75°
B. 60°
C. 30°
D. 45°
Solution: “Just passes over horizontally” means the top of the tower is at the maximum height, so R/2 = 200 m ⇒ R = 400 m. H = 100 m. tanθ = 4H/R = 4 × 100/400 = 1 ⇒ θ = 45°.
Question 6
A projectile thrown at an angle of 30° with the horizontal has a range R1 and attains a maximum height h1. Another projectile thrown, with the same velocity, at an angle 30° with the vertical, has a range R2 and attains a maximum height h2. The relation between R1 and R2 is:
A. R1 = R2/2
B. R1 = R2
C. R1 = 2R2
D. R1 = 4R2
Solution: 30° with the vertical = 60° with the horizontal. R = u2sin2θ/g. R1 = u2sin60°/g and R2 = u2sin120°/g. Since sin60° = sin120°, R1 = R2.
Question 7
In the previous question, what is the relation between h1 and h2?
A. h1 = h2/4
B. h1 = h2/3
C. h1 = h2/2
D. h1 = h2
Solution: H = u2sin2θ/(2g). h1 = u2sin230°/(2g) = u2(1/4)/(2g). h2 = u2sin260°/(2g) = u2(3/4)/(2g). h1/h2 = (1/4)/(3/4) = 1/3 ⇒ h1 = h2/3.
Question 8
When a projectile is at the highest point of its trajectory, the directions of its velocity and acceleration are:
A. parallel to each other
B. anti-parallel to each other
C. inclined to each other at an angle of 45°
D. perpendicular to each other
Solution: At the highest point, the vertical component of velocity is zero; only the horizontal component remains. Acceleration due to gravity acts vertically downward. Since velocity is horizontal and acceleration is vertical, they are perpendicular to each other.
Question 9
Which of the following remains constant during the motion of a projectile fired from a point?
A. kinetic energy
B. momentum
C. vertical component of velocity
D. horizontal component of velocity
Solution: In projectile motion, gravity acts only in the vertical direction, so there is no horizontal force. The horizontal component of velocity (u cosθ) therefore remains constant throughout the motion.
Question 10
Four projectiles are projected with the same speed at angles 20°, 35°, 60° and 75° with the horizontal. The range will be the maximum for the projectile whose angle of projection is:
A. 20°
B. 35°
C. 60°
D. 75°
Solution: Range R = u2sin2θ/g is maximum when 2θ = 90°, i.e., θ = 45°. Among the given angles, 35° is closest to 45°, and complementary pairs give equal ranges: sin(2×20°) = sin40°, sin(2×35°) = sin70°, sin(2×60°) = sin120° = sin60°, sin(2×75°) = sin150° = sin30°. The largest value is sin70°, so θ = 35° gives maximum range.
