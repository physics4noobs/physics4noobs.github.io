Ray Optics — Daily Test Series

Grade XII • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
An object is placed 30 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 20 cm. The image formed is:
A. Virtual, erect, magnified
B. Real, inverted, magnified
C. Real, inverted, diminished
D. Virtual, erect, diminished
Solution: Using mirror formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f. With u = −30 cm, f = −20 cm: 1/v = 1/(−20) − 1/(−30) = −1/20 + 1/30 = −1/60. So v = −60 cm. Since |v| > |u|, the image is real, inverted, and magnified (m = −v/u = −60/30 = −2).
Question 2
The critical angle for total internal reflection for a medium of refractive index √2 is:
A. 30°
B. 60°
C. 45°
D. 90°
Solution: sin(C) = 1/n = 1/√2. Therefore C = sin−1(1/√2) = 45°.
Question 3
A convex lens of focal length 10 cm is placed in contact with a concave lens of focal length 15 cm. The focal length of the combination is:
A. 30 cm
B. −30 cm
C. 6 cm
D. 25 cm
Solution: 1/f = 1/f1 + 1/f2 = 1/10 + 1/(−15) = 1/10 − 1/15 = (3 − 2)/30 = 1/30. So f = 30 cm (converging).
Question 4
A ray of light passes from glass (n = 1.5) to water (n = 1.33). The ray will:
A. Bend towards the normal
B. Not bend at all
C. Undergo total internal reflection always
D. Bend away from the normal
Solution: When light passes from a denser medium (glass, n = 1.5) to a rarer medium (water, n = 1.33), it bends away from the normal (angle of refraction > angle of incidence). TIR occurs only if the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle.
Question 5
The minimum deviation produced by a prism of refracting angle 60° and refractive index √2 is:
A. 45°
B. 30°
C. 60°
D. 90°
Solution: n = sin((A + Dm)/2) / sin(A/2). So √2 = sin((60 + Dm)/2) / sin30° = sin((60 + Dm)/2) / 0.5. Therefore sin((60 + Dm)/2) = √2/2 = sin45°. So (60 + Dm)/2 = 45°, giving Dm = 30°.
Question 6
The power of a lens of focal length 25 cm is:
A. 0.25 D
B. 25 D
C. 4 D
D. 2.5 D
Solution: P = 1/f (in metres) = 1/0.25 = 4 D (dioptre).
Question 7
An object is placed 15 cm from a convex lens of focal length 10 cm. The image is formed at:
A. 30 cm on the other side
B. 30 cm on the same side
C. 6 cm on the other side
D. At infinity
Solution: Using lens formula: 1/v − 1/u = 1/f. With u = −15 cm, f = 10 cm: 1/v = 1/10 + 1/(−15) = 1/10 − 1/15. Wait: 1/v = 1/f + 1/u = 1/10 + 1/(−15) = 1/10 − 1/15 = (3 − 2)/30 = 1/30. So v = 30 cm (positive = other side of lens).
Question 8
In a compound microscope, the magnifying power for normal adjustment is given by (L = tube length, fo = objective focal length, fe = eyepiece focal length, D = least distance of distinct vision):
A. D/fo
B. fo/fe
C. D/fe
D. (L/fo) × (D/fe)
Solution: For a compound microscope in normal adjustment (final image at infinity): M = (L/fo) × (D/fe), where L is the tube length (distance between lenses minus their focal lengths).
Question 9
A convex mirror always forms an image that is:
A. Real and inverted
B. Virtual, erect, and diminished
C. Virtual, erect, and magnified
D. Real and magnified
Solution: A convex mirror always forms a virtual, erect, and diminished image for any real object position. The image is formed behind the mirror between the pole and focus.
Question 10
The magnifying power of an astronomical telescope in normal adjustment is 20. If the focal length of the eyepiece is 5 cm, the focal length of the objective is:
A. 100 cm
B. 4 cm
C. 25 cm
D. 200 cm
Solution: For an astronomical telescope in normal adjustment: M = fo/fe. So fo = M × fe = 20 × 5 = 100 cm.
Question 11
The refractive index of a medium with respect to vacuum is 1.5. The speed of light in this medium is:
A. 4.5 × 108 m/s
B. 1.5 × 108 m/s
C. 2 × 108 m/s
D. 3 × 108 m/s
Solution: n = c/v. So v = c/n = 3 × 108 / 1.5 = 2 × 108 m/s.
Question 12
A ray of light is incident on a glass slab at an angle of 60°. If the refractive index of glass is √3, the angle of refraction is:
A. 45°
B. 60°
C. 15°
D. 30°
Solution: By Snell's law: n1 sin i = n2 sin r. So 1 × sin60° = √3 × sin r. sin r = (√3/2)/√3 = 1/2. Therefore r = 30°.
Question 13
Two thin lenses of focal lengths +20 cm and −40 cm are kept in contact. The focal length of the combination is:
A. −20 cm
B. +40 cm
C. +20 cm
D. −40 cm
Solution: 1/f = 1/f1 + 1/f2 = 1/20 + 1/(−40) = 1/20 − 1/40 = (2 − 1)/40 = 1/40. So f = +40 cm (converging).
Question 14
A diamond has a refractive index of 2.42. Its critical angle for total internal reflection is approximately:
A. 24.4°
B. 45°
C. 30°
D. 48.8°
Solution: sin C = 1/n = 1/2.42 = 0.413. C = sin−1(0.413) ≈ 24.4°. The small critical angle of diamond is responsible for its brilliance.
Question 15
An object is placed at the centre of curvature of a concave mirror. The image formed is:
A. At the focus, diminished
B. At infinity, highly magnified
C. At the centre of curvature, same size, real, and inverted
D. Behind the mirror, virtual
Solution: When an object is placed at C (u = R = 2f), the image is also formed at C. The image is real, inverted, and the same size as the object (magnification m = −1).
