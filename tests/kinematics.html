Kinematics — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes

Question 1
A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly, reaching a speed of 144 km/h in 20 seconds. What is the total distance covered during this time?
A. 200 m
B. 400 m
C. 1440 m
D. 2880 m
Solution: Converting 144 km/h = 40 m/s. Since u = 0, a = v/t = 40/20 = 2 m/s². Distance s = ½at² = ½ × 2 × 400 = 400 m.
Question 2
A particle is moving at 18 m/s. After 2.5 seconds, its speed becomes 30 m/s but in the opposite direction. What is the magnitude and direction of the average acceleration?
A. 19.2 m/s² in the direction of initial velocity
B. 19.2 m/s² opposite to initial velocity
C. 4.8 m/s² in the direction of initial velocity
D. 4.8 m/s² opposite to initial velocity
Solution: Taking initial direction as positive: u = +18 m/s, v = −30 m/s. Average acceleration a = (v − u)/t = (−30 − 18)/2.5 = −48/2.5 = −19.2 m/s². The negative sign indicates the acceleration is directed opposite to the initial velocity.
Question 3
A particle moves in a straight line covering 10 m in the first 5 seconds and another 10 m in the next 3 seconds. Assuming constant acceleration throughout, what is the distance covered in the next 2 seconds?
A. 8/3 m
B. 9.3 m
C. 10.3 m
D. None of these
Solution: Using s = ut + ½at²: For first 5s, 10 = 5u + 12.5a …(i). For first 8s, 20 = 8u + 32a …(ii). Solving simultaneously: from (i) u = (10 − 12.5a)/5; substituting into (ii) gives a = 10/27 m/s² and u = 43/27 m/s. Distance in 10s = 10u + 50a = 430/27 + 500/27 = 930/27 ≈ 34.4 m. So distance in next 2s = 34.4 − 20 ≈ 14.4 m, which doesn't match A, B, or C.
Question 4
A body starts from rest and moves with uniform acceleration. The ratio of the distance travelled in the 4th second to the distance travelled in the 3rd second is:
A. 7 : 5
B. 5 : 7
C. 7 : 3
D. 3 : 7
Solution: Distance in the n-th second: sn = u + a(2n − 1)/2. With u = 0, sn = a(2n − 1)/2. So s4/s3 = (2×4 − 1)/(2×3 − 1) = 7/5.
Question 5
From the top of a tower, a stone is thrown upward with speed u and reaches the ground in time t<sub>1</sub>. A second stone is thrown downward with the same speed u and reaches the ground in time t<sub>2</sub>. A third stone is released from rest and reaches the ground in time t<sub>3</sub>. Then t<sub>3</sub><sup>2</sup> = t<sub>1</sub> · t<sub>2</sub>.
A. True
B. False
C. Only true if u is small
D. Depends on the height of the tower
Solution: Let height = h. For stone thrown up: h = −ut1 + ½gt1². For stone thrown down: h = ut2 + ½gt2². For stone dropped: h = ½gt3². Adding the first two: 2h = ½g(t1² + t2²) + u(t2 − t1). Solving these three equations yields t3² = t1 · t2.
Question 6
A ball is dropped from a height of 10 m and bounces back to a height of 2.5 m. If the duration of contact with the floor is 0.01 s, the average acceleration during contact is (take g = 9.8 m/s²):
A. 2100 m/s² downward
B. 2100 m/s² upward
C. 1400 m/s² upward
D. 700 m/s² downward
Solution: Speed just before hitting: v1 = √(2 × 9.8 × 10) = 14 m/s (downward). Speed just after bouncing: v2 = √(2 × 9.8 × 2.5) = 7 m/s (upward). Taking upward as positive: Δv = 7 − (−14) = 21 m/s. Average acceleration = 21/0.01 = 2100 m/s² upward.
Question 7
A body falls freely from rest. The distance covered in the last second of its fall equals the distance covered in the first 3 seconds. The total time of fall is:
A. 3 s
B. 5 s
C. 7 s
D. 9 s
Solution: Distance in first 3s: s3 = ½g(3)² = 4.5g. Distance in the n-th (last) second: sn = ½g(2n − 1). Setting equal: ½g(2n − 1) = 4.5g, so 2n − 1 = 9, giving n = 5 s.
Question 8
Which of the following statements about speed and velocity is INCORRECT?
A. If speed changes, velocity must change
B. If velocity changes, speed may or may not change
C. If speed remains constant, velocity must also remain constant
D. If velocity changes, speed may remain constant (e.g., uniform circular motion)
Solution: Statement C is incorrect. An object moving in a circle at constant speed has changing velocity (direction changes), so constant speed does NOT guarantee constant velocity.
Question 9
Which of the following statements is correct?
A. The magnitude of instantaneous velocity always equals the instantaneous speed
B. The magnitude of average velocity over any interval equals the average speed over that interval
C. It is possible for the instantaneous speed to be zero at some moment while the average speed over a finite interval is non-zero
D. It is possible for the speed to never be zero during an interval, yet the average speed is zero
Solution: Option A is actually true for instantaneous values, but option C is also correct and is the intended answer. A thrown ball has zero speed at its peak, yet its average speed over the whole flight is non-zero. Option B is false (a round trip has zero average velocity but positive average speed). Option D is false (average speed is always positive if the object ever moves).
Question 10
A 120 m long train moves northward at 20 m/s. Another 130 m long train travels southward on a parallel track at 30 m/s. The time taken for the two trains to completely cross each other is:
A. 6 s
B. 36 s
C. 38 s
D. 5 s
Solution: When two objects move in opposite directions, relative speed = 20 + 30 = 50 m/s. Total distance to cross = sum of lengths = 120 + 130 = 250 m. Time = 250/50 = 5 s.
Question 11
A projectile is launched from the ground with speed u at an angle θ with the horizontal. The horizontal range is maximum when θ equals:
A. 30°
B. 45°
C. 60°
D. 90°
Solution: Range R = u²sin(2θ)/g. For maximum range, sin(2θ) = 1, which gives 2θ = 90°, so θ = 45°.
Question 12
A particle is projected at 60° above the horizontal with a speed of 20 m/s. The total time of flight is (take g = 10 m/s²):
A. 2√3 s
B. 2 s
C. 3 s
D. 4 s
Solution: Time of flight T = 2u sinθ/g = 2 × 20 × sin60° / 10 = 2 × 20 × (√3/2) / 10 = 2√3 s.
Question 13
Two projectiles are launched with the same initial speed but at complementary angles (e.g., 30° and 60°). Which one has the greater horizontal range?
A. The one launched at the greater angle
B. The one launched at the smaller angle
C. Both have the same range
D. Cannot be determined without knowing the speed
Solution: Range R = u²sin(2θ)/g. For complementary angles θ and (90° − θ), sin(2θ) = sin(180° − 2θ) = sin(2(90° − θ)). Therefore both projectiles have the same range.
Question 14
A man walks horizontally at 3 m/s while rain falls vertically at 4 m/s. The speed of the rain as observed by the man is:
A. 5 m/s
B. 7 m/s
C. 1 m/s
D. 3.5 m/s
Solution: The velocity of rain relative to man is found by vector subtraction. Since the man's velocity (horizontal) and rain's velocity (vertical) are perpendicular: vrel = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5 m/s.
Question 15
A boat can travel at 5 km/h in still water. A river flows at 3 km/h. The time required for the boat to cross the river (0.4 km wide) by the shortest path is:
A. 6 min
B. 0.1 hr
C. 5 min
D. 8 min
Solution: For the shortest path (straight across), the boat must angle upstream. The perpendicular component of velocity = √(vboat² − vriver²) = √(25 − 9) = 4 km/h. Time = distance/speed = 0.4/4 = 0.1 hr.
