Kinematics Level 2 — Sprint Test 1

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
Average velocity of a particle moving in a straight line, with constant acceleration a and initial velocity u, in first t seconds is:
A. u + (1/2)at
B. u + at
C. (u + at)/2
D. u/2
Solution: Average velocity = total displacement / time = (ut + (1/2)at2)/t = u + (1/2)at.
Question 2
A body travelling along a straight line traversed one third of the total distance with a velocity 4 m/s. The remaining part of the distance was covered with velocity 1 m/s for half the time and with velocity 3 m/s for the other half of time. The mean velocity averaged over the whole time of motion is:
A. 2.4 m/s
B. 4 m/s
C. 1.9 m/s
D. 3 m/s
Solution: Let total distance = 3d. First d at 4 m/s: t1 = d/4. Remaining 2d: average speed = (1 + 3)/2 = 2 m/s, time t2 = 2d/2 = d. Total time = d/4 + d = 5d/4. Mean velocity = 3d ÷ (5d/4) = 12/5 = 2.4 m/s.
Question 3
The accelerations of a particle as seen from two frames S1 and S2 have equal magnitude of 4 m/s2. Then:
A. The frames must be at rest with respect to each other
B. The frames may be moving with respect to each other but neither should be accelerated with respect to the other
C. The acceleration of S2 with respect to S1 may either be zero or 8 m/s2
D. The acceleration of S2 with respect to S1 may be anything between zero and 8 m/s2
Solution: If the acceleration as seen from both frames has magnitude 4 m/s2, and the frames have relative acceleration arel, then by vector addition, |arel| can range from |4 − 4| = 0 to |4 + 4| = 8 depending on direction. So D is correct.
Question 4
Three particles start from the origin at the same time, one with a velocity v1 along x-axis, the second with a velocity v2 along y-axis and the third along x = y line. The velocity of the third so that the three may always lie on the same line is:
A. v1v2 / (v1 + v2)
B. √2 · v1v2 / (v1 + v2)
C. √3 · v1v2 / (v1 + v2)
D. zero
Solution: At time t, positions: P1 = (v1t, 0), P2 = (0, v2t), P3 = (v3t/√2, v3t/√2). For collinearity, the three points must satisfy the determinant condition. This gives v3 = √2 · v1v2 / (v1 + v2).
Question 5
A cubical room has dimensions 4 ft × 4 ft × 4 ft. An insect starts from one corner O and crawls (does not fly) to reach a corner on the opposite side of the body diagonal. Find the minimum distance travelled by the insect to reach the destination.
A. (4√2 + 4) ft
B. √48 ft
C. √80 ft
D. None of these
Solution: The shortest crawling path is found by unfolding the cube. Unfolding one face: the path becomes the diagonal of a rectangle 8 ft × 4 ft. Distance = √(82 + 42) = √(64 + 16) = √80 ft.
Question 6
In the previous problem, the magnitude of displacement is:
A. Zero
B. √80 ft
C. √48 ft
D. None of these
Solution: The displacement is the straight-line body diagonal of the cube = √(42 + 42 + 42) = √48 ft.
Question 7
A point mass starts moving in a straight line with constant acceleration α from rest at t = 0. At time t = 2 s, the acceleration changes sign, remaining the same in magnitude. The mass returns to the initial position at time t = t0 after start of motion. Here t0 is:
A. 4 s
B. (4 + 2√2) s
C. (2 + 2√2) s
D. (4 + 4√2) s
Solution: Phase 1 (0 to 2 s): velocity v = 2α, distance covered = 2α. Phase 2 (t > 2 s): decelerates at α. Velocity = 2α − α(t − 2). Stops at t = 4 s; total distance from origin = 2α + 2α = 4α. Then moves back toward origin. Returns when: 4α = (1/2)α(t − 4)2, so (t − 4) = 2√2, giving t0 = (4 + 2√2) s.
Question 8
A particle moving with uniform acceleration along a straight line covers distances a and b in successive intervals of p and q seconds. The acceleration of the particle is:
A. pq(p + q) / [2(bp − aq)]
B. 2(aq − bp) / [pq(p − q)]
C. (bp − aq) / [pq(p − q)]
D. 2(bp − aq) / [pq(p + q)]
Solution: Using s = ut + (1/2)αt2: for first interval, a = up + (1/2)αp2 …(i); for both intervals, a + b = u(p + q) + (1/2)α(p + q)2 …(ii). From (i): u = (a − (1/2)αp2)/p. Substituting into (ii) and simplifying gives α = 2(bp − aq) / [pq(p + q)].
Question 9
A ball is thrown vertically upwards. It was observed at a height h twice, with a time gap of Δt. The initial velocity of the ball is:
A. √(8gh + g2(Δt)2)
B. √(8gh + 4g2(Δt)2)
C. (1/2)√(8gh + g2(Δt)2)
D. None of these
Solution: h = ut − (1/2)gt2 gives t2 − (2u/g)t + 2h/g = 0. The two roots t1, t2 satisfy t2 − t1 = Δt and t1 + t2 = 2u/g. Then (Δt)2 = (t1 + t2)2 − 4t1t2 = 4u2/g2 − 8h/g. So 4u2/g2 = (Δt)2 + 8h/g. Therefore u = (1/2)√(g2(Δt)2 + 8gh).
Question 10
The driver of a car moving with a speed of 10 ms−1 sees a red light ahead, applies brakes and stops after covering 10 m distance. If the same car were moving with a speed of 20 ms−1, the same driver would have stopped the car after covering 30 m distance. Within what distance can the car be stopped if travelling with a velocity of 15 ms−1? Assume the same reaction time and the same deceleration in each case.
A. 18.75 m
B. 20.75 m
C. 22.75 m
D. 25 m
Solution: Let reaction time = t0, deceleration = a. Case 1: 10t0 + 102/(2a) = 10. Case 2: 20t0 + 202/(2a) = 30. From these two equations: 10t0 + 50/(a) = 20, giving t0 = 0.5 s and a = 10 m/s2. For v = 15 ms−1: d = 15 × 0.5 + 152/(2 × 10) = 7.5 + 11.25 = 18.75 m.
