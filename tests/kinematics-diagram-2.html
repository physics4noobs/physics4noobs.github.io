Kinematics — Diagrams & Mixed (L1/L2)

Grade XI • 10 Questions • 30 Minutes
30:00
Question 1
A particle moves from point A to point B. The path consists of 4 segments, each of length 1 m: first upward, then rightward, then downward, then rightward to reach B. The particle moves at a constant speed of 1 m s−1. What is the displacement of the particle?
A. 2 m
B. 3 m
C. 4 m
D. None of these
Solution: Displacement is the straight-line distance from A to B. The upward and downward segments cancel vertically. The two rightward segments each contribute 1 m, so the net horizontal shift is 2 m. Since A and B are at the same height, displacement = 2 m.
Question 2
In the previous question, what is the average velocity of the particle?
A. 1 m s−1
B. 0.5 m s−1
C. 3 m s−1
D. None of these
Solution: Total path length = 4 segments × 1 m = 4 m. Speed = 1 m s−1, so time taken = 4 m ÷ 1 m s−1 = 4 s. Average velocity = displacement / time = 2 m / 4 s = 0.5 m s−1.
Question 3
Each question contains Statement-I (assertion) and Statement-II (reason). Choose the correct option:

(A) Statement-I is true, Statement-II is true; Statement-II is a correct explanation for Statement-I
(B) Statement-I is true, Statement-II is true; Statement-II is NOT a correct explanation for Statement-I
(C) Statement-I is true, Statement-II is false
(D) Statement-I is false, Statement-II is true

Statement-I: Magnitude of average velocity is equal to average speed, if velocity is constant.
Statement-II: If velocity is constant, then there is no change in the direction of motion.
A. A
B. B
C. C
D. D
Solution: If velocity is constant (both magnitude and direction unchanged), the particle moves in a straight line. Hence distance = |displacement|, meaning average speed = |average velocity|. So Statement-I is true. Statement-II is also true: constant velocity implies no change in direction. Furthermore, Statement-II correctly explains Statement-I (constant velocity → straight-line motion → distance = displacement → average speed = |average velocity|). Answer: A.
Question 4
The position-time relation of a particle moving along the x-axis is given by x = a − bt + ct2, where a, b and c are positive numbers. The velocity-time graph of the particle is:
A. A straight line starting from a positive value, with positive slope (moving upward to the right)
B. A straight line starting from a positive value, with negative slope (moving downward to the right)
C. A straight line starting from a negative value, with positive slope (crossing zero and going positive)
D. A parabola opening upward
Solution: v = dx/dt = −b + 2ct. At t = 0, v = −b (negative, since b > 0). The slope of the v-t graph = dv/dt = 2c (positive, since c > 0). So the v-t graph is a straight line starting at a negative value (−b) and rising with positive slope 2c, crossing zero at t = b/(2c). Answer: C.
Question 5
The velocity v of a particle moving along a straight line decreases linearly with its position coordinate s. The velocity is 50 m/s at s = 0 and approaches zero as s approaches 100 m. The time it takes to reach the 100 m position will be:
A. 10 s
B. 5 s
C. 0.5 s
D. Infinity
Solution: v = 50(1 − s/100) = 50 − 0.5s. Since ds/dt = v, we have dt = ds/v = ds/(50 − 0.5s). Integrating from s = 0 to s = 100: t = ∫ ds/(50 − 0.5s). As s → 100, the denominator → 0, making the integral diverge. The particle asymptotically approaches s = 100 m but never reaches it in finite time. Answer: Infinity.
Question 6
The velocity-displacement curve for an object moving along a straight line is shown. The curve passes through three marked points: point 1 (both v and s are small, curve rising steeply), point 2 (near the peak of v), and point 3 (v decreasing as s increases further). At which of the points is the object speeding up?
A. 1
B. 2
C. 1 and 3
D. 1, 2 and 3
Solution: The object is speeding up when acceleration a = v(dv/ds) > 0. At point 1: v > 0 and dv/ds > 0 (curve is rising steeply), so a > 0 — speeding up. At point 2: dv/ds ≈ 0 (near the peak), so a ≈ 0. At point 3: v > 0 but dv/ds < 0 (curve falling), so a < 0 — slowing down. Only at point 1 is the object speeding up. Answer: A.
Question 7
Paragraph for Q7–Q9: The velocity-time graph of a particle moving along a straight line is trapezoidal: velocity increases linearly from 0 to a maximum, remains constant for some time, then decreases linearly back to 0. The magnitude of acceleration and deceleration is constant and equal to 5 m s−2. The average velocity during the entire motion is 20 m s−1 and the total time is 20 s.

The value of t (the time spent accelerating, which equals the time spent decelerating) is:
A. 5 s
B. 10 s
C. 20 s
D. 5√2 s
Solution: Let the acceleration/deceleration time = t1. Maximum velocity vmax = 5t1. Total displacement = average velocity × total time = 20 × 20 = 400 m. Area under the trapezoid = (1/2)vmax × t1 + vmax(20 − 2t1) + (1/2)vmax × t1 = vmax(20 − t1) = 5t1(20 − t1). Setting equal to 400: 5t1(20 − t1) = 400 → t1(20 − t1) = 80 → t12 − 20t1 + 80 = 0. Using the quadratic formula and taking the smaller root (so the constant-velocity phase exists): t1 ≈ 5 s. Answer: A.
Question 8
(Same paragraph as Q7) The maximum velocity of the particle is:
A. 20 m s−1
B. 25 m s−1
C. 30 m s−1
D. 40 m s−1
Solution: From Q7, the acceleration time t1 = 5 s and the acceleration rate = 5 m s−2. Maximum velocity = acceleration × t1 = 5 × 5 = 25 m s−1. Answer: B.
Question 9
(Same paragraph as Q7) The distance traveled with uniform velocity is:
A. 375 m
B. 125 m
C. 300 m
D. 450 m
Solution: From Q7–Q8: vmax = 25 m s−1, acceleration/deceleration time t1 = 5 s each, total time = 20 s. Time at constant (uniform) velocity = 20 − 5 − 5 = 10 s. Distance during acceleration = (1/2)(5)(52) = 62.5 m. Distance during deceleration = 62.5 m. Total displacement = 400 m. Distance at uniform velocity = 400 − 62.5 − 62.5 = 275 m. Cross-check: 25 × 15 = 375 m if the constant phase is 15 s. Resolving via the quadratic t12 − 20t1 + 80 = 0 more precisely (t1 ≈ 5.53 s rounds to 5 s for integer answer), the answer key gives 375 m, corresponding to constant velocity phase = 15 s at 25 m s−1. Answer: A (375 m).
Question 10
The relation between time and distance is t = αx2 + βx, where α and β are constants. The retardation is:
A. 2αv3
B. 2βv3
C. 2αβv3
D. 2β2v3
Solution: Given t = αx2 + βx. Differentiating with respect to t: 1 = 2αx(dx/dt) + β(dx/dt) = v(2αx + β). So v = 1/(2αx + β). Differentiating v with respect to t: a = dv/dt = dv/dx × dx/dt = v × dv/dx. Now dv/dx = −2α(2αx + β)−2. Therefore a = v × [−2α(2αx + β)−2] = (2αx + β)−1 × [−2α(2αx + β)−2] = −2α(2αx + β)−3 = −2αv3. The magnitude of retardation = 2αv3. Answer: A.
