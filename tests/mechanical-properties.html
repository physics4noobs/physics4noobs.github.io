Mechanical Properties — Daily Test Series

Grade XI • 15 Questions • 90 Minutes
90:00
Question 1
A wire of length 2 m and cross-sectional area 1 mm² is stretched by a force of 200 N. If Young's modulus is 2 × 1011 N/m², the extension produced is:
A. 1 mm
B. 2 mm
C. 4 mm
D. 0.5 mm
Solution: Y = FL/(AΔL). So ΔL = FL/(AY) = 200 × 2/(1 × 10−6 × 2 × 1011) = 400/(2 × 105) = 2 × 10−3 m = 2 mm.
Question 2
The stress in a wire of diameter 2 mm carrying a load of 100 N is:
A. 3.18 × 107 N/m²
B. 1.27 × 108 N/m²
C. 5 × 107 N/m²
D. 1 × 107 N/m²
Solution: Area = πr² = π(1 × 10−3)² = π × 10−6 m². Stress = F/A = 100/(π × 10−6) = 100/(3.14 × 10−6) ≈ 3.18 × 107 N/m².
Question 3
Two wires of the same material have lengths in the ratio 1 : 2 and diameters in the ratio 2 : 1. When the same force is applied, the ratio of their extensions is:
A. 1 : 8
B. 8 : 1
C. 1 : 8
D. 2 : 1
Solution: ΔL = FL/(AY) ∝ L/A ∝ L/d². So ΔL1/ΔL2 = (L1/L2) × (d2/d1)² = (1/2) × (1/2)² = (1/2)(1/4) = 1/8.
Question 4
The elastic potential energy stored per unit volume of a strained body is:
A. Stress × Strain
B. Stress / Strain
C. Stress² / Young's modulus
D. ½ × Stress × Strain
Solution: Energy per unit volume (energy density) = ½ × Stress × Strain = ½ × σ × ε.
Question 5
A rubber band can be stretched to several times its original length and still return. This property is called:
A. Plasticity
B. Elasticity
C. Viscosity
D. Ductility
Solution: Elasticity is the property of a material to return to its original shape after the deforming force is removed. Rubber is highly elastic in this sense.
Question 6
The bulk modulus of a material is defined as:
A. −ΔP / (ΔV/V)
B. ΔP × (ΔV/V)
C. F / (A × ΔL/L)
D. FΔL / (AL)
Solution: Bulk modulus K = −ΔP / (ΔV/V) = −V(ΔP/ΔV). The negative sign ensures K is positive since ΔV is negative when pressure increases.
Question 7
A steel wire and a copper wire of the same length and same cross-section are stretched by the same force. If Young's modulus of steel is twice that of copper, the ratio of their extensions is:
A. 2 : 1
B. 4 : 1
C. 1 : 2
D. 1 : 1
Solution: ΔL = FL/(AY). For same F, L, A: ΔL ∝ 1/Y. So ΔLsteel/ΔLcopper = Ycopper/Ysteel = 1/2. The ratio is 1 : 2.
Question 8
The Poisson's ratio of a material is 0.3. If a wire of this material is stretched, the ratio of lateral strain to longitudinal strain is:
A. 0.7
B. 0.3
C. 3.33
D. 1.3
Solution: Poisson's ratio σ = lateral strain / longitudinal strain. Given σ = 0.3, the ratio of lateral strain to longitudinal strain is 0.3.
Question 9
A wire is stretched by a certain force. The work done in stretching is stored as elastic PE. If the extension is doubled, the elastic PE becomes:
A. Same
B. Double
C. Triple
D. Four times
Solution: Elastic PE = ½ × (YA/L) × (ΔL)². PE ∝ (ΔL)². If ΔL is doubled, PE becomes 4 times.
Question 10
The breaking stress of a wire does not depend on:
A. Length of the wire
B. Material of the wire
C. Temperature
D. Cross-sectional area
Solution: Breaking stress is a property of the material, not the dimensions. It does not depend on the length of the wire. Note: while the breaking force depends on cross-sectional area, the breaking stress (force per unit area) is independent of area too, but length is the clearest answer as it has no effect whatsoever.
Question 11
A wire of length L stretches by ΔL when a force F is applied. A wire of the same material but of length 2L and diameter 2d (where d is the original diameter) is stretched by the same force. Its extension will be:
A. ΔL
B. 2ΔL
C. ΔL/2
D. 4ΔL
Solution: ΔL' = F(2L)/(π(2d/2)² × Y) = F(2L)/(πd² × Y) × (1/4) × 4 = 2FL/(4 × π(d/2)² × Y). Let A = πd²/4. ΔL' = F(2L)/(4A × Y) = (FL)/(2AY) = ΔL/2.
Question 12
The modulus of rigidity relates to:
A. Change in volume
B. Change in shape
C. Change in length
D. Change in density
Solution: The modulus of rigidity (shear modulus) is the ratio of shearing stress to shearing strain. It relates to changes in shape at constant volume.
Question 13
A wire suspended from the ceiling supports a load of 50 kg. If the wire's cross-sectional area is 0.5 mm², the stress in the wire is (g = 10 m/s²):
A. 109 N/m²
B. 108 N/m²
C. 107 N/m²
D. 106 N/m²
Solution: F = mg = 50 × 10 = 500 N. A = 0.5 mm² = 0.5 × 10−6 m². Stress = F/A = 500/(0.5 × 10−6) = 109 N/m².
Question 14
The stress-strain curve for a ductile material has a region after the yield point where the material deforms permanently. This region is called the:
A. Elastic region
B. Fracture region
C. Hooke's region
D. Plastic region
Solution: Beyond the yield point, permanent deformation occurs. This is the plastic region of the stress-strain curve.
Question 15
A copper wire and a steel wire of the same diameter are connected end to end and stretched by a force. Young's modulus of steel is about twice that of copper. The ratio of extension of the copper wire to the steel wire is:
A. 1 : 2
B. 1 : 1
C. 2 : 1
D. 4 : 1
Solution: Same force, same area, same length assumed. ΔL ∝ 1/Y. Since Ysteel = 2Ycopper, ΔLcopper/ΔLsteel = Ysteel/Ycopper = 2. The ratio is 2 : 1.
