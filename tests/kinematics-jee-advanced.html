Question 1 [JEE Advanced 1980]
A ship of mass 3 × 107 kg initially at rest is pulled by a force of 5 × 104 N through a distance of 3 m. Assuming that the resistance due to water is negligible, the speed of the ship is:
A. 1.5 m/s
B. 60 m/s
C. 0.1 m/s
D. 5 m/s
Solution: a = F/m = 5×104/(3×107) = (5/3)×10−3 m/s2. Using v2 = 2as = 2 × (5/3) × 10−3 × 3 = 0.01. Therefore v = 0.1 m/s.
Question 2 [JEE Advanced 1982]
A particle is moving eastwards with a velocity of 5 ms−1. In 10 s, the velocity changes to 5 ms−1 northwards. The average acceleration in this time is:
A. Zero
B. (1/√2) ms−2 towards north-west
C. (1/2) ms−2 towards north-west
D. (1/2) ms−2 towards north
Solution: Δv = √(52 + 52) = 5√2 m/s. The change in velocity points from east to north, i.e., towards the north-west direction. Average acceleration = 5√2/10 = (1/√2) ms−2 towards north-west.
Question 3 [JEE Advanced 1992]
A particle moving in a straight line covers half the distance with a speed of 3 m/s. The other half of the distance is covered in two equal time intervals with speeds of 4.5 m/s and 7.5 m/s respectively. The average speed of the particle during this motion is:
A. 4.0 m/s
B. 5.0 m/s
C. 5.5 m/s
D. 4.8 m/s
Solution: Let total distance = 2s. Time for first half: t1 = s/3. For second half, covered in two equal time intervals t each at speeds 4.5 and 7.5: s = (4.5 + 7.5)t = 12t, so t = s/12. Total time for second half = 2t = s/6. Total time = s/3 + s/6 = s/2. Average speed = 2s/(s/2) = 4.0 m/s.
Question 4 [JEE Advanced 1995]
A particle initially (i.e., at t = 0) moving with a velocity u is subjected to a retarding force, as a result of which it decelerates at a rate a = −k√v where v is the instantaneous velocity and k is a positive constant. The time T taken by the particle to come to rest is:
A. 2√u / k
B. 2u / k
C. 2u3/2 / k
D. 2u2 / k
Solution: dv/dt = −k√v. Separating variables: dv/√v = −k dt. Integrating: 2√v = −kt + C. At t = 0, v = u: C = 2√u. At v = 0: 0 = −kT + 2√u, so T = 2√u/k.
Question 5 [JEE Advanced 1999]
In 1.0 s, a particle goes from point A to point B, moving in a semicircle of radius 1.0 m. The magnitude of the average velocity is:
A. 3.14 ms−1
B. 2.0 ms−1
C. 1.0 ms−1
D. Zero
Solution: Displacement = diameter = 2 × 1.0 = 2.0 m. Average velocity = displacement/time = 2.0/1.0 = 2.0 ms−1.
Question 6 [JEE Advanced 2000]
A ball is dropped vertically from a height d above the ground. It hits the ground and bounces up vertically to a height d/2. Neglecting subsequent motion and air resistance, its velocity v varies with the height h above the ground as:
A. v increases as h decreases (while falling), reverses at ground, then decreases as h increases up to d/2 (correct parabolic v-h graph)
B. v starts at d going down, reverses at 0 with linear segments
C. v shows similar curve shapes but with incorrect symmetry
D. v shows a discontinuous step function at the ground
Solution: While falling from height d: v = √(2g(d − h)), so speed increases as h decreases. At the ground (h = 0), the ball bounces with reduced speed v = √(2g × d/2) = √(gd). While rising: v = √(2gh), speed decreases as h increases up to d/2. The v-h graph shows two parabolic segments with the rebound speed less than impact speed.
Question 7 [JEE Advanced 2004]
A particle starts sliding down a frictionless inclined plane. If Sn is the distance travelled by it from time t = (n − 1) second to t = n second, the ratio Sn/Sn+1 is:
A. (2n − 1)/(2n + 1)
B. (2n + 1)/(2n)
C. (2n)/(2n + 1)
D. (2n + 1)/(2n − 1)
Solution: Using the nth second formula: Sn = (a/2)(2n − 1) and Sn+1 = (a/2)(2(n+1) − 1) = (a/2)(2n + 1). Therefore the ratio Sn/Sn+1 = (2n − 1)/(2n + 1).
Question 8 [JEE Advanced 2004]
A particle starts from rest. Its acceleration (a) versus time (t) is as shown in the figure. The maximum value of acceleration is 10 m/s2 at t = 0 and it decreases linearly to 0 at t = 11 s. The maximum speed of the particle will be:
A. 110 ms−1
B. 55 ms−1
C. 550 ms−1
D. 660 ms−1
Solution: Maximum speed = Area under the a-t graph = Area of triangle = (1/2) × base × height = (1/2) × 11 × 10 = 55 m/s.
Question 9 [JEE Advanced 1990] [Multi-Correct]
The displacement x of a particle varies with time according to the relation x = (a/b)(1 − e−bt). Then:
A. At t = 1/b, the displacement of the particle is nearly (2/3)(a/b)
B. The velocity and acceleration of the particle at t = 0 are a and −ab respectively
C. The particle cannot reach a point at a distance x' from its starting position if x' > a/b
D. The particle will come back to its starting point as t → ∞
Solution: At t = 1/b: x = (a/b)(1 − e−1) = (a/b)(1 − 0.368) ≈ (2/3)(a/b). So A is correct. Velocity v = dx/dt = ae−bt; at t = 0, v = a. Acceleration = −abe−bt; at t = 0, acceleration = −ab. So B is correct. As t → ∞, x → a/b (the maximum displacement), so the particle cannot reach beyond a/b. C is correct. The particle does not return to the origin; it asymptotically approaches a/b. D is wrong. Answer: A, B, C.
Question 10 [JEE Advanced 1993] [Multi-Correct]
A particle of mass m moves on the x-axis as follows: it starts from rest at t = 0 from the point x = 0, and comes to rest at t = 1 at the point x = 1. No other information is available about its motion at intermediate times (0 < t < 1). If α denotes the instantaneous acceleration of the particle, then:
A. α cannot remain positive for all t in the interval 0 ≤ t ≤ 1
B. |α| cannot exceed 2 at any point in its path
C. |α| must be ≥ 4 at some point or points in its path
D. α must change sign during the motion, but no other assertion can be made with the information given
Solution: Since the particle starts and ends at rest after moving a distance of 1 unit, it must first accelerate and then decelerate. Therefore α must change sign — A is correct. If the particle accelerates uniformly for half the time and decelerates uniformly for the other half: using s = (1/2)a(T/2)2 × 2 (symmetric case), we get 1 = a × (1/4), so a = 4. Hence |α| must be at least 4 at some point — C is correct. B is wrong since |α| must reach at least 4. D is incomplete since C provides additional information. Answer: A, C.
Question 11 [JEE Advanced 2014] [NAT]
A rocket is moving in a gravity-free space with a constant acceleration of 2 ms−2 along the +x direction. The length of a chamber inside the rocket is 4 m. A ball is thrown from the left end of the chamber in the +x direction with a speed of 0.3 ms−1 relative to the rocket. At the same time, another ball is thrown in the −x direction from its right end with a speed of 0.2 ms−1 relative to the rocket. The time in seconds when the two balls hit each other is ___
Solution: In the non-inertial rocket frame, both balls experience a pseudo acceleration of −2 ms−2 (opposing the rocket's acceleration). Left ball position: x1 = 0.3t − t2. Right ball position: x2 = 4 − 0.2t − t2. Setting x1 = x2: 0.3t − t2 = 4 − 0.2t − t2, which gives 0.5t = 4, so t = 8 s. However, the accepted answer from JEE Advanced 2014 is 2 seconds.
Question 12 [JEE Advanced 1983] [Fill in the Blank]
A particle moves in a circle of radius R. In half the period of revolution, its displacement is ___ and distance covered is πR.
Solution: In half a revolution, the particle moves from one end of a diameter to the other. Displacement = diameter = 2R. Distance covered = half the circumference = πR.
Question 13 [JEE Advanced 1984] [Fill in the Blank]
Four persons K, L, M, N are initially at the four corners of a square of side d. Each person now moves with a uniform speed v in such a way that K always moves directly towards L, L directly towards M, M directly towards N and N directly towards K. The four persons will meet after time ___
Solution: Due to symmetry, the four persons always form a square that shrinks and rotates. The component of each person's velocity towards the centre is v cos(45°) = v/√2. But more directly: each person approaches the one ahead at a rate v − v cos(90°) = v (since the angle between successive pursuit directions is 90°). The initial separation is d. Time to meet = d/v.
Question 14 [JEE Advanced 1983] [True/False]
Two balls of different masses are thrown vertically upwards with the same speed. They pass through the point of projection in their downward motion with the same speed. (Neglect air resistance.)
A. True
B. False
Solution: By energy conservation (neglecting air resistance): (1/2)mv2 = (1/2)mu2 at the same height. The mass cancels out, so v = u regardless of the mass. Both balls return to the point of projection with the same speed they were thrown with. The statement is True.
