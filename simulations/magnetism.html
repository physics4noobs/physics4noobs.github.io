🌘 Lorentz Force

Grade XII • Moving Charges & Magnetism

About This Simulation

When a charged particle enters a uniform magnetic field perpendicular to its velocity, it experiences the Lorentz force, causing it to move in a circular path. The radius of this circular motion depends on the particle's mass, velocity, charge, and the magnetic field strength. The magnetic field here points into the screen (shown as X marks), and the positive charge enters from the left.

F = qv × B   |   r = mv/(qB)   |   T = 2πm/(qB)   |   f = qB/(2πm)
