⚡ Faraday's Law

Grade XII • Electromagnetic Induction

About This Simulation

This simulation demonstrates Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction. A bar magnet moves through a coil of wire, and the changing magnetic flux through the coil induces an electromotive force (EMF). As the magnet approaches the coil, the EMF increases; when leaving, the EMF reverses polarity (Lenz's Law). The real-time EMF graph shows how the induced voltage changes during the process.

ε = −NdΦ/dt   |   Φ = BA cos θ   |   ε = BLv (motional EMF)
