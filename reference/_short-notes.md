## Vectors

### Vector Representation

- **Vector notation:** `→a = a_x î + a_y ĵ + a_z k̂`
- **Magnitude:** `|→a| = √(a_x² + a_y² + a_z²)`

### Dot Product (Scalar Product)

- **Definition:** `→a · →b = a_x b_x + a_y b_y + a_z b_z`
- **Alternate form:** `→a · →b = |→a||→b| cos θ`

### Cross Product (Vector Product)

- **Definition:** `→a × →b = (a_y b_z - a_z b_y)î + (a_z b_x - a_x b_z)ĵ + (a_x b_y - a_y b_x)k̂`
- **Magnitude:** `|→a × →b| = |→a||→b| sin θ`
- **Matrix form:**
```
→a × →b = |î  ĵ  k̂|
          |a_x a_y a_z|
          |b_x b_y b_z|
```

## Kinematics

### Basic Equations

- **Average velocity:** `v_avg = ΔS/Δt`
- **Instantaneous velocity:** `v_inst = dS/dt`
- **Average acceleration:** `a_avg = ΔV/Δt`
- **Instantaneous acceleration:** `a_inst = dV/dt`

### Kinematic Equations

- `S = ut + ½at²`
- `v² = u² + 2aS`
- `v = u + at`

### Relative Velocity

- `v_AB = v_A - v_B` (velocity of A relative to B)

## Projectile Motion

### Key Formulas

- **Initial velocity components:** `u_x = u cos θ`, `u_y = u sin θ`
- **Time of flight:** `T = 2u sin θ/g`
- **Horizontal range:** `R = u² sin 2θ/g`
- **Maximum height:** `H = u² sin²θ/(2g)`
- **Height at time t:** `y = u_y t - ½gt² = (u sin θ)t - ½gt²`

## Laws of Motion

### Newton's Laws

1. **1st Law (Inertia):** An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force.

2. **2nd Law (Force):** `F = ma` (Force = Mass × Acceleration)

3. **3rd Law (Action & Reaction):** For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

### Key Concepts

- **Pseudo Force:** In accelerating frames, apparent force `F_pseudo = ma`
- **Centripetal Force:** `F_c = mv²/r = mω²r` (directed toward center)
- **Friction:** `f = μN` (where μ is coefficient of friction)

## Work, Power & Energy

### Work

- **Definition:** `W = F·S = FS cos θ`
- **Work-Energy Theorem:** `ΔKE = W_net`

### Power

- **Definition:** `P = dW/dt = F⃗·v⃗`
- **Average Power:** `P_avg = W/t`
- **Instantaneous Power:** `P = dW/dt`

### Energy

- **Kinetic Energy:** `KE = ½mv²`
- **Potential Energy (gravitational):** `U = mgh` (conservative force)
- **Work-Energy Theorem:** `W_net = ΔKE`
- **Energy Conservation:** `KE + U = Constant` (for conservative forces)

### Conservative Forces

- Work done by conservative forces is path-independent
- Mechanical energy is conserved in conservative systems

## Center of Mass

### Center of Mass Position

- **x-coordinate:** `x_cm = Σ(m_i x_i) / Σm_i`
- **y-coordinate:** `y_cm = Σ(m_i y_i) / Σm_i`
- **z-coordinate:** `z_cm = Σ(m_i z_i) / Σm_i`

### Center of Mass Velocity

- `v⃗_cm = Σ(m_i v⃗_i) / Σm_i`

### Moments (Geometric Centers for Standard Shapes)

- **Hollow Cone:** `h = h/4`
- **Solid Cone:** `h = h/4`
- **Hollow Hemisphere:** `z = h/2`
- **Solid Hemisphere:** `z = 3h/8`

### Force on Center of Mass

- `F⃗_net = M a⃗_cm`

## Collision

### Types of Collision

1. **Elastic Collision:** Kinetic energy is conserved
2. **Inelastic Collision:** Kinetic energy is not conserved

### Momentum Conservation (Always)

- `m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂`

### Elastic Collision

- Energy Conservation: `½m₁u₁² + ½m₂u₂² = ½m₁v₁² + ½m₂v₂²`
- Can be zero (for certain conditions)

### Coefficient of Restitution

- **Definition:** `e = (Separation velocity)/(Approach velocity) = (v₂ - v₁)/(u₁ - u₂)`
- **For elastic collision:** `e = 1`
- **For inelastic collision:** `e < 1`
- **For perfectly inelastic:** `e = 0`

### One-Dimensional Collision

For m₁ >> m₂ (unequal masses):
- Solve using CoR in m₁ frame
- Use velocity exchange for elastic collision

## Rigid Body Dynamics

### Angular Motion

- **Angular displacement:** `θ = ωt`
- **Angular velocity:** `ω = dθ/dt` (or `ω = v/r`)
- **Angular acceleration:** `α = dω/dt = d²θ/dt²`

### Equations of Rotational Motion

- `θ = ω₀t + ½αt²`
- `ω = ω₀ + αt`
- `ω² = ω₀² + 2αθ`

### Torque and Angular Momentum

- **Torque:** `τ⃗ = r⃗ × F⃗`
- **Magnitude:** `τ = r⊥F = rF sin θ`
- **Angular Momentum:** `L⃗ = r⃗ × p⃗ = m(v⃗ × r⃗)`
- **Magnitude:** `L = mvr sin θ`
- **Rotational analog:** `τ = Iα = dL/dt`

### Relationship Between Linear and Angular Quantities

- **Velocity:** `v⃗ = ω⃗ × r⃗`
- **Tangential acceleration:** `a_t = αr`
- **Centripetal acceleration:** `a_c = ω²r = v²/r`

### Equilibrium Conditions

- **Translational equilibrium:** `ΣF⃗ = 0` (or `τ_net = 0`)
- **Rotational equilibrium:** `Στ = 0`

### Period and Frequency

- `ω = 2πf` (relationship between angular velocity and frequency)
- `T = 1/f` (period)

## Moment of Inertia

### Definition

- `I = Σm_i r_i²` or `I = ∫r² dm`

### Standard Moments of Inertia

- **Rod (about center):** `I = mL²/12`
- **Rod (about end):** `I = mL²/3`
- **Ring (about center):** `I = mR²`
- **Disc (about center):** `I = mR²/2`
- **Hollow Cylinder:** `I = mR²`
- **Solid Cylinder:** `I = ½mR²`
- **Hollow Sphere:** `I = ⅖mR²`
- **Solid Sphere:** `I = ⅖mR²`

### Kinetic Energy in Rotation

- **Translational KE:** `K = ½mv²`
- **Rotational KE:** `K = ½Iω²`
- **Total KE (rolling):** `K = ½m(v_cm)² + ½Iω²`

### Parallel Axis Theorem

- `I_parallel = I_cm + md²` (where d is distance from center of mass)

### Perpendicular Axis Theorem

- For planar objects: `I_z = I_x + I_y`

## Gravitation

### Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation

- **Force:** `F = G(M·m)/r²`
- **Gravitational field:** `g = GM/r²` (at distance r from mass M)

### Gravitational Potential Energy

- **Definition:** `U = -GMm/r`
- **At surface:** `g' = g[1 - (2h/R)]` (for h << R)
- **Variation of g:** `g' = g[1 - (2h/R)]`

### Variation of g with Height and Depth

- **With height h:** `g' = g(1 - 2h/R)²`
- **With depth d:** `g' = g(1 - d/R)` or `g' = g[1 - (2h/R)]` (for d << R)

### Orbital Motion

- **Orbital velocity:** `v_orbital = √(GM/r)`
- **Orbital velocity at surface:** `v_surface = √(GM/R) = √(gR)`
- **Escape velocity:** `v_escape = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR)`

### Kepler's Laws

1. **1st Law:** Orbits are elliptical with the sun at one focus
2. **2nd Law:** Equal areas are swept in equal times (constant areal velocity)
3. **3rd Law:** `T² ∝ a³` (where a is semi-major axis)

### Orbital Parameters

- Semi-major axis relation: `T² ∝ r³` (for circular orbits `T² = (4π²/GM) × r³`)

## Simple Harmonic Motion

### Hooke's Law

- **Force:** `F = -kx` (restoring force proportional to displacement)
- **Spring constant:** `k` (stiffness)

### SHM Equations

- **Displacement:** `x = A sin(ωt + φ)` (amplitude A, angular frequency ω, phase φ)
- **Velocity:** `v = dx/dt = Aω cos(ωt + φ)`
- **Acceleration:** `a = dv/dt = -Aω² sin(ωt + φ) = -ω²x`

### Energy in SHM

- **Total Energy:** `E = K + U = ½mω²A²` (constant)
- **Kinetic Energy:** `K = ½mv² = ½mω²(A² - x²)`
- **Potential Energy:** `U = ½kx² = ½mω²x²`
- **Force constant:** `k = mω²`

### Period of Oscillation

- **Spring:** `T = 2π√(m/k)`
- **Simple Pendulum:** `T = 2π√(L/g)`
- **Physical Pendulum:** `T = 2π√(I/(mgd))` (where I is moment of inertia about pivot, d is distance from pivot to center of mass)

### Superposition of SHM

- **Same frequency:** Resultant is SHM with same frequency
- **Different frequencies:** Beats occur with beat frequency `f_beat = |f₁ - f₂|`

## Waves

### Wave Properties

- **Amplitude:** A (maximum displacement)
- **Wavelength:** λ (distance between successive crests)
- **Period:** T (time for one complete oscillation)
- **Frequency:** f = 1/T
- **Angular frequency:** ω = 2πf = 2π/T
- **Wave number:** k = 2π/λ
- **Wave velocity:** v = λf = λ/T = ω/k

### Wave Equation

- **General form:** `∂²y/∂x² = (1/v²)(∂²y/∂t²)`
- **Solution:** `y = A sin(kx - ωt)` or `y = A sin(kx + ωt)`
- **Also written as:** `y = A sin[2π(x/λ - t/T)]`

### Standing Waves

- **Condition:** `y₁ = A sin(kx - ωt)` and `y₂ = A sin(kx + ωt)`
- **Resultant:** `Y = 2A cos(kx) sin(ωt)` (nodes at x = nπ/k where n = 0,1,2,...)
- **Node condition:** Node IFF `2x = n(λ/2)` where integer n

## Sound Waves

### Longitudinal Waves

- **Displacement:** `S = S₀ sin[ωt - x/v)]`
- **Pressure:** `P = P₀ cos[ωt - x/v)]`
- **Relation:** `P = -B(∂S/∂x)` where B is bulk modulus
- **Relation:** `P_max = B·(ω·S₀)·(v/B) = v·ρ·ω·S₀`

### Wave Properties

- **Intensity:** `I = (P₀²)/(2ρv) = P₀S₀v` where ρ is density
- **Velocity (general):** `v = √(B/ρ)`

### Sound Intensity

- **Formula:** `I = (P₀²)/(2ρv)` where P₀ is pressure amplitude
- **Also:** `I = (2πf)² · ρ · S₀²` (where S₀ is displacement amplitude)
- **Intensity ratio:** `I = I₁/I₂ = (A₁/A₂)²` (same frequency and medium)

### Closed Organ Pipe (One End Closed)

- **Fundamental frequency:** `f₀ = v/(4L)`
- **Natural frequencies:** `f_n = (2n+1)v/(4L)` (n = 0,1,2,...)

### Open Organ Pipe (Both Ends Open)

- **Fundamental frequency:** `f₀ = v/(2L)`
- **Natural frequencies:** `f_n = nv/(2L)` (n = 1,2,3,...)

### Resonance Column Setup

- **For closed pipe:** `L = (2n+1)λ/4` (resonance condition)
- **For open pipe:** `L = nλ/2` (resonance condition)

### Beats

- **Beat frequency:** `f_beat = |f₁ - f₂|`
- **Beat period:** Same as oscillation but with modulated amplitude
- **Occurs when:** Two waves of slightly different frequencies overlap

### Doppler Effect

- **Observed frequency:** `f' = f(v ± v_observer)/(v ∓ v_source)`
- **When source moves toward observer:** `f' = f(v)/(v - v_s)`
- **When source moves away:** `f' = f(v)/(v + v_s)`
- **When observer moves toward source:** `f' = f(v + v_o)/v`
- **When observer moves away:** `f' = f(v - v_o)/v`

## Light Waves

### Wave Properties of Light

- **Plane waves:** `E = E₀ sin(ωt - kx)`, `I = ε₀c E²`
- **Spherical waves:** `E = (E₀/r) sin(ωt - kr)`, `I = I_a/r²`

### Young's Double Slit Experiment

- **Path difference:** `Δx = (2πd sin θ)/λ`
- **Condition for constructive interference:** `Δx = nλ` (n = 0,1,2,...)
- **Condition for destructive interference:** `Δx = (2n+1)λ/2` (n = 0,1,2,...)

- **Fringe width:** `ω = λD/d` (where D is distance to screen, d is slit separation)
- **Optical path difference:** `Δx = (2πd sin θ)/λ = d sin θ`

### Phase Difference

- **Path difference Δx:** `δ = (2π/λ) × Δx`
- **General relation:** `tan E = (A₁ sin φ)/(A + A₂ cos φ)` (for superposition)

### Interference Through Thin Film

- **Path difference:** `Δx = 2μd` (for normal incidence, μ is refractive index)
- **Constructive:** `Δx = nλ` (in-phase)
- **Destructive:** `Δx = (n+½)λ` (out-of-phase)

### Law of Malus

- **Intensity after polarizer:** `I = I₀ cos²θ` (where θ is angle between polarizers)

## Optics

### Reflection

- **Law of reflection:** `i = r` (angle of incidence = angle of reflection)
- **Curved mirrors:** `1/f = 1/u + 1/v` (mirror formula)
- **Magnification:** `m = -v/u`

### Refraction

- **Snell's Law:** `n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂`
- **Critical angle:** `sin θ_c = n₂/n₁` (for total internal reflection)
- **Apparent depth:** `Real depth/Apparent depth = n`

### Lenses

- **Lens formula:** `1/f = 1/v + 1/u`
- **Power:** `P = 1/f` (in diopters, f in meters)
- **Magnification:** `m = v/u`
- **Lens maker's formula:** `1/f = (μ-1)(1/R₁ - 1/R₂)` (for thin lens)

### Prism

- **Angle of deviation:** `δ = (i₁ + e₂) - A` where A is prism angle
- **At minimum deviation:** `r₁ = r₂ = A/2` and `i₁ = e₂`
- **Refractive index:** `n = sin[(A+δ_m)/2]/sin(A/2)`

### Spherical Surface

- **Refraction at curved surface:** `n₂/v - n₁/u = (n₂-n₁)/R`
- **Magnification:** `m = (n₁v)/(n₂u)`

### Thin Lenses

- **Lens formula:** `1/f = 1/v + 1/u`
- **Lens maker's formula:** `1/f = (μ-1)(1/R₁ + 1/R₂)` (convex lens)
- **Power:** `P = 1/f` (focal length in meters)

### Microscope

- **Simple microscope:** `m = (25/f)` (for relaxed eye, f in cm)
- **Compound microscope:** `m = (m_o × m_e)` (product of objective and eyepiece magnifications)
- **Resolving power:** `R = λ/(2D)` or `R_min = 1.22λ/D`

### Dispersion

- **Cauchy's formula:** `μ = μ₀ + (A/λ²) + ...`
- **For small angle A ≈ i:** Mean deviation `δ_y = (μ_y - 1)A`
- **Angular dispersion:** `θ = (μ_y - μ_r)A`
- **Dispersive power:** `Ω = (μ_y - μ_r)/(μ - 1)` (also written as `Ω = (μ_B - μ_R)/(μ - 1)`)

### Telescope

- **Magnification:** `m = -f_o/f_e`
- **Length:** `L = f_o + f_e`
- **Resolving power:** `R = D/1.22λ`

## Heat and Temperature

### Temperature Scales

- **Celsius to Fahrenheit:** `F = (9/5)C + 32`
- **Kelvin:** `K = C + 273.16` (approximately 273)

### Heat and Internal Energy

- **Heat capacity:** `C = ΔQ/ΔT`
- **Specific heat:** `c = ΔQ/(m·ΔT)`
- **Latent heat:** `L = Q/m`

### Thermal Expansion

- **Linear expansion:** `L = L₀(1 + αΔT)`
- **Area expansion:** `A = A₀(1 + βΔT)` where `β ≈ 2α`
- **Volume expansion:** `V = V₀(1 + γΔT)` where `γ ≈ 3α`

## Thermodynamics

### First Law of Thermodynamics

- `ΔQ = ΔU + W` where:
  - ΔQ = heat added to system
  - ΔU = change in internal energy
  - W = work done by system = ∫PdV

### Adiabatic Process

- **No heat exchange:** `ΔQ = 0`
- **Result:** `W = -ΔU` (work = negative change in internal energy)

### Isothermal Process

- **Constant temperature:** `ΔU = 0`
- **Result:** `W = ΔQ`

### Isobaric Process

- **Constant pressure:** `W = PΔV = nRΔT`

### Ideal Gas Law

- `PV = nRT` (for n moles of ideal gas)
- van der Waals: `(P + a/V²)(V - b) = nRT`

### Kinetic Theory

- **Internal Energy:** `U = (f/2)nRT` where f is degrees of freedom
  - Monatomic: f = 3
  - Diatomic: f = 5
- **Equipartition of energy:** `K = ½kT` per degree of freedom

### Maxwell Distribution

- **Probability distribution:** `P(v) = 4π(m/(2πk_B T))^(3/2) v² exp(-mv²/(2k_B T))`
- **Most probable velocity:** `v_probable = √(2k_B T/m) = √(2RT/M)`
- **Average velocity:** `v_avg = √(8k_B T/(πm)) = √(8RT/(πM))`
- **RMS velocity:** `v_rms = √(3k_B T/m) = √(3RT/M)`

### Second Law of Thermodynamics

- **Entropy change:** `ΔS = ΔQ_rev/T`
- **Efficiency of heat engine:** `η = W/Q_h = 1 - Q_c/Q_h`
- **Carnot efficiency:** `η_Carnot = 1 - T_c/T_h`

### Specific Heat Relations

- **For ideal gas:** `C_p = C_v + R`
- **Heat capacity ratio:** `γ = C_p/C_v`
- **Mayer's relation:** `C_p - C_v = R`

### Thermal Stress

- **Linear thermal stress:** `Stress = E × (strain) = E × α × ΔT`

### Heat Transfer

- **Conduction:** `dQ/dt = -kA(dT/dx)` (Fourier's law)
- **Thermal resistance:** `R_thermal = L/(kA)`
- **Thermal resistance for series:** `R_total = R₁ + R₂`
- **Thermal resistance for parallel:** `1/R_total = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂`

### Hydrostatic Pressure

- **Continuity equation:** `A₁v₁ = A₂v₂`

### Bernoulli's Equation

- `P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant`

### Viscosity

- **Viscous force:** `F = -ηA(dv/dy)` (Newton's law of viscosity)

### Torricelli's Theorem

- **Efflux velocity:** `v = √(2gh)`

### Stokes' Law

- **Drag force:** `F = 6πηrv` (for sphere of radius r moving with velocity v)

### Vertical Motion in Fluid

- **Terminal velocity:** `v_terminal = 2r²(ρ_s - ρ_f)g/(9η)` (Stokes' law)

### Poiseuille's Equation

- **Volume flow rate:** `Q = (πPr⁴)/(8ηL)` (through cylindrical pipe)

## Electrostatics

### Coulomb's Law

- **Force:** `F = (1/4πε₀)(q₁q₂/r²)` or `F = kq₁q₂/r²`
- **Electric field:** `E = F/q = (1/4πε₀)(Q/r²)`

### Dipole Moment

- **Definition:** `p⃗ = q·d⃗` (charge × separation)
- **Dipole in field:** `U = -p⃗·E⃗ = -pE cos θ`
- **Electric field of dipole:** `E_dipole = (1/4πε₀)(2p cosθ)/r³` (along axis)

### Gauss's Law

- **Integral form:** `∮ E⃗·dA⃗ = Q/ε₀`
- **For spherical symmetry:** `E = Q/(4πε₀r²)` (outside), `E = Qr/(4πε₀R³)` (inside)
- **For uniform sheet:** `E = σ/(2ε₀)`
- **For infinite line charge:** `E = λ/(2πε₀r)`

### Electric Potential

- **Definition:** `V = W/q = (1/4πε₀)(Q/r)`
- **Potential energy:** `U = qV`
- **Relation to field:** `E = -dV/dr` (for radial symmetry)

### Capacitance

- **Definition:** `C = Q/V`
- **Parallel plate capacitor:** `C = ε₀εᵣA/d` (A = area, d = separation, εᵣ = relative permittivity)
- **Spherical capacitor:** `C = 4πε₀εᵣ(ab/(b-a))` (a and b are radii)
- **Cylindrical capacitor:** `C = 2πε₀εᵣL/ln(b/a)` (L = length)

### Capacitors in Circuits

- **Series:** `1/C_eq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + ...`
- **Parallel:** `C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + ...`

### Energy Storage in Capacitor

- **Energy:** `U = ½QV = ½CV² = Q²/(2C)`
- **Energy density:** `u = ½ε₀E²`

### Dielectric Effects

- **With dielectric:** `C = C₀εᵣ` (where εᵣ is relative permittivity)
- **Force on capacitor:** `F = (ε₀A/2d²)(V²)` (constant voltage)

## Capacitors

### Capacitance Formula

- **General:** `C = (εε₀A)/d` (where ε is dielectric constant)

### Capacitor Types

- **Parallel plate:** `C = (2πε₀L)/ln(L/D_co)`
- **Sphere:** `C = 4πε₀r` (isolated sphere)
- **Sphere in dielectric:** `C = 4πε₀εᵣr`

### Charging and Discharging

- **Charging (with dielectric):** `q(t) = CV₀(1 - e^(-t/RC))` (time constant τ = RC)
- **Discharging:** `q(t) = q₀e^(-t/RC)`

### Energy and Force

- **Force between plates:** `F = (ε₀AV²)/(2d²)` (constant voltage)
- **With dielectric:** `Force = (ε₀A/2d²)(V²)` with dielectric

## Current Electricity

### Current and Resistance

- **Current:** `I = dQ/dt` (charge per unit time)
- **Current density:** `J = I/A = σE` (where σ is conductivity)
- **Drift velocity:** `v_drift = J/(ne)` (where n is electron density)
- **Resistance:** `R = ρL/A` (ρ = resistivity)
- **Conductance:** `G = 1/R = σA/L`

### Ohm's Law

- `V = IR` (voltage = current × resistance)
- `R = R₀(1 + αΔT)` (temperature dependence)

### Resistors in Circuits

- **Series:** `R_eq = R₁ + R₂ + ...`
- **Parallel:** `1/R_eq = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...`

### Kirchhoff's Laws

- **Junction Law:** `ΣI_in = ΣI_out` (sum of currents into node = sum out)
- **Loop Law:** `ΣV = 0` (sum of voltages around closed loop = 0)

### Power

- **Power dissipated:** `P = I²R = V²/R = VI`

### EMF and Internal Resistance

- **Terminal voltage:** `V = ε - Ir` (where ε is EMF, r is internal resistance)

## Magnetism

### Magnetic Force

- **On moving charge:** `F⃗ = q(v⃗ × B⃗)`
- **Magnitude:** `F = qvB sin θ`

- **On current-carrying wire:** `F⃗ = I(L⃗ × B⃗)`
- **Magnitude:** `F = BIL sin θ`

### Magnetic Dipole

- **Dipole moment:** `μ⃗ = IA⃗` (current × area)
- **Magnetic field due to dipole:** `B = (μ₀/(4π))(2μ/r³)` (along axis)
- **Torque on dipole:** `τ = μB sin θ`

### Hall Effect

- **Hall voltage:** `V_H = (IB)/(ne × t)` (where t is thickness)
- **Hall coefficient:** `R_H = 1/(ne)`

### Magnetic Field

- **Straight wire:** `B = (μ₀I)/(2πr)`
- **Circular loop (at center):** `B = (μ₀I)/(2R)`
- **Solenoid:** `B = μ₀nI` (where n is turns per unit length)
- **Toroid:** `B = (μ₀NI)/(2πR)` (where N is total number of turns)

### Ampere's Law

- `∮ B⃗·dl⃗ = μ₀I_enclosed`

### Biot-Savart Law

- **Differential form:** `dB = (μ₀I)/(4π)(dl × r⃗)/r³`

### Magnetic Field due to Current Configurations

- **Straight wire:** `B = (μ₀I)/(2πr)`
- **Center of circular loop:** `B = (μ₀I)/(2R)`
- **Arc of ring:** `B_arc = (μ₀Iθ)/(4πr)` (for arc subtending angle θ)
- **Center of arc:** `B = (μ₀I)/(4πR)` (for complete ring)

### Solenoid

- **Magnetic field:** `B = μ₀nI` (inside ideal solenoid)
- **Inductance:** `L = μ₀n²AL = μ₀N²A/L` (where N is total turns, L is length)

### Toroid

- **Magnetic field:** `B = (μ₀NI)/(2πR)` (at radius R from center)

### Ampere's Law in Different Configurations

- **Straight wire:** Use circular path around wire
- **Solenoid:** Use rectangular path inside solenoid
- **Toroid:** Use circular path inside toroid

### Tangent Galvanometer

- **Calibration:** `B = k tan θ` (where k depends on coil properties)
- **Earth's magnetic field component:** `B_E = B tan φ` (where φ is angle of deflection)

### Magnetometer

- **Measurement:** `T = (2π)/(μ₀B)√(I/Mμ_B)` (period related to magnetic moment)

### Permeability

- **Relation:** `B = μH = μ₀(1 + χ_m)H = μ₀μᵣH` (where χ_m is susceptibility, μᵣ is relative permeability)

## Electromagnetic Induction

### Faraday's Law

- **Induced EMF:** `ε = -dΦ/dt` (Φ is magnetic flux)
- **Magnetic flux:** `Φ = ∮ B⃗·dA⃗ = BA cos θ`

### Lenz's Law

- **Direction of induced current:** Opposes the change in magnetic flux that caused it
- **Result:** Induced EMF creates current in direction that opposes flux change

### Motional EMF

- **In moving conductor:** `ε = BLv` (L = length, v = velocity perpendicular to B)
- **Induced EMF in rotating loop:** `ε = NBA ω sin(ωt)` (N = turns, A = area)

### Self-Inductance

- **Definition:** `L = Φ/I` (flux linkage per unit current)
- **Induced EMF:** `ε = -L(dI/dt)`
- **Energy stored:** `U = ½LI²`

### Mutual Inductance

- **Definition:** `M = Φ₂/I₁` (flux in coil 2 per unit current in coil 1)
- **Induced EMF:** `ε₂ = -M(dI₁/dt)`

### AC Circuits

#### RC Circuit

- **Impedance:** `Z = √(R² + X_C²)` where `X_C = 1/(ωC)`
- **Phase angle:** `tan φ = -X_C/R = -1/(ωRC)`

#### LR Circuit

- **Impedance:** `Z = √(R² + X_L²)` where `X_L = ωL`
- **Phase angle:** `tan φ = X_L/R = ωL/R`

#### LCR Circuit

- **Impedance:** `Z = √(R² + X²)` where `X = X_L - X_C = ωL - 1/(ωC)`
- **Phase angle:** `tan φ = (ωL - 1/(ωC))/R`
- **Resonance:** At `ω = 1/√(LC)`, impedance is minimum (= R)

#### AC Power

- **Apparent power:** `S = V_rms × I_rms`
- **Real power:** `P = VI cos φ`
- **Power factor:** `cos φ = R/Z`

### Reactance

- **Capacitive reactance:** `X_C = 1/(ωC)`
- **Inductive reactance:** `X_L = ωL`

### Resonance in LCR Circuit

- **Resonant frequency:** `f = 1/(2π√(LC))` (angular frequency `ω = 1/√(LC)`)
- **Quality factor:** `Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀RC)`

### Transformer

- **Voltage relation:** `V_p/V_s = N_p/N_s` (transformer equation)
- **Current relation:** `I_p/I_s = N_s/N_p`
- **Power conservation:** `V_p I_p = V_s I_s` (ideal transformer)
- **Efficiency:** `η = (V_s I_s)/(V_p I_p)` = 1 for ideal transformer

### Time Constant

- **RC circuit:** `τ = RC`
- **LR circuit:** `τ = L/R`

### Energy Density in Magnetic Field

- `u = B²/(2μ₀)` (energy per unit volume)

### Rotating Coil (EMF Generation)

- **EMF:** `ε = NBA ω sin(ωt)` (N turns, area A, angular velocity ω)

## Modern Physics

### Photon Energy and Momentum

- **Energy:** `E = hf = hc/λ`
- **Momentum:** `p = E/c = h/λ`
- **Mass-energy relation:** `E = mc²`

### Photoelectric Effect

- **Einstein's photoelectric equation:** `hf = Φ + KE_max` (where Φ is work function)
- **Stopping potential:** `V_s = KE_max/e`
- **Threshold frequency:** `f₀ = Φ/h`

### Bohr's Atom Model

- **Postulates:** Electrons orbit nucleus in discrete energy levels
- **Quantization of angular momentum:** `L = mvr = n(h/2π)` (where n = 1,2,3...)
- **Bohr radius:** `a₀ = (4πε₀h²)/(me²) = 0.529×10⁻¹⁰ m`
- **Energy levels:** `E_n = -13.6 eV/n²` (hydrogen)

### Energy Transitions

- **Photon frequency:** `f = (E₂ - E₁)/h`
- **Wavelength:** `λ = hc/(E₂ - E₁)`

### Rydberg Formula

- **For hydrogen:** `1/λ = R∞(1/n₁² - 1/n₂²)`
- **Rydberg constant:** `R∞ = 1.097×10⁷ m⁻¹`

### X-Ray Spectrum

- **Moseley's Law:** `ν = R_c(Z - σ)²(1/n₁² - 1/n₂²)` (where σ is screening constant)

### Atomic Mass Defect

- **Mass defect:** `Δm = (Z·m_p + N·m_n) - m_nucleus`
- **Binding energy:** `BE = Δm·c²`

### Nuclear Decay

- **Activity:** `A = λN` (decay rate)
- **Radioactive decay law:** `N(t) = N₀e^(-λt)`
- **Half-life:** `t_{1/2} = ln(2)/λ = 0.693/λ`
- **Average life:** `τ_avg = 1/λ`

## Semiconductors

### Semiconductor Diode

- **Half-wave rectifier:** `I = I_d + I_L` (dark current + photocurrent)
- **Full-wave rectifier:** Converts AC to DC with both half-cycles

- **Triode diode:** Three terminals (grid, anode, cathode)

### Transistor

- **BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor):**
  - Base, Collector, Emitter terminals
  - Current amplification: `I_c = β·I_b` (where β is current gain)
  - Voltage gain: `A_v = -μ·R_L/r_d` (where μ is amplification factor)

- **Transconductance:** `g_m = ΔI_c/ΔV_be`

### Logic Gates

- **AND gate:** Output is 1 only when all inputs are 1
- **OR gate:** Output is 1 when at least one input is 1
- **NOT gate:** Output is opposite of input
- **NAND gate:** Output is 0 only when all inputs are 1
- **NOR gate:** Output is 1 only when all inputs are 0

## Key Summary

### Essential Concepts

1. **Always use SI units** unless specifically asked otherwise
2. **Vector quantities** require both magnitude and direction
3. **Conservative forces** maintain mechanical energy
4. **Momentum is always conserved** in isolated systems
5. **Energy conservation** applies universally
6. **Symmetry principles** are powerful in solving problems
7. **Boundary conditions** are crucial in wave phenomena

### Problem-Solving Strategy

1. Identify all given quantities and unknowns
2. Draw diagrams and establish coordinate systems
3. Apply relevant conservation laws (momentum, energy, charge)
4. Use kinematic or dynamic equations as appropriate
5. Check dimensions and reasonableness of answer
6. Consider limiting cases and special scenarios

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Confusing displacement with distance
- Misapplying conservation laws (need to verify system is isolated)
- Ignoring vector nature of quantities
- Forgetting to convert units
- Not considering all forces in free-body diagrams
- Confusing speed with velocity
- Misunderstanding phase in wave phenomena
